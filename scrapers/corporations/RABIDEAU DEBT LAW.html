Company Profile


Issues pertaining to debt and tax liability can be overwhelming for individuals and businesses alike. If you or your company owes money to creditors, banks, or the government, the consequences of inaction are devastating. In addition to the threat of legal action and seizure of your assets (including personal assets such as your home, car, and savings), your family and your lifestyle are now exposed to serious risk.

At Rabideau Law, we understand the importance of taking quick, decisive action to address your debt problems before its too late. Our team of professionals has the knowledge and experience to protect what matter most. We stand between you and your creditors  including the Canada Revenue Agency  to ensure that your rights and assets are properly defended against legal action. Our firms confidential representation services can reduce or eliminate debt, prevent judgments against you, remove liens and garnishes, unfreeze bank accounts and stop collections. Clients across Canada now enjoy the peace of mind of knowing that they no longer have to live in fear of their debt.

Product / Service / Licensing

Service Name:
Debt Settlement
Debt settlement is the process whereby we will negotiate, on your behalf, with your creditors a reduced amount in exchange for a lump sum payment.

Only unsecured debts, such as credit card debts, lines of credits, loans and medical expenses to name a few, can be enrolled into the program. Secured debts, such as mortgages, car loans (where you are still in possession of the vehicle) and line of credits secured by the equity in your home, cannot be enrolled in the program. Canada Revenue income tax, HST and source deduction debts and student loans also cannot be enrolled in the program.

The lump sum payment can be achieved in a few ways:

By using the equity in your home with an institutional or private mortgage;
Loans from an institutional or private lender;
Funds saved on a monthly basis into a trust account set up on your behalf.
For clients that have equity in their home and can qualify for a mortgage, either refinance or private second mortgage, we would use those mortgage funds to negotiate your debts and receive substantial savings.

The same is true for clients that can qualify for a personal or business loan, we are able to use those funds to settle debts on a lump sum basis.

For clients that are unable to put together a lump sum amount, we offer the option for our clients to save funds, on a monthly basis, into a third party special purpose account where funds accumulate overtime for the settlement process. Once enough funds are saved we will begin negotiating with your creditors on an individual basis.

Our legal fee for negotiating your debts is 17% (plus taxes) of the total enrolled debt amount. For the lump sum settlement plans, this 17% would be paid out with each debt that is settled. For instance, if we were to settle your Visa credit card debt of $10,000, on paying out such settlement we would collect our legal fee of $1,700 (plus taxes).

For settlement plans where funds need to accumulate in a special purpose account, our legal fee would be paid out over the first 18 months for programs that are 36 months or more, or over half the duration of the term for plans that are less than 36 months. For instance, taking into consideration that same $10,000 Visa credit card, we would collect our legal fee of $1,700 (plus taxes) over the first 18 months of the program.

In addition, for monthly payment settlement plans, there is a monthly fee for the special purpose bank account in the amount of $11.35 and there is a monthly maintenance fee of $35 (plus applicable provincial and federal taxes) for each month that you are enrolled in the program after the initial 18 months, or after half the duration of the program, whichever is shorter.
