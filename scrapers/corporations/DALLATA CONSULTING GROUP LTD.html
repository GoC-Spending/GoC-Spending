Company Profile


Dallata Consulting Group Ltd.(DCG Ltd.) is a very unique, innovative, and distinguished goal oriented company that strives to anticipate their customers needs and assist them in achieving their ultimate goals.

Dallata Consulting Group Ltd. was formed in 2004 as a result of merging seven internationally acclaimed companies that existed in four countries on three continents.

The resulting merger thus gives Dallata Consulting Group Ltd. a global business reach, with head office in Toronto, Canada, and branch offices in New Zealand, USA, Jordan, Syria, Egypt, and local Agents in Riyadh Saudi Arabia, and sister companies in Dubai UAE, London England, Chittagong Bangladesh, Casablanca Morocco.

At the beginning, Dallata Consulting Group specialized in the fields of business and financial consulting. As the demand from our customers grew, so did our company and we added a Trading House of commodities to our portfolio. Evolution of the Consulting and Trade House divisions led to the establishment of the most recent division which is the Research and Development division.

Our latest addition was our IT division late 2009, which deals with turnkey IT projects. Such as E commerce, CRM solutions, Website developing.


Under the visionary leadership of the CEO of DCG Ltd., Mr. Akram Hamid, the company represents clients from over 27 countries and it is constantly adding new clients to its portfolio.

Product Name:
Alfalfa Cubes
DCG alfalfa cubes are produced from premium quality, leafy alfalfa harvested at its peak for optimum nutrient contents.

Some of the finest quality hay in the world is produced in Canada. Fertile soils, a long growing season and few pests make for ideal growing and harvesting conditions. DCG producers have a long history of shipping hay across Canada, USA and the World. Customers from Japan, Korea, Europe, Middle East, Florida, Texas and Wisconsin are among the growing numbers of customers who purchase thousands of tons of DCG hay annually. You can be confident that DCG hay producers can meet your needs regardless of where you live.

Dried and cubed in a 1 1/4" format, our cubes are the natural choice for a healthy, happy horse. DCG Alfalfa Cubes are highly digestible and a much more efficient source of protein, minerals and vitamins than even the best quality hay.

Easy to store, easy to handle DCG forage cubes deliver the same high quality nutrition & taste all year long! Now DCG provides a choice of formats to deliver high quality nutrition and meet the needs of horses with a preference for modest dimensions.

DAILY FEEDING INSTRUCTIONS
Feed at a rate of 1% of horse body weight (10lbs for a 1000lb animal). A 2 gallon (8L) will hold about 5 lbs (2.25kg) of cubes. A flake of hay will weigh about 4 lbs (2kg), with about 1lb (0.5kg) lost in feeding. Ground level feeding recommended.

Wheat Flour
DCG all-purpose wheat flour is made only from the finest grains, and will make any recipe you need it for taste so much better!

All-purpose flour is the finely ground endosperm of the wheat kernel separated from the bran and germ during the milling process. Product Name:
Barley Feed
DCG barley is a globally known product sought after for its high quality and richness in nutrients.
DCG has consistently been a major producer of high-quality barley on the world stage. DCGs barley is subject to the strict quality standards of the Canadian Grain Commission's grain quality control program, which includes varietal control, licensing of elevators, product inspection and weighing, and sanitation and quality monitoring programs. Some of the ongoing research in the province focuses on the development of enhanced barley cultivators with those qualities that meet specific customer needs. These projects and those ongoing in other provinces in Canada, continually make Canada a leader in barley varietal and processing development.

As much as 80 per cent of all barley grown in Alberta every year is used as feed for livestock. Much of barley grown every year is used as feed for livestock, resulting in beef that is finely textured and delicately marbled in white fat.

Barley is also used to feed hogs, poultry and sheep. And some 400,000 to 600,000 tons of feed barley are exported annually, primarily to Japan (mainly for beef and hogs) and Saudi Arabia (mainly for camels).

Barley is considered ideal feed for livestock for several reasons.
 Barley has the genetic diversity to perform well in many of the provinces growing conditions
 Barley is an excellent source of energy (starch)
 Barley contains more protein than corn (which is also widely used for livestock feed)
 Barley has a higher level of starch digestion in ruminants than corn (despite having a lower starch content)
 Barley can be the total grain portion beef cattle and dairy cows eat

Barley Feed
DCG barley is a globally known product sought after for its high quality and richness in nutrients.
DCG has consistently been a major producer of high-quality barley on the world stage. DCGs barley is subject to the strict quality standards of the Canadian Grain Commission's grain quality control program, which includes varietal control, licensing of elevators, product inspection and weighing, and sanitation and quality monitoring programs. Some of the ongoing research in the province focuses on the development of enhanced barley cultivators with those qualities that meet specific customer needs. These projects and those ongoing in other provinces in Canada, continually make Canada a leader in barley varietal and processing development.

As much as 80 per cent of all barley grown in Alberta every year is used as feed for livestock. Much of barley grown every year is used as feed for livestock, resulting in beef that is finely textured and delicately marbled in white fat.

Barley is also used to feed hogs, poultry and sheep. And some 400,000 to 600,000 tons of feed barley are exported annually, primarily to Japan (mainly for beef and hogs) and Saudi Arabia (mainly for camels).

Barley is considered ideal feed for livestock for several reasons.
 Barley has the genetic diversity to perform well in many of the provinces growing conditions
 Barley is an excellent source of energy (starch)
 Barley contains more protein than corn (which is also widely used for livestock feed)
 Barley has a higher level of starch digestion in ruminants than corn (despite having a lower starch content)
 Barley can be the total grain portion beef cattle and dairy cows eat
