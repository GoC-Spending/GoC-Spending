Company Profile


Electric Edge was founded in September, 2000 by three partners: Bryan Stevenson, Phil Bailey, and Greg Rusak (Greg was with Electric Edge until June, 2009). Each partner had many years of experience in different fields including website and web applications development, client/server software development, and database design. The partners had worked on projects of all sizes, including development of major systems for organizations such as TELUS, BC Ferry Services, Canfor, Worksafe BC, and many others.

Our company was formed when the three founders realized that by combining their diverse skills, they could provide the full spectrum of systems development services with unsurpassed quality.

In spite of forming the company in the darkest days of the dot-com collapse of 2000, our company has been profitable and has grown steadily every year. Today, the partners and staff of Electric Edge Systems Group produce top-quality websites, web applications, and software for numerous clients of all sizes.

We are often contacted by organizations with websites and applications that were produced by individuals or companies that were unable to complete the projects, or that produced poor quality results. A considerable portion of our work involves redevelopment of web, software, and database projects in order to raise them to the high standards that we are known for.

For the last several years, we have evolved into experts in fisheries management systems through our extensive work for Fisheries and Oceans Canada - Pacific Region. Major changes have taken place in fisheries management, not only in Canada, but around the world. We have been at the forefront of building systems to implement those changes for various Canadian fisheries. As a result, we are extending our reach as we promote our skills and experience to governments and fishery organizations around the world.

For more information, please contact us.

Product / Service / Licensing

Product Name:
Electronic Fishing Data Capture System
Around the world, conservation of marine life is a growing concern. In many areas, these concerns are addressed through increased monitoring of fishing activity. One method is to monitor the offloading of catch from fishing vessels and record the quantity of each species that was caught and offloaded. Those that perform this work are referred to as "dockside monitors".

In fisheries that use dockside monitoring, fishing vessels are required to provide notification prior to the start of a fishing trip and again when fishing has concluded and the vessel is returning to port. These notifications are referred to as "hails". At a minimum, hails provide the time and place where the vessel will be offloading its catch.

In addition to providing data to regulatory and industry bodies, hails provide advance notice to dockside monitors so that they can meet the vessel upon its arrival in port and monitor the offloading of catch. The hail data can also help to ensure that vessels cannot go out fishing when certain regulatory requirements are not met.

Electric Edge Systems Group has developed a complete solution for electronic hails from vessels while they are at sea.

Service Name:
Fisheries Management Solutions
Electric Edge Systems Group builds quality, enterprise-level systems for the management of commercial fisheries.

Fisheries Management - Changing Times and Requirements
Around the world, we are witnessing a fundamental shift in fishery management. Declining stocks require increased enforcement through trip, catch, and offload monitoring. Environmental concerns require drastic reductions in discarded bycatch. This is being handled through individual vessel quota that allows for retention of bycatch species and the transfer of quota between vessels.

The quantity of data being tracked and the need to process and report on it quickly and effectively has never been greater. This requires new software applications, revamped databases, and new systems that are geared toward the requirements of today's fisheries.

What We have been Doing
For the last several years, Electric Edge Systems has worked closely with the Pacific region of Fisheries and Oceans Canada to maintain and add new functionality to existing systems and to create entirely new systems. During that time, Fisheries and Oceans Canada has implemented a wide variety of changes in various aspects of its business including trip/catch monitoring and integration of various fisheries via bycatch retention and individual transferable quota.

Electric Edge has been at the forefront of implementing the many changes to Fisheries and Oceans Canada's systems. We have created or performed major work on the following systems:

* Fishery Operations System
* Quota Management System
* Scientific Licensing System
* Plant Inspections System
* National Recreational Licensing System (publically accessible)
* Regional External Party Management System

Electric Edge Systems is a group of highly skilled, dedicated I.T. professionals with experience in all aspects of systems development including the following:

* Business Analysis
* Technical Design
* Data Modeling
* Database Development
* Programming of Web and Client/Server Systems
* Unit and Integrated Testing
* Documentation and User Training
* Ongoing Support and Systems Maintenance

What We are Doing Now
Going forward, Electric Edge Systems Group is expanding its operations. We will build upon our experience with Fisheries and Oceans Canada by providing our services and expertise to government departments and other fishery management organizations around the world. We will be seeking projects ranging from high-level consultative/advisory roles, to complete end-to-end systems planning, development, and ongoing support. We will be pleased to work independently or in partnership with organizations in the countries that they serve.

Electric Edge Systems will add value to fishery management organizations as they create, adapt and improve I.T. systems to cope with the demands of today's fisheries and legislation.

Key / Major clients:

BC Tel / Telus:<br>
Designed and implemented a Corporate Intranet for
the Human Resource Department of Telus. The
project involved the use of several different
technologies including; Microsoft Access, dBase,
and Cold Fusion. This site provides corporate
information to over 3000 Telus managers
throughout BC and Alberta. The system was an
integral part of re-structuring the company
during the recent BC Tel / Telus merger.
<br><br>
BC Ferry Corporation (Traffic Management
System):<br>
Lead developer of the Traffic Management System
(TMS). As ferry tickets for vehicles and foot
passengers are purchased through PC POS,
statistical information is fed into TMS. The data
is displayed in real time in the traffic
control tower. Tower operators use the data for
tracking all traffic in the compound and for
loading the traffic onto the appropriate vessels
in accordance with coast guard regulations. TMS
was developed in PowerBuilder 5.0 using the pfc
library.
<br><br>
BC Ferry Corporation (Point-of-Sale System):<br>
Co-developed PC POS - The point of sale system
now in use at all BC Ferry Terminals that have
ticket booths. BC Ferries transports over 25
million passengers per year with annual revenues
of approximately $500 million. Over 80% of all
corporate revenues are processed through PC POS.
<br><br>
PC POS was built using PowerBuilder with the
Powersoft Foundation Class (pfc) library. PC POS
is architected for continuous service at
locations that may be subject to frequent network
outages. Each POS device is a Windows-based PC
with its own local Sybase SQL Anywhere
database. Attached peripherals include an
electronic cash drawer, customer display, barcode
scanner and ticket printer. Each POS database
communicates with an Oracle 7.3 database in the
ferry terminal. The Oracle databases connect to a
central Sybase System 11 database in Victoria.
All inter-database communication occurs as a
background process. In the event of a network
outage, the databases accumulate transactions
until the network is re-connected. Upon re-
connection, all data is streamed to the target
databases.
