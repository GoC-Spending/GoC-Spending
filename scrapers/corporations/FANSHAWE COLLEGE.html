The Centre for Research & Innovation -- which also houses the Fanshawe College Industry Innovation Centre -- is a Fanshawe College service department.

Unique applications:

Our new Centre for Sustainable Energy and Environments, known as CSEE and funded by the Natural Sciences and Engineering Council of Canada (NSERC), focuses on renewable energy technologies and sustainable buildings. This supports city and area efforts to develop a green technologies sector in our regional and spur job creation. One project underway is development of a more efficient solar battery charging and energy management system for electrically-powered solar utility vehicles. While these vehicles have great potential to save huge amounts of money in energy costs, at present they cant run for very long on a single charge and take too long to recharge. This makes their regular use impractical.

Using a golf cart as a platform, our team converted three carts to solar power and, this summer, continued field testing at several area golf courses. Data showed an increase in battery efficiency and performance of about 20%. At the start of our project, a cart could be driven long enough to complete two 18-hole rounds of golf on one charge. With improvements, this summers field testing resulted in the team getting five rounds of golf out of a single charge. Thats significant. It means that a solar powered vehicle that used to run for about four hours a day can now be used all day long. Additionally, our system allows the battery to recharge all day as the vehicle is being used. In fact, the greatest complaint of one of our host partners was that the cart recharged itself so completely by the end of the day, it was unnecessary to plug it into an electrical outlet at night. Work on this project is continuing to determine its applicability to other types of small utility vehicles.

In another project, a Fanshawe team worked with Virelec Solar, a Mississauga-area company that designs and installs renewable energy systems, solar panels and controls. Our studies into the impact of the Canadian climate on the performance of solar panels has allowed Virelec to develop more effective energy solutions for its clients, thus enabling the company to better demonstrate the economic benefits of such installtions to future clients. We were delighted to learn that following project completion, the company was so impressed with our student team member, it hired him for a full-time job.

A team from our School of Building Technology recently assisted a small company called LAVENDER SENSE in the design of an environmentally-friendly, sustainable conference centre and chapel. The students will help build the structure on company property in the first of what we hope will be a series of collaborative projects with this business. Other recent projects completed by Fanshawe teams include include a sustainable, affordable housing project with Habitat for Humanity, and a land use study and design charette for the Port Stanley Harbour.

Lest all you think we do is technology-related, Fanshawes largest area of research activity is teaching, learning and student success. We have a number of projects underway or in the works with community and non-profit agencies and other institutions. For example, a researcher from our counselling and accessibility services department has been involved in a series of investigations aimed at better supporting postsecondary students with mental health disabilities. A large number of these students never complete their postsecondary programs; unemployment in this group averages 70-80%. Working in conjunction with a University of Western Ontario researcher and led by LEADS Employment Services, the project is aimed at developing supports to improve graduation and employment rates for these individuals. The team is optimistic that  with the right support model  more of these students can graduate, get jobs, be lifted out of poverty and enjoy a better quality of life. Other researchers at Fanshawe are focusing on research into various educational aspects of autism, assistive technology for people with disabilities, early childhood development, and so on.
