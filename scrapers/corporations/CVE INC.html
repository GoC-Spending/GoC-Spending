Company Profile


CVE Inc. is a professional Medical, Occupational, Vocational, and Employment service provider assisting in the Rehabilitation of individuals in Eastern South Eastern and Northern Ontario, as well as Western Quebec. Our team is devoted to reducing the personal, social and financial cost of injury, disability or illness to all individuals involved. Our dedication and proactive approach to you and to our programs and services, reflect our determination to achieve our common goals.

MEDICAL MANAGEMENT


Discharge Planning

A determination of services and equipment required by an individual upon discharge through evaluation and liaison with the social worker and other hospital staff.


Assessment of Pre and Post-Accident Activities (N.L.A)

An evaluation of an individual's ability and competency through performance and the description of his or her life activities prior to and following a motor vehicle accident (M.V.A).


Case Management
The progressive and individualized coordination of services in order to ensure that the required services are implemented. Includes:

Individualized home visits.
Liaison with physicians for continued care.
Monitoring existing services.
Ensuring that the client attend medical and other appointments as appropriate.
Developing and implementing rehabilitation plan.


Coordination of Functional Capacity Evaluation (F.C.E)

The gathering and organization of information for a timely functional capacity evaluation (F.C.E).



Coordination of Independent Medical Examination (I.M.E). / Insurer's Examination (I.E.)

Determination of the best resource for an I.M.E or an I.E and coordination of the assessment. Includes evaluation of the report and recommendations.



Coordination of Designated Assessment Center (D.A.C.) Assessments

Completion of forms and consolidation of entire medical records which is then forwarded to the designated assessment centre (D.A.C).



Review and Collection of Medical and Vocational Documentation
The gathering of information about the individual prior to, during, and following the evaluation. Includes:

Referral information.
Social and work history.
Medical, psychological and psycho-social information.
Program goals and plans.
Test results.
Services to be provided.
Signed reports and notations.
Notes from case conferences.
Termination decision.
Final report findings and recommendations.


Management of Catastrophic Cases

Involves complete management of injuries such as head, spinal, amputations, etc.


Future Care Costs Analysis (Catastrophic Injuries)

A determination of what an individual's lifetime needs are expected to be. May include the creation of medical - legal documentation for claim settlement.


Development of Return-to-Work Plans

Monitoring the individual's progress, providing and recommending necessary equipment when needed as well as maintaining liaisons with other health professionals, union, and/or employers regarding alternative or modified work.





OCCUPATIONAL THERAPY






Physical Demands Analysis (P.D.A)

A systematic and objective procedure that measures the physical strength, and endurance required to perform a specific job or a cluster of related jobs.



Functional Capacity Evaluation (F.C.E)

A standardized process that measures an individual's physical tolerance. The evaluation considers sitting, standing, walking, climbing, kneeling and other relevant physical activities and how they may affect his or her ability to perform work tasks.




Job-Site Analysis and Job Modification (Ergonomic Assessments)

Involves the gathering, evaluating, and recording of objective, and complete data on a work environment to develop recommendations that optimize an employee's ability to perform his or her work tasks. Recommendations may include environmental adaptations, adaptive equipment and devices, job process modification, or other ergonomic considerations.



Education and Training
In a one on one or group setting, discussion topics can be on one of the following but not limited to:

Energy Conservation.
Pacing Techniques.
Proper Body Mechanics.
Relaxation Techniques.
Home Safety.
Sleep Hygiene.
Activity Modification.
Stress Management.

Normal Life Activities

An assessment of an individual's level of pre and post-accident ability to perform their activities of daily living, geared to promote a safe and timely return to the pre-accident level of functioning.



Home Safety Assessment (Discharge Planning)
An assessment of an individual's ability to safely manage self-care and mobility tasks within the home environment. Includes:

Architectural barriers and safety risks.
The home or post-discharge residence.
Attendant care needs (Form 1).
Equipment and assistance recommendations to promote safety and independence.



Home Site Modification

An assessment of the home environment to identify potential architectural modifications and accommodations in order to promote an individual's safety and independence. Includes the coordination of modifications with a licensed contractor, if needed.



Modified and Graduated Return-to-Work Programs

The coordination of a program to help an individual reintegrate into gainful employment. May include a gradual increase of work hours and duties to promote a successful return-to-work following injury or illness.



Future Care Costs Analysis

A determination of what an individual's lifetime needs are expected to be. May include the creation of medical - legal documentation for claim settlement.




Coordination of Independent Medical Examination (I.M.E.) / Insurer's Examination (I.E.)

The facilitation of referrals for an I.M.E or I.E. Includes the scheduling of appointments and the compilation of comprehensive referral documentation.



File Review
Review of the information obtained about the individual prior to, during, and following evaluations. Includes:

Referral information and questions.
Social and work history.
Medical, psychological and psycho-social information.
Program goals and plans.
Test results.
Services to be provided.
Signed reports and notations.
Notes from case conferences.
The termination decision.
Final report findings and recommendations.





VOCATIONAL MANAGEMENT





Case Management

The coordination of services in order to ensure the clients progress through the rehabilitation process. Includes case reviews at regular intervals.



Evaluation

Process that systematically uses real or simulated work for assessment and vocational exploration purposes, in order to assist individuals in their vocational development. Begins with an interview to determine vocational potential, i.e. is the person a suitable applicant for a return-to-work program? If vocational services are appropriate, what jobs could this individual perform?. Includes the incorporation of medical, psychological, social, vocational, educational, cultural, and economic data. Identifies suitable employment alternatives, potential for training, and recommendations for return-to-work and includes screening and selection testing.



Transferable Skills Analysis

A process of developing a profile of an individual's occupational and educational history and identifying traits and skills that can be used in alternative occupations.


Counseling and Planning

The process of advising an individual regarding his or her vocational assets and liabilities when choosing a suitable occupation, assisting in the formulation of realistic return-to-work goals, and providing personal support through the return-to-work process. Consideration is given to motivation, medical and psychological condition, education, training, work history, and areas of interest.



Labour Market Research
The investigation of existing jobs and more specifically their appropriateness given the following:

Individual's transferable skills and residual functional capacity.
Salary range.
Hiring trends and their geographic component.
Employment availability.
Qualifications that may be needed.
Job specifications.
Growth potential / future outlook.




EMPLOYMENT SERVICES




Active Job Search
A program that assists individuals with organizing and developing productive job searching skills. Includes:

Goal setting.
Identification of job skills.
Résumé and cover letters.
Interview preparation.
Support prior to and following job interviews.



Intensive Job Search Training  IJST
An ideal program for job ready individuals that covers every aspect of job searching in a period of four (4) weeks. Includes:

Assistance in dealing with change of employment.
Goal setting, employment research, identifying hidden skills, confidence building.
Cover letter, résumé, interviewing preparation, cold calls, proper telephone skills.
Information session and assistance with job search resources (i.e. internet, newspapers, job banks, networking, etc.).
Active job search and interview support.



Placement
Research of appropriate job leads using a variety of client and specialist resources in order to establish employer contacts that will assist the client in finding suitable employment. Includes:

Assessment of placement readiness.
Development of an individualized placement plan.
Job seeking, on the job, and follow up training/support to increase success of placement.
Résumé, cover letter, and interview preparation.
Placement within an organization that will allow for maximum re-integration.
Review and assessment of occupations and if need be, recommendations for possible job task modifications for a successful return-to-work outcome.


Community Based Work Hardening

A work oriented treatment process that is individualized and performed within the setting of an actual work environment. The tasks involved to perform the particular job duties are structured and graded to progressively increase physical tolerances, stamina, endurance, and productivity utilizing our Employment and Occupational Therapy departments with the eventual outcome being a successful return to the workforce.



Employment Planning

Involves establishing steps to employment including exploration of training requirements, establishment of goals and supported labour market research.



Job Coaching

Individualized one-on-one assistance within job placement that includes, but is not limited to travel training, skill training at job site, ongoing assessment, and long-term assessment.
