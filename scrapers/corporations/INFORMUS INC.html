(1) BUSINESS CONSULTING

Ian MacDonald has decades of experience in general management, sales, marketing, research, new product development, sales management, commercial risk analysis, collections, bankruptcy and project administration.
He assists corporations in:
(a) developing systems to avoid commercial risk losses.
(b) resolving disputes with suppliers, customers and government departments.
EXPERIENCE:
DUN & BRADSTREET - Credit Reporter (determined commercial risk by interviewing thousands of business owners), Reporting Supervisor (trained reporters and managed hundreds of correspondents in analyzing commercial risk), Sales Representative - Marketing Services Division (sold commercial market identifiers to sales, advertising and marketing executives) General Manager - Marketing Services Division (established, trained and organized a dynamic national sales team), General Sales Manager of All Divisions (successfully motivated a team of 100 sales reps and managers in 3 divisions in 12 offices)
CREDITEL - Ontario Regional Manager (bottom line responsibility for four offices and two hundred employees in collections, sales & administration), Senior Vice President - Credit Reporting Division (bottom line responsibility for hundreds of employees across Canada in 15 offices. Built a successful credit reporting operation that took away half of Dun & Bradstreet's market in Canada. Invented an "amazingly predictive" commercial risk scoring system.)
DRAKE / OFFICE OVERLOAD - Intercontinental Collections Limited (President of a small collection agency), Screening Systems iternational Limited (General Manager - developed a senior executive screening service)
Marketing Overload (General Manager - selling and conducting market research studies)
EQUIFAX- Vice President (responsible for the integration of the Creditel acquisition)

(2) ART DEALER

A faster, easier, inexpensive way for collectors to sell their art. To sell a painting, a current, reasonable price must first be determined . At our expense, we research and provide you with our recommended fair price.
FIRST STEP - Contact us and provide us with as much information as you can about your painting.
THE PROCESS - As soon as an agreed-to-price is determined, Informus commences marketing your painting. When Informus receives payment from a buyer, Informus then purchases the painting from you. Informus Inc. adds $500 to the "agreed-to-prices" to cover its expenses. The buyer pays this $500, not you. At any time you can ask Informus to cease marketing your painting. There are no contracts to sign. It is straightforward transaction. You get your agreed-to-price. The painting stays in your possession until the day you receive full payment for it.

Upon learning of this intrusion, the Secretary of State sends analyst, Dr. Robert Lyons, to Saint Matts to assess the situation.
