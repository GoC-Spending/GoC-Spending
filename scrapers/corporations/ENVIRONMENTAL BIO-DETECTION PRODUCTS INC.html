Company Profile

Environmental Biodetection Products Inc. (EBPI) is an environmental biotechnology firm dedicated to the production, R&D and marketing of biotechnology-based environmental testing products. EBPI is a private company incorporated in 1992.
EBPI is a technological leader in: toxicity, genotoxicity, and mutagenicity colorimetric bioassays based on freeze dried bacteria and specialized reagents; microbial colorimetric bioassay based on specialized media with specific inducers, substrates and sterilized polymerizing agent.
EBPI products are self-contained kits requiring minimal facilities and no previous analytical experience. They are rapid, reliable, cost-effective and easy to use. Individual kits are as follows;
Toxi-Chromotest: A microplate toxicity bioassay to determine acute toxicity in water, effluent and other liquids. The strain utilized a rough mutant strain of E. coli which is prepared in a stressed condition to allow for increased sensitivity.
SediTox: The SediTox platform is based on novel bacterial strains also used in EBPI’s line of Toxi-ChromoTest, SOS-ChromoTest and the UMU-ChormoTest products for direct sediment toxicity and genotoxicity. The SediTox Kit employs a direct exposure method to assess total responses from all soluble, insoluble, organic, inorganic, and volatile molecules in a given sample without extraction steps.By employing our genotoxicity strains with this method, EBPI has developed the first direct contact assay for subchronic endpoints like DNA damage.
SOS-Chromotest: A microplate genotoxicity bioassay based on the primary response of a genetically engineered bacteria to determine DNA genotoxicity damage in water, wastewater, sediment, air chemicals, food, cosmetics, and biological fluids.
EBPI has developed the umu-c test into the UMU-CHROMOTEST,a simple procedure based upon the International Organization for Standardization protocol ISO 13829 (Water Quality- Determination of the genotoxicity of water and waste water using the umu-test). The umu test uses the genetically engineer Salmonella typhimurium TA1535 [pSK1002] which are exposed to different concentrations of the samples to be tested. The test is based on the induction of the umuC-gene which is fused to the lacZ-gene which is responsible for the production of β-galactosidase, which can easily be assayed as an indirect measure of DNA damage, or genotoxicity.
Muta-ChromoPlate: A microplate version of the traditional 'pour plate' Ames Test, considerable more sensitive and user friendly, to detect DNA mutagenic activity in water, wastewater, sediment, air, chemicals, food, cosmetics and biological fluids.
Ames-384 ISO: EBPI's Ames ISO procedure was developed to provide the same reliable assessments of mutagenic activity in environmental samples as the Muta-Chromoplate with the added benefits of using smaller sample sizes, less reagents and plastics, a pre-exposure period and less waste. The test is run using a 384 well micro-plate with results available within 3-4 days.
Ames-MOD ISO: This method combines the benefits of the ISO method while maintaining the ease-of-use of the Muta-ChromoPlate™ method. Smaller sample sizes are required and less reagents are used which is like the ISO, however, reversion media mixtures amount and 96-well microplates facilitate pipetting and sample transfer to incubation step which mirrors the Muta-ChromoPlate™ Ames test procedure. Tests employ a 48-well assessment format by dividing standard 96 well micro-plates in half, for each sample or control.
Ames Express Strains: The EBPI Ames ExpressTM strains are unique to the Ames strain as these strains also express either human P450 1A2 or GST-theta enzymes internally which allows for the bio-activation of xenobiotic molecules into DNA reactive species in the absence of a S9 mix. The ability to induce revertant mutation through bio-activation makes these Salmonella strains the ideal biomarker in assessing mutagenic properties in test samples.
Distributor of the MicroBioTests line of ToxKits for Canada and the United States. The MicroBioTests kits are based on ISO, EPA and OECD methods using higher level organisms such as daphnia, rotifer, and algae. These kits (like EBPI's line of toxicity testing kits) are used for the detection and quantification of the toxicity of chemicals and or solid and liquid wastes which pollute aquatic and terrestrial environments. All product lines have the unique characteristics that they are culture and maintenance free bioassays, which do not require stock culturing of the test organisms.
Coliplate: A bioassay for quantitative measure of coliform and E. coli, indicative of fecal pollution in water and wastewater.
Watercheck: A qualitative bioassay for the presence/absense of Coliforms and E. coli in potable water supplies.
EBPI's test kits are designed to adapt to the needs of: industry, municipalities, academia, research and analytical laboratories, environmental consultants, governments, health and nature preservation organizations. EBPI has clients worldwide and frequently works in partnership with them to meet their environmental challenges.

(EBPI) is offering simple, rapid and cost-effective methods for testing water, wastewater, other fluids, solids and air for existence or absence of toxic materials and microbial pollutants.
