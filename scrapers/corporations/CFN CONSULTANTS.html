Product / Service / Licensing

Service Name:
Procurement
CFN works with clients to:

- identify achievable competitive opportunities;

- select partners and negotiate teaming arrangements;

- analyze the acceptability of proposed solutions;

- contribute to red-team analysis and bid response review;

- assist in the development and implementation of Industrial/Regional Benefits (IRB) plans to meet government requirements; and

- develop a suitable communications plan in support of a proposal submission.


Service Name:
Consulting (Marketing)
CFN Partners and Associates continuously gather information about government programs that present business opportunities for industry, with emphasis on those related to defence and security.

CFN interprets and analyzes what is said and written, and then forecasts emerging directions of government projects, plans, budgets and procurement priorities. Written analyses are distributed to clients regularly and when there are major developments of interest.

From the experience and knowledge base of the Partners and Associates, CFN provides advice, counsel and support to clients seeking to provide the capabilities needed to meet the stated and anticipated requirements.



Service Name:
Government Relations
In the development of a strategy to do business with the government and international agencies, CFN works with clients to define goals and objectives, identify immediate and longer term opportunities, and to assess the strengths and weaknesses of the competition.



Service Name:
Industrial Benefits
Major equipment and service expenditures for the military result in the need for contractors to provide industrial and regional benefits, or IRBs. CFN has provided services in this area for several years now and has a centre of excellence capable of dealing with all IRB needs. CFN provides extensive assistance to companies with IRB obligations  from strategy development, to detailed planning, to researching opportunities, to the submission of transaction proposals. With its extensive network throughout the industry, CFN is also able to facilitate linkages between those with IRB obligations and those who can assist in fulfilling them.



Service Name:
Consulting (Canadian Government)
Overall, CFN Consultants offers consulting services to companies wishing
to do business with Canadian federal government departments and
in particular, with the Department of National Defence. CFN's
Partners and Associates offer an in-depth and comprehensive
understanding of departmental corporate administration,
organization and culture including policies, planning, practices
and requirements associated with operations, training, personnel
issues, project management, maintenance and logistics support
programs, the federal contracting process including financial and
budgeting matters, industrial benefit planning and research and
development activities.

Our primary services include, inter alia: assistance in the
formulation of strategic business planning; assessment of
technical requirements, risk, operational needs and budgetary
limitations; analysis of options and trade-offs relating to
federal government programs, plans, budgets and procurement
priorities and trade and procurement policies and regulations.

Other services include assistance to clients in developing
competitive bids and unsolicited proposals. Specifically, CFN
works closely with clients to: identify achievable competitive
opportunities; select partners and negotiate teaming
arrangements; analyse the acceptability of proposed solutions;
participate in red-teaming of proposals and development of
suitable communnications plans in support of a proposal
submission. CFN also works with clients to: establish and
maintain a positive relationship with the customer departments;
develop successful strategies for the selection of team partners
for major subcontracts; develop contingency plans to deal with
unexpected problems; and, develop strategies for the resolution
of disputes. Additionally CFN has a centre of excellence to provide
support for all aspects of industrial and regional benefits issues.
