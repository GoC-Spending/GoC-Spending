Company Profile


Theresa Majeran, a Métis woman from Northern Manitoba (Thompson), is actively involved with the Aboriginal community in Edmonton. She is proud of her culture and works to promote it amongst the young people she meets, while marketing the Belcourt Brosseau Métis Awards program throughout the province of Alberta.

She has been promoting these awards since 2006 and now enjoys many great relationships with the past and present recipients of these awards. As a contractor her business is all about relationship building along with communications and marketing with the community. Guiding our youth is her passion. Theresa is now starting to encourage corporations to donate to the BBMA endowment, adding to the fund will ensure more students are funded for their education in the future. A new fund was start this year to honor the Métis Elder Marge Friedel who was instrumental in presenting more than 300 Sashes to encourage and celebrate the educational accomplishments of youth who receive a BBMA.

She is involved with the Keytayuk (the old ones) Society, whose mandate is to ensure more Aboriginal students graduate from high school. She believes that with increased graduation rates for our people will come a higher demand for post-secondary learning and go on to sustain great careers for Aboriginal people in Canada today.

Karma Business Communications Inc.
Karma began in 2006 with a single consulting contract providing Marketing and Communications services with a Métis focus for the Belcourt Brosseau Métis Awards program, administered by the Edmonton Community Foundation. Under the mentorship and guidance of three Métis entrepreneurial men, Dr. Herb Belcourt, Georges R. Brosseau Q.C. and Orval Belcourt, who are great role models for success in their communities, she became a communications consultant for the Belcourt Brosseau Métis Awards program and started her own business. She enjoys her work with the Oil and Gas industry creating long term relationships that can ensure employment of so youth as the graduation form University and college.

Unique applications:

In my business I utilize the prower of good relationships and energy created by the people I work with.

Cause and effect. For each action there is an equal and opposite reaction. There are a lot of ways to describe karma, but it is essentially the belief that your present circumstances are the product of previous actions, and what you do today will affect what happens tomorrow.

Key / Major clients:

2006-15
The Belcourt Brosseau Métis Awards Annual celebration first held in 2006 has been a very successful cultural event within Albertas Métis community, with attendance of approximately 400 guests each year. Planning all the aspects of a large event including venue/menu selection, designing an evening program with a cultural component (The Métis Sash Ceremony), selecting entertainment, arranging for speakers, volunteers, invitations and guest list, hotel room booking, networking session prior to the event, and budget management both from a cost and revenue generation perspective. Sponsorship raised for this event is now over $148,000.00 for the four years. Over 50 different government, corporate and community partners have now been involved in the celebrations. Approximately 200 Métis Sashes have been presented to the recipients of the Belcourt Brosseau Métis Awards. This event has now grown to be a must attend event which provides an opportunity to share the pride experienced by Métis students as they begin their educational journeys or enter into their careers.

2009
On the first St Albert National Aboriginal Day Event held in Lions Park in partnership with the City of St Albert, Karma secured federal funding from Canadian Heritage for the event including providing access to Elders, cultural entertainment, food and Aboriginal crafts people. Full event coordination service provided for one day celebration.

2008
University of Alberta Faculty of Native Studies Department 20th Anniversary Celebration. Karma produced a video highlighting the four Deans that have been involved in the success of this Faculty.

2008
Crossroads Tipi village at Fox Farms site in Edmonton held with the Aboriginal Womens Professional Association and Non Aboriginal Women. Cultural exchange event.

2007
Aboriginal Womens Professional Association Aboriginal Womens Business Conference. Karma was responsible for booking the venue, ordering the food, arranging for speakers, gathering the gifts and writing the program.

2007
Aboriginal Perspectives Day for 200 teachers and 20 Elders in conjunction with the Indigenous Elders Cultural Resource Circle Society and Alberta Education. We had 5 Tipis set up for teachings and a feast of buffalo stew, bannock and berries was provided. This was an outdoor event held at Fox Farms in Edmonton. Keynote address was held inside a large tent. The event concluded with a Round Dance. All cultural protocols were maintained and demonstrated for the teachers benefit.
