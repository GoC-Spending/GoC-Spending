Success stories:

Alcatel, a multinational technology firm, won a massive $650 million dollar contract with the London Underground Subway Lines to re - signal their subway tracks. Mike Efford Motion Design produced a series of 3D animations that demonstrated how Alcatels systems would operate, showing each piece of gear operating at the right moment in a well  coordinated sequence of action. Executives from the London Underground quickly understood the essence of the technology, and awarded them the contract (reported in the Toronto Star, Saturday Oct. 4, 2003 Alcatel division wins $650 million contract. Will re-signal London tube lines. 100 jobs to be created in Weston").

Sarafinchin Geo  Engineering used animation to help save a piece of the southern shore of Barbados. They presented, using computer animation sequences, a simulation of what would happen if the government of Barbados chose to ignore the dangerous erosion underneath scenic shoreline cliffs. Restaurants, hotels and tourists lives were at risk. 3D computer animations created by Mike Efford Motion Design led to their expertly engineered solution being adopted by the government.

MediFlow used 3D animation to demonstrate the features and benefits of their innovative water  filled medical pillows and took the lead in North American sales in that product category. Broadcast television commercials featured the animations on TV stations across Canada and the United States. After two years the company was so successful they used their cash position to acquire a competitor. Once again, 3D computer animation from Mike Efford Motion Design was able to help customers see exactly why a companys product was better.

Testimonial:

I just wanted to let you know that my client Murray Sarafinchin of Sarafinchin Geo-Engineering Consultants was ecstatic about the final result of the animation you created for him, simulating the potential breaking up of the southern coastline in Barbados over time...
With this outstanding animation, Mr. Sarafinchin was able to successfully demonstrate to his client, the president of Barbados, the precariousness of the coastline situation
