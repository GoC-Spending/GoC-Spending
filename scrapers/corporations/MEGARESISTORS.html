Company Profile


We are a Canadian company specialized in the design and manufacture of high quality power resistors including Neutral Grounding Resistors, Dynamic Braking Resistors and Harmonic Filter Resistors.

We operate a Quality Management System which complies with the requirements of ISO 9001: 2008 for the Design and Manufacture of Power Resistors (Certificate No. CERT-0082524. Expiry Date: February 19, 2018. QMI-SAI)

We have been granted authorization to engage in the business of providing services within the practice of professional engineering in the Province of Ontario in accordance with the provisions of the Professional Engineers Act (Certificate No. 100500344, PEO)

We have one of the widest selections of Neutral Grounding Resistors including the most advanced High Resistance Grounding Systems available in the world.

Our products meet national and international standards (Including NEC, CSA, IEEE, NEMA and IEC) and can be custom made to suit your specific requirements.

Our products have been installed worldwide in trains, ships, mines, power plants, factories, oil platforms, airports built by companies such as ABB, Alcoa, AMEC, Areva, Bechtel, Chevron, Duke, Flour Daniel, General Electric, Jacobs, Magna, Mittal, Schlumberger, Schneider Electric, Sheel, SNC Lavalin, Siemens, Southern Copper, Suncor, Techint, Ultramar, URS, Valero and Worley Parsons among many others.

Dynamic Braking Resistors
The purpose of a dynamic braking resistor is to slow down or to quickly stop a motor by draining excess voltage and keeping it within safe tolerances. Our braking resistors dissipate the excess voltage as heat. This can help to lower the wear and tear of friction braking components, enable faster braking and eliminate the risk of a runaway due to overheating.

When removed from a power supply, most DC motors will act as electrical generators due to their permanent magnets. If a resistor is then connected as a load, the energy produced by the rotational inertia of the DC motor will be dissipated by the resistor slowing down the motor. While AC motors do not have permanent magnets in their rotors, they do have an induced magnetic field created by the rotating magnetic field in the stator. The energy lost in the stator will backfeed into the variable frequency drive (VFD), which will rise the voltage on the DC bus in the VFD. The greater the difference between the output of the VFD and the rotor’s actual speed, the more energy will be fed into the VFD. If the VFD tries to brake the motor too quickly, the voltage will rise too much and damage the VFD. Most VFDs will shut down as a safety feature before this happens, and the motor will coast to a stop by friction alone. With appropriately sized braking resistors the motor can be stopped much more quickly without raising the voltage to unsafe levels.

Motor Starting Resistors
Product Name:
High Resistance Grounding Systems
When the neutral of a system is not grounded it is possible for destructive transient over-voltages to appear from line to ground during normal switching of a circuit having a line-to-ground fault. With a High Resistance Grounding System if a phase-to-ground fault occurs in the system, the Neutral Grounding Resistor will limit the ground fault current to a safe value, generally five Amps or less. The Current Transformer will enable the Ground Fault Relay to read the current and if the current is above the pre-programmed threshold the Ground Fault Relay will provide a visual or an audible alarm. High Resistance Grounding Systems are recommended for installations that require continuous service even after a phase ground fault occurs. At MegaResistors we have the widest range and the most advanced High-Resistance Grounding Systems in the world.
