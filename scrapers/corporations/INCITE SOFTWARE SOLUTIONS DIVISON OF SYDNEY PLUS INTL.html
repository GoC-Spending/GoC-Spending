Product / Service / Licensing

Product Name:
Messenger Workplace
Messenger WorkPlace software from ISS conforms to the X.400
Message Handling System protocol: this software enables the
integration of E-mail systems of a differing nature. The
software runs on Microsoft NT platforms. It has an MTA
configuration and management tool that provides the capability to
remotely manage multiple MTAs.
Messenger WorkPlace Gateways support the following mail and
groupware systems:
· cc:Mail
· Microsoft Mail
· Lotus Notes
· SMTP/MIME (including mailers based on the Internet MIXER
standard)


Product Name:
Messenger 400
ISS's Messenger 400 MTA is a high performance-messaging
server that provides a powerful foundation for services and
applications. Messenger 400 offers a choice of store-and-forward
technology, including SMTP (POP3) for exploiting the benefits of
the Internet, and X.400, the industry standard for
mission-critical applications. And Messenger 400 supports a broad
range of protocols for network connectivity including TCP/IP,
X.25 and APS (async).
Messenger 400 is supported on the following Unix Platforms.

AIX 4.2, 4.3.3
Digital 4.0
HP-UX 10.20, 11.00
Linux Redhat

Solaris 2.6; 2.7; 2.8

systems:
· cc:Mail
· Microsoft Mail
· Lotus Notes
· SMTP/MIME (including mailers based on the Internet MIXER
standard)

Product Name:
Messenger Directory, X.500
Messenger 500 conforms to the X.500 directory services standard,
Standalone directory or can be used to integrate the directories
of proprietary electronic messaging products.

KFKI Meets the Messaging Needs of Hungary's
Government Infrastructure
The flexibility and security provided by X.400
standards-based messaging have prompted the
Hungarian Prime Minister's Office to install a
messaging system that uses as its foundation
Infonet Software Solutions [ISS] X.400-based
Messenger 400 software.
The new message-handling system forms the heart of
the Hungarian Governmental Information
Infrastructure Development (KIIF), an initiative
designed to meet the basic information needs of
the country's political and administrative system.
Timed for implementation prior to the country's
general election, the new system will link the
Prime Minister's Office (PMO) with key Ministries
and government departments an initial total of
1,500 users.
A well-known Hungarian systems integrator, KFKI
Computer Networks, in partnership with EDS and
ICON, acquired the contract in competition against
offerings from IBM, Digital, Bull, Hewlett-Packard
and ICL. The project includes immediate
implementation of an X.400 PRMD and, in the
future, an X.500-based register supply network.




The United States Department of Treasury Uses ISS
Products to Connect More Than
220,000 Employees to an Enterprise-Wide Messaging
Network
The United States Department of Treasury is
comprised of fourteen business units, including
the Internal Revenue Service (IRS), Secret
Service, Customs, Alcohol and Tobacco Services
(ATS), Departmental Offices (DO), Office of Thrift
Supervision (OTS), Office of the Comptroller of
Currency (OCC), Bureau of Engraving and Printing,
Bureau of Public Debt, Financial Management
Service, Office of the Inspector General (OIG),
Financial Crimes Enforcement Network (FINCEN),
Federal Law Enforcement Training Center, and the
United States Mint. While all offices differ in
function, they also vary in size. The IRS
comprises the largest office with more than 93,000
employees while the smallest office, FINCEN,
employs 275. In total, the various units consist
of more than 220,000 employees.
Infonet Software Solutions (ISS) provides
enterprise-wide messaging to the departments
within the Treasury as part of the Treasury
Communications System (TCS) contract awarded to
ISS's industry ally, TRW. This eight-year, $750
million dollar contract manages the Treasury's
entire network, the largest private network in the
U.S.
