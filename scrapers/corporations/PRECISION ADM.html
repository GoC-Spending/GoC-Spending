Company Profile


We have created an environment we call an Advanced Digital Manufacturing™ (ADM) hub in Winnipeg that incorporates additive manufacturing and precision CNC subtractive manufacturing capabilities. What differentiates us from the competition in Canada is that we own the entire process, starting from the design and engineering, through to the actual additive manufacturing, followed by post processing using our subtractive and heat treatment processes. We also have the resources for R&D projects, consulting and CNC CMM Dimension Inspection reporting.

Product / Service / Licensing

Service Name:
Additive Manufacturing (3D Printing)/CNC manufacturing
We have two metal printers up and running at our Buffalo Place facility here in Winnipeg. One machine is dedicated to Titanium to prevent any risk contamination. Here is a list of all the materials that we’re geared up to print on the EOS M 290’s:
• Stainless steel 15-5 (PH1)
• Cobalt Chrome (MP1)
• Titanium (Ti64 & Ti64 ELI)
• Maraging steel (MS1)


We also have a Nakamura NTRX300 robotic multi-axis machining centre up and running as well as an AgieCharmilles Cut 30 P submergible wire EDM with NX CAD/CAM and CAMplete verification software. These machines are in house to complement our AM builds.

Machine specs below;
Multi-Axis Turn-Mill
Nakamura NTRX-300
Max Turning Diameter: 640mm/25.2”
Max Turning Length: 1150mm/45.28”
Y-Axis: 250mm (+/- 125mm)/ 9.84” (+/- 4.92”)
B-Axis: 225° (-120° + 105°)
5-Axis Simultaneous Machining
Renishaw Part Probe and Tool Probe

Submerged Wire EDM
GF Cut30P
Max Workpiece: 1049mm x 800mm x 350mm/41.3” x 31.5” x 13.78”
Axis Travel: 599.4mm x 398.7mm x 350mm/23.6” x 15.7” x 13.78”
Max Workpiece Weight: 2200 lbs

Software applications we have in house;

Altair HyperWorks Desktop; Altair’s core suite of software tools for Finite Element Analysis (FEA) and design optimization. It provides the user with very detailed control of all analysis parameters. It is widely used in the Automotive and Aerospace industries.

HyperMesh; Used for preparing FEA studies by creating meshed Finite Element Models based on geometry imported from CAD packages.

HyperView; Visual interface for producing plots (i.e. stress, displacement, strain, temperature distributions, etc.) and interrogating results produced by solvers.

Altair HyperWorks Solvers; Optistruct & RADIOSS
Optistruct Analysis Solver; Used for simulating Linear and Non-linear behavior of mechanical parts.

Optistruct Optimization Solver; Iterates several analyses to converge at results that are optimized by topology, shape and size for a given set of design requirements.

RADIOSS; Dynamic & Non-Linear Solver used simulating high impact scenarios (such as crash tests).
solidThinking Inspire and Evolve Inspire; Designer level topology optimization tool for generating design concepts. Suggests locations to remove material based loading conditions to lighten parts without compromising structural integrity.

Evolve; NURBS based modeler for modeling over the optimized geometry produced by Inspire. Used for creating smooth/sleek industrial designs.

SolidWorks with Simulation Premium; CAD modeling tool for creating new mechanical designs and the manufacturing drawings.
SolidWorks Simulation Premium; Simple, user-friendly tool used for basic linear, non-linear and dynamic FEA.

Materialise 3-maticSTL; Prepare data for a quick and efficient finite element analysis.
Clean/smooth topology optimized geometry directly in STL models.
Design or repair missing or badly scanned components.
Efficiently create textures, perforations and patterns on STL data.
Design lightweight structures that lower weight without compromising strength or that intentionally allow the variable flexibility/rigidness within a part.

Altair SIMLAB; Creates mathematical design optimization experiments to fine-tune designs.

AcuSolve CFD Solutions; Altair’s Computation Fluid Dynamics package for simulate fluid flow in channel and open volume spaces.

Moldex3D Moldex3D; is a CAE product for the plastics injection molding industry, enabling in-depth simulation of a wide application range of injection molding processes.


We have created an environment we call an Advanced Digital Manufacturing™ (ADM) hub in Winnipeg that incorporates additive manufacturing and precision CNC subtractive manufacturing capabilities. What differentiates us from the competition in Canada is that we own the entire process, starting from the design and engineering, through to the actual additive manufacturing, followed by post processing using our subtractive and heat treatment processes. We also have the resources for R&D projects, consulting and CNC CMM Dimension Inspection reporting.
