Company Profile


Claremont Communications is a leader in the Global IT and Software Engineering Services field using
proven project management methodologies to achieve outcomes for your competitive advantage.

Whether your organzation is large, medium or small, together with our partners, Claremont will consult with you to streamline requirements, derive efficient plans, implement effective solutions and assist your organization every step of the way.

Since 1995, we have worked closely with governments, business, organizations and individuals to improve their workplace health and environment. These efforts have brought into place "best practices" for all concerned.

At Claremont, were proud to work with our partners Intel, Microsoft, IBM, Oracle, VMWare, Cisco, 3Com, Benq, Polycom, MySQL, Samsung and other major worldwide corporations to provide technology to meet the needs of sustainable development projects and bring technological innovation to the forefront.

Energy Conservation: Wise use and careful management of energy resources by reducing wasteful energy use, using energy for a given purpose more efficiently, or reducing energy use altogether.

At Claremont Communications' we believe each of us must lessen our dependency on non-renewable resources and reduce the costs connected with excessive energy use.

We have initiated working partnerships with government, industry and organizations to achieve the lofty goals of the Canadian Government including certification of BACnet systems.

Unique applications:

- Wrote technical security document on implementing remote interfaces to the Building Information Database Server (BIDS) project for PWGSC. This included the evaluation of current security measures for PWGSC Databases and recommended means to ensure continued security. Recommendations adopted.

- Claremont Communications designed, planned, and implemented successful muti-vendor demonstration of the Canadian Automated Building Protocol gateways and Workstation. This project required the linking for remote control of three Health Canada Buildings (Laboratory Centre for Disease Control, Banting Research Building and Health Protection Building) Tunney's Pasture Campus for continuous monitoring. An additional aspect was the linking of the Radiation Protection Building 15 km away through fiber cabling. This was the first such demonstration of CAB Protocol outside of the Lab.

- Managed project to create neural network fault detection and diagonsis program to troubleshoot Building Automation Program.
