Company Profile


MultiCultural Business Solutions Overview

MultiCultural Business Solutions Inc. (MCB) provides cross-culture consulting services that help organizations and individuals manage cultural differences and turn the challenges into opportunities.

At the organization level, MCB works with:
 Organizations in a wide range of public and private sectors, e.g. professional services, financial services, engineering and natural resources, to build an effective multicultural workforce and provide value to their culturally diverse customers
 Professional associations and regulatory bodies to enhance their service to internationally trained professionals
 Universities and colleges to help international students to adapt to the Canadian study environment
 Government and non-profit organizations to help skilled immigrants integrate into the Canadian workplace

MCB provides training workshops and presentations to
 Senior leaders on adapting their leadership style to foster an organization culture that capitalizes on the strength of diversity
 Line managers on building productive multicultural teams and increasing their collective effectiveness;
 HR professionals on developing programs to more effectively recruit, integrate and develop culturally diverse employees
 Front-line staff of service organizations, e.g. hospitals, banks, on better servicing culturally diverse clients;
 Staff and board members of regulatory bodies and professional associations on building cultural sensitivity into their programs and practices
 Settlement workers on helping new immigrants finding professional work

MCB provides consulting services to a number of organizations in the following area:
 Design and facilitate structured team building off-sites for multicultural teams
 Review leadership development programs to incorporate cultural sensitivity into the program
 Review recruitment and selection processes to ensure that they do not eliminate candidates for reasons not related to their ability to perform the job
At the individual level, MCB coaches
 Leaders and managers to increase their effectiveness of working with their multicultural team members, managers and peers
 Expatriates both inbound to and outbound from Canada to be successful in unfamiliar environment and continue to grow their career
 Internationally trained professionals find jobs that make use of their skills and integrate into the Canadian work environment

Our Approach combines four key elements to help our clients understand and reap the benefits of cultural diversity.
1. We focus on invisible differences. Culture is often compared to an iceberg: with visible and invisible differences. Our focus is on the invisible. We help our clients understand why culturally different people interact professionally in ways that seem strange and wrong to them.
2. We believe most people have the best intentions at heart. Most people do not want to discriminate, to be unfair, or to be disrespectful. The challenge is that, when people do not recognize what they dont know, they usually place the blame on others for undesirable events. We want to help people understand how cultural differences are demonstrated in workplace behaviours and work together to find solutions for productive interactions.
3. We approach cultural differences from a practical perspective. All our work is rooted in real-life experience in the workplace. We understand that, at the end of the day, our clients do not want to become cultural diversity experts  they want to get their work done and reach their business objectives within a multicultural context.
4. We provide depth of analysis. We want to make a measurable difference in the effectiveness of our clients by providing them solutions that are grounded in a root-cause analysis of the situation and have been extensively tested in many organizations.


Our Team

Lionel Laroche, Ph.D., P. Eng.

Lionel received his education in France and the United States and started his career in Canada as an engineer. The cultural adaptation process after immigrating to Canada and working on international assignments taught him so much that he decided to use the knowledge to help professionals from all over the world succeed and organizations benefit from the opportunities brought by a diverse workforce.

Over the past fifteen years, Lionel has provided cross-cultural training, coaching and consulting services to over 20,000 people through a wide range of business, government, academic, professional and non-profit organizations in 13 countries (Canada, the U.S., Bermuda, Mexico, Peru, Belgium, Switzerland, France, the UK, Turkey, Hong Kong, China and Korea).

Lionel is a thought provoking and educational speaker / facilitator who presented at over 200 conferences and events across Canada and the United States. He is the author of two books: (1) Managing Cultural Diversity in Technical Professions; (2) Recruiting, Retaining and Promoting Culturally Different Employees.

Lionel holds a Ph.D. in Chemical Engineering from the California Institute of Technology, USA and a Diplôme dingénieur polytechnicien from the École Polytechnique de Paris, France.

Caroline Yang, CHRP, CCP

Caroline has fifteen years of human resources experience working with Nortel and Compaq in China and ATI (now AMD), TD Bank and Manulife in Canada. In her role as a human resources business partner, Caroline has worked with a diverse workforce and supported multicultural teams of highly skilled technical professionals.

Providing services to a broad range of public and private sector organizations, Caroline has worked with GE Canada, Total E&P Canada, Scotiabank, City of Kitchener, Citizenship and Immigration Canada, University of Ottawa and Ontario Regulators for Access Consortium.

Caroline was interviewed by Radio Canada International to share her experience of integrating skilled immigrants into the Canadian workplace. She has multiple publications on leadership, teamwork, global HR policies and total rewards by HRPA, WorldatWork and CERC. She will present at HRPAs 2013 Annual Conference.

Caroline graduated from City University of Hong Kong with a Post-Graduate Certificate in Business Administration and a CIDA (Canadian International Development Agency) training program at Simon Fraser University in translation.

Product / Service / Licensing

Product Name:
Organization and Culture Questionnaire (TM)
With this questionnaire, respondents identify their own cultural preferences in three key cultural areas: Hierarchy, Individualism and Risk Tolerance. The results are used to identify potential issues for expatriates by comparing their individual scores and responses with the distribution of scores and responses obtained by professionals in the country where this expatriate will be located.

Service Name:
Intercultural training
ITAP Canada provides companies with the tools necessary for
conducting business effectively in the global marketplace. We
offer solutions to cross cultural problems including relocation,
emigration to Canada, repatriation and diversity in the
workplace through customized training programs and consulting
services. The programs are delivered by certified, highly
trained personnel.

Service Name:
Preparation for International Business
Because doing business in an unfamiliar country or region of the
world can be fraught with challenges, ITAP Canada has designed a
training probram to help employees conduct international
business transactions. "Doing Business Internationally" provides
practical information about interacting with foreign
professionas in business settings and explores the impact of
culture and values on management, work and communication. The
training program focusses on one particular country or region of
interest to our clients. This one or two day workshop includes a
simulation of problems arising in cross-cultural situations,
videos and practical exercises.

Service Name:
Training programs in Cultural Diversity
Canadian corporations are deeply affected by the recent arrival
of many skilled immigrants in Canada and the rapid change in
their workd force demographics. To help companies overcome the
challenges of internal cultural diversity and reap the
corresponding benefits, ITAP Canada has designed training
programs that address various aspects of corporate diversity.
These include:
Interviewing New Canadians: Overcoming Cultural Barriers to
Skills Identification
Managing a Culturally Diverse Workforce
Working in A Culturally Diverse Company
Effective Communication Skills Training to Help Bridge Cultural
Differences is an individual coaching program designed to help
New Canadians understand the main features of the work,
communication and management style preferences of Canadians.

Service Name:
Preparation for International Relocation
Since expatriates are responsible for representing their
company's best interests, successful international relocation is
key to the success of a company's global operations. Because of
the high failure rates observed in foreign assignments, ITAP
Canada has designed training programs that prepare expatriates
and their families for the most crucial phases, their departure
and their return. These programs are offered both to Canadian
expatriates and foreign employees relocating to Canada:
Living in Country X explores how the employees and their
families can adjust to the way of life in the destination country
Working in Country X trains employees (and their spouses, if
applicable) in the skills needed to adjust faster to a new
position in a new country.
Working Spouse helps expatrates' spouses who plan on working in
their destination country adjust to the situation.
Children addresses the needs of children aged 7 to 12, providing
them with their own training manual and global games.

Service Name:
Preparation for Repatriation
Returning home is often more difficult than making the initial
adjustment to a new culture. Therefore, ITAP Canada has designed
two programs to alleviate "return shock". These one day programs
ease family adjustment into their old, and at the same time, new
country by focusing on the changes that have taken place during
their time away.
Returning to Live in Canada focuses on lifestyle changes.
Returning to Work in Canada focuses on changes in the workplace.

Service Name:
Cross-cultural Training Services
MCB Solutions helps people working in multicultural environments
achieve their professional objectives by providing them with the
right training, coaching and consulting services.
