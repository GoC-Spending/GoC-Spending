Company Profile


Infinite Jib™ Inc. designs and manufactures small Unmanned Aerial Systems (UAS). specializes in the production of premium, commercial grade, heavy-lift multirotor aircraft systems, which operate a wide range of payload options. This design approach provides payload flexibility to our industrial customers that service a wide range of applications.

Infinite Jib™ Inc. aircraft have been in service for years satisfying the remote aerial needs of US Corp of Engineers, construction corporations, engineering and survey companies. Our reliable reputation is founded on our emphasis on excellence in manufacturing and the stock we take with the care of our customers. Founded in 2011, Infinite Jib™ Inc. is a private, proudly Canadian owned company operating in Schomberg, Ontario, Canada.

Product / Service / Licensing

Product Name:
Infinite Jib Surveyor UAS
Infinite Jib's are considered as one of the most powerful UAS (under 25kg) capable of operating wind conditions that typically ground other drones and deliver the critical quality of aerial data.

The Surveyor UAS payload is an integrated DSLR camera that captures high-resolution, geo-tagged images. The camera is mounted to a stabilization gimbal system that neutralizes undesirable aircraft motion. The range of positional GPS accuracy achieved with these aircraft is: on the XY 1.5cm to 2cm and on the Z between 2-3cm, equal to the accuracy of the ground control points used in the survey. Profiles generated by the system are in the sub cm scale. The high quality DSLR camera and lens combination delivers the demanding precision for structural inspection applications.

The flight control system provides manual control as well as auto takeoff and landing, position hold and return to home location as well as waypoint flight programming.

In addition to the live video feed of the camera’s perspective, there are two additional point of view cameras that provide a forward view of the aircraft and another positioned downward facing.


Infinite Jib™ Inc. Surveyor aircraft are considered to be complaint small Unmanned Air Vehicle (UAV) system (25 Kg or less) that is operated within visual line compliance with the design standards for small UAVs as outlined in Transport Canada Staff Instruction (SI) No. 623-001.

Instruction program includes UAV Pilot training compliant with Transport Canada TP15263 criteria as well as an in-depth system operation/maintenance program.
