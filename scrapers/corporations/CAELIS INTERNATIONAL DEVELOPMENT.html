Company Profile


Caelis is a specialized consultancy dealing with ICT (Information and Communications Technology) in development, with a focus on education, e-commerce, trade development, eco-tourism, tele-health, government on-line, enabling communications policy and regulatory environments, and affordable and reliable networking alternatives to provide voice and data connectivity for developing countries. Our principal clients are the World Bank and the Asian Development Bank. Caelis has an associate roster of over 100 highly qualified experts, institutions, organizations and firms.

Product / Service / Licensing

Service Name:
ICT Consultancy
South Asia Regional Economic Cooperation (SASEC) Information Highway development project funded by Asian Development Bank - The objective is to set up high speed Internet access Community e-Centers (CeCs) in more than 100 remote villages in Bhutan, the mountainous north of Bangladesh and of India and Nepal, which will bring distance education, telemedicine, electronic mail, social networking, and e-commerce opportunities to the villagers. Our role is the development of regional research and training centres (RTCs) in the major tertiary institutions in each country that develop the courses and applications that will be provided to the CeCs. There is a major effort to build the ICT servicing capacity in each of these centres, and to design and implement appropriate human and hardware/software infrastructure capability and new technologies. Team of seven, including four engineers, an Internet designer-trainer, and a senior educator.
Hashemite Kingdom of Jordan, reporting to the Minister: Improvement of practices in policy, strategic planning and operations in this multi-donor $480 million education reform project. Formulate recommendations and methodology on delegation of authority, competitive pricing and quality of connectivity on a national basis to provide e-learning and specialized services to the MoEs 70,000 employees and two million students. Negotiations with telecommunications carriers, ISPs and content providers. Review of quality of service for the national network and the 70,000 PCs and software in the MoEs facilities. Establishment of continuing dialogue with public and private stakeholders to develop a new policy framework and introduce a Policy and Strategic Planning Secretariat to build capacity within the Ministry. Liaison with several other donors to coordinate contributions in technology, e-content, network panning and architecture. Advise Minister and General Secretaries on policy and planning issues and agree appropriate implementation mechanisms and controls by MoE management. Consolidation and structuring of knowledge resources and precedents under six easily accessible headings for all MoE staff. Development of appropriate usage policy for MoE assets, and setup of Jordan First Procurement Strategy to build national capacity, introduction of more productive and timely procurement strategy, IP repository, deployment strategy for technology to schools through the build-up of the human resource infrastructure. Recruitment and mentoring of a Jordanian national CTO.
Sri Lanka: planning and implementation of Asia-Pacifics first TCP/IP high capacity network for education (NDEN). Caelis International was the international lead for the project consortium. $55 million public-private sector project to provide affordable access to all learners in the country through more than 150 Multi Media Centres, adding broadband residential, institutional and business connectivity, and support at the lowest prices in the region. National network uses direct access to international gateway and undersea fibre; established ISP status, and designed and implemented 100 to 400 megabit wireless backbone with high performance cacheing multipurpose consolidated servers. Rated as very successful by client and ADB.
May 2005  ADB  Contract to research current global practices and benefits in the use of Information and Communications Technologies (ICTs) in education in developed countries and determine how they may apply to the Banks developing member countries in Asia  Pacific. Detailed status reports on ICT policies and utilization of ICTs in education and training for each of the Banks member countries and report on connectivity, content, institutional and access issues. Identification of barriers to wider scale introduction of ICTs in developing countries and presentation of range of alternative solutions based on best practices and success stories elsewhere. Recommendations on improving access to knowledge in rural and remote areas, and on integration of ICT expertise in social sector field operations for the Banks portfolio managers. Review of ADBs Education Policy and ICT Strategy to better integrate and harmonize cross-cutting policy and operational priorities. Proposals formulated and adopted by client to improve staff project evaluations and to identify bankable projects with ICT components. See http://www.adb.org/Documents/Reports/ICT-Education-Training/default.asp
December  March 2003  ADB  Contract to develop a six year economic and social scenario for public  private partnerships in a national TCP/IP high capacity network to support distance education with over 100 Telecenters. Preparation and validation of business plan with over 30 domestic and international stakeholders. Supported proposal including drafting of all documents with permanent staff, including Presidents approval document, internal approvals and loan agreement signature with client.
August  November 2002  ADB  Technical Assistance Project: Distance Education Modernization, Sri Lanka  As senior Information Communications Technologies (ICT) and Business Development expert, and project director for the contractor, to manage an extensive consultation with key stakeholders in government and the private sector to determine the most appropriate public-private business operating model for a national distance education system, identify and review policy and regulatory issues, consult on network architecture and scalability from hub to telecentres, forecast costs and revenue under various scenarios, and determine stakeholder buy-in for a national overlay TCP/IP (Internet) network for postsecondary education and community development. In addition, ICT equipment, software, personnel and service budgets have been developed with detailed specifications for tendering, as well as comprehensive human resource and training requirements for the initial six years of the project.

October 2001  August 2002 - ADB RETA, ICT in the Pacific  Team Leader to assess the current and planned status in Information and Communications Technologies in each Pacific ADB member country, brief key stakeholders of best practices elsewhere, recommend broad policy and regulatory options for governments to stimulate use of ICT, identify needs and possible solutions, scope bankable projects, and prepare Technical Assistance documents with designated officials in client countries in various fields such as distance education, tele-health, e-commerce, governance, emergency preparedness, national ICT policies. Organise and moderate donor and beneficiary seminars in collaboration with ADB and other regional donors, and develop proposals to a stage where they can be advanced by ADB staff. Tasks include recommendations on alternative reliable and cost effective means of connecting bank staff to headquarters for data, fax, video communications, while they are on mission in remote and isolated areas; development of templates for national ICT strategies for member countries, and finalization of strategy documents in selected client countries.
February - June 2001  ADB ICT in Finance in Nepal  To recommend a communications and transactional system providing transparency for all local banks, financial institutions, microfinance operations, and the central bank. Project scope included mapping and roles of credit bureau, payments and settlements, chattel mortgages, electronic transaction processing, POS services, and smart cards for small business and families in remote regions, etc. Delivered to Bank approved (in principle) project and loan documents, detailed TORs, and contractual papers.

June 1989  June 2001  The World Bank  Policy and Regulatory Strengthening in Sri Lanka as Project Director. Themes included: Interconnection, new telecommunications and broadcasting legislation, public hearings process, security issues, rural communications, spectrum management, wireless communications, issues in competition, cost elements, emergency telecommunications, and policies to stimulate the introduction of new technologies and investment. Recruited, selected and managed 36 experts over the three year project.

June 1989  July 2002  World Bank, CIDA and Industry Canada  Policy and regulatory support for the Ministry of Communications and the TRAI  India. Project director. Spectrum management, equipment and systems type-approval, policy on competition, advanced satellite and wireless communications etc. Analysis of key firms in the Indian ICT sector and of their potential in regional and international markets. Review of partnership and collaborative arrangements in technology parks in Hyderabad and in Bangalore. Identification of key factors for success.
