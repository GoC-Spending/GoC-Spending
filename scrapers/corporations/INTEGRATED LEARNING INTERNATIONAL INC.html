Product / Service / Licensing

Service Name:
Educational and Training Services
Integrated Learning International Incorporated (ILI Inc.), a
wholly-owned Canadian Company, was founded to respond to the
Training and Development needs of employees at all levels of
Government and of the private sector.

We offer proven and unparalleled experience, expertise and
knowledge. Our services are offered in both official languages
and our training experts have developed a reputation for
achieving the highest possible standards of excellence.

We conduct our business based on values which promote a
reasonable cost to our clients, the highest quality of service,
responsiveness to our clients needs, innovative solutions which
are focused on sound and real concern for the human dimension.
All delivery methods encompass a practical application in the
classroom as well as in the work environment.

All course offerings can be delivered in generic format or can
be customized for specific client needs.

Contract / Procurement Management:

The purpose of courses in this area is to provide employees and
managers, who are required to carry out purchasing / contracting
activities, with a knowledge of the policies, procedures and
guidelines used in the procurement of goods, services and
construction.

Courses range from (1) day, How to Use Your Acquisition Card, to
(5) days, Advanced Contracting, with a variety of courses in
between. These courses are aimed at participants ranging from
operational support staff, supervisor and senior managers to
procurement and contracting officers supporting the operational
areas with more in-depth knowledge and experience in the full
array of processes involved in the procurement field. Included
in all the courses, to varying degrees, is a review of policies
and guidelines, delegation of authority, risks and liabilities,
reporting requirements, the principles of materiel management
and the management and competitive processes necessary to be
successful in this area.

Some courses also include the Trade Agreements, their impact on
Canadian Procurement and the Alternate Dispute Resolution
Mechanisms, used in the private and public sectors to resolve
contract conflicts.

Risk Management, In Contracting and Indemnification

This two (2) day course allows participants to review and
discuss the fundamentals of the Treasury Board policy on Risk
Management and the interim policy on Indemnification in
Contracting. The course outlines the main elements of a risk
management process and makes contracting officers more aware of
the types of issues they may have to deal with and when it is
appropriate to seek legal advice. The differences between
indemnification and insurance are also reviewed as well as
discovering when either applies.

Risk Management,In Property Management

This one and one-half (1 ½) day course furthers the
understanding of the concepts and principles of risk management
as they apply to Property Management and the Alternate Form of
Service Delivery. The four phases of the risk management
process are discussed and applied in practical situations.
Participants also gain a better understanding of their roles and
responsibilities in matters of risk management in both Property
Management and in the Alternate Form of Service Delivery.

Project Management: (Planning & Control)

Courses in Project Management are available from a two-day (2)
version to the five(5) days version described below.

This comprehensive five (5) day course takes middle managers,
team members and specialists through the concepts and technical
aspects of managing projects. It includes practical techniques
and skills required to efficiently plan, manage, control and
complete a project. Subjects covered are policies,
interpersonal skills, management skills, planning,
implementation and control. Network planning techniques such as
CPM and PERT are also discussed and can be the target of a
course in themselves. Computer software demonstrations and
applications are also available.

Project Management (for Informatics Projects)

This five (5) day course is designed for participants who will
undertake management or leadership functions within an
Informatics project. Within the framework of Informatics
guidelines and standards, participants will learn how to plan an
Informatics project and how to carry that project to successful
implementation. The structure of the course is based on
applying the project management techniques of planning,
organizing, controlling and completing an Informatics project.
Project risk is a major theme throughout the course, as well as
addressing the Systems Development Life Cycle (SDLC).
Leadership issues are also addressed.

Business Management Skills

Negotiation Skills

This two (2) or three (3) day course is designed for officers
and managers who participate in the planning and conduct of
formal negotiations on behalf of their organization, who
negotiate for goods, services, land, building leases, joint
funding of projects or any number of daily situations. The
negotiation cycle: planning, conduct and conclusion of a
negotiated agreement, will be covered on this course. The
theory and practical exercises include the different types of
negotiations, from single-issue, single party to multi-
issues, multiparty. It also emphasizes the importance of
thorough planning, as well as strategies and tactics that help
you achieve your objectives.

Alternate Dispute Resolution and Mediation

This three (3) day course introduces Alternative Dispute
Resolution (ADR) as a field which recognizes conflict as a
natural process and takes a multi-disciplinary approach to
dealing with problems, disputes and deep-seated conflicts.
Participants will gain analytical skills and intervention
techniques necessary to assist parties to negotiate agreements
where resolutions are found based on interests in a win-win
environment. Mediation is treated as a form of assisted
negotiation, processes that encourage and support collaborative,
non-adversarial and consensus-based outcomes are emphasized.

Cost Benefit Analysis

This five (5) day course takes participants through an
introduction to cost-benefit analysis where you will be able to
evaluate CBAs presented to you. The training is theory and
applied practice of actual analysis which allows the
participants to evaluate and understand outcomes and conducting
risk and decision analysis. Participants will be able to
construct spreadsheets of costs and benefits over time, then put
a value on intangible benefits and costs, as well as explain
price inflation, discount rates and adjustments for distorted
market prices.

Problem Solving / Decision Making

This three (3) day course is designed for all participants who
wish to improve their problem solving / decision - making
skills. In a multi-phase process, you will learn how to clearly
state your problem, diagnose your problem, generate alternatives
and select the solution that best meets your needs. You will
also learn how to analyze and plan actions to deal with future
effects.

Managing Teams / Team Building

This two (2) day course is designed for project leaders and team
members who work together in less traditional environment. It
examines individual behaviors, interpersonal dynamics and the
facilitator leadership style that are conducive to successful
teamwork. It will provide participants with the understanding
to best display the skills, knowledge, experience and commitment
of people working together in teams.

Managing Change

This three (3) day course helps participants learn to manage
change effectively. It deals with change on a practical level.
Participants use real examples of change situations to work on
throughout the course. The worksheets used can become jobs aids
back at the office. Participants learn about working
relationships with management teams and internal consultants,
and how to identify the process of change, perform diagnostics,
plan change, implement change and evaluate results.

Train the Trainer

This three (3) day course is designed for anyone who is called
upon to teach in their area of expertise. This course will show
participants how to prepare and deliver training in a relaxed
and professional manner. The presentations, demonstrations,
discussions and exercises include learning how to overcome
nervousness, prepare lesson plans, hold the attention and
interest of participants, manage course time and deal with
problems.

Designing Organization Structures

This three (3) day course is designed for Human Resources
Advisors or managers and superiors who anticipate the need for
structural reviews within their organization. This practical
rather than a theoretical course will introduce the key issues
and concepts in organization structure design, how to plan and
manage the change process and how to implement and evaluate the
new design.

Facilitation Skills

This two (2) day course deals with how facilitators help work
groups become more effective. It is designed to integrate the
theory and practice of group facilitators. It describes a set
of core values and congruent principles and shows how to use
various techniques to apply the principles in practice. The
course is designed for employees who want to become or already
are facilitators and for participants who want to use
facilitation skills without formally filling the role. It will
explore techniques such as Nominal Group Technique and Focus
Group Technique. It will explore how to begin and end
meetings, helping the group to solve problems, how to set and
follow ground rules, dealing with emotions and working with
other facilitators.
