Company Profile


Jones Packaging Inc. is an internationally recognized leader in delivering unique and integrated solutions for printed packaging, contract packaging, and medication dispensing and delivery.

Led by the fourth generation of the Jones family, the company has four manufacturing and/or warehousing sites in Canada as well as distribution facilities in the UK and Spain. With an expansive range of packaging products and services, the companys roster of clients includes many of the most recognized global pharmaceutical and consumer brands. As well, Jones supports the medication dispensing market through consumables, cart and kiosk sales to pharmacies, hospitals and long-term care facilities across Canada and Europe.

Jones has always been a proud leader in driving innovation and was the first to bring a number of important healthcare packaging advances to the Canadian marketplace, including the introduction of the first child safety vials to the Canadian pharmacy market and co-development a blister card designed for multi-medication regimes.

Over the years Jones has also forged strong alliances with some of the most widely respected international packaging industry organizations. These global relationships give Jones a high profile within its industry and also provide the company with the ongoing opportunity to be first to bring new ideas and best practices to North American and European markets.

Alliances:

Strategic alliances:

Jones is particularly interested in growth opportunities through partnership, distribution, acquisition or start-up businesses in areas such as:
- Partnering with international pharmaceutical/nutraceutical/health and beauty companies to package their products for the North American market
- Acting as a consultant to such companies to assist with navigation of the regulatory environment and distribution
- Exploring sales opportunities in new markets for healthcare packaging including monitored dose systems and point of use distribution
- Identifying new healthcare packaging products for possible distribution in Canada
