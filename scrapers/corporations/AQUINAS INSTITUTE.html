Company Profile


Aquinas Institute Corporation dba Aquinas Institute is anchored on the principles of the Catholic faith, knowledge and charity.

Our mission is to help people; to provide quality service to our clients; and to treat the people working with us with integrity, dignity and professionalism, as well as, to provide them with a happy working environment, a sense of belonging and above average pay rates in the hope that their happiness with the Corporation would be extended to our clients and to their own respective families.

Aquinas Institute was officially registered as a corporation in the Province of British Columbia on September 9, 2005. The AIC holds a current business licence from the City of Vancouver.

We are committed to the pursuit of excellence in interpreting & translation services as well as in adult education & training. See the Vancouver Sun newspaper, Open for Business, January 23, 2006, p. D4.

Aquinas Institute is a leading interpreting & translation service company and a leading training center for court interpreters in the Province of British Columbia.

Aquinas Institute is the specialist in court-judicial-legal interpreting services in British Columbia. We have been providing court-judicial-legal interpreting services since 2006. We provide interpreting services in medical cases only when these cases are parts of court litigations.

The Corporation has three (3) websites on the Internet, namely:

www.aquinasinstitute.ca
www.catholicmeditations.ca.
www.interpretersandtranslators.ca

The Corporation operates its business activities at 205 - 938 Howe Street, Vancouver, B.C. V6Z 1N9 Canada. Its office is located in a modern, 11-storey building, which is directly opposite the B.C. Law Courts.

Aquinas Institute Corporation is an equal opportunity employer. The Corporation does not discriminate on the basis of race, national or ethnic origin, color, religion, language, sex, beauty or disability. We welcome visible minorities to be part of our company.


INTERPRETING & TRANSLATION SERVICES

Aquinas Institute officially opened its interpreting & translation service section in January 2006. The AIC has the largest scope of over 250 languages/dialects and over 3,500 registered linguists to make the language needs of any client a priority. To obtain a copy of the language list, please send an email to info@aquinasinstitute.ca.

Aquinas Institute is committed to providing quality, accurate, confidential, punctual, ethical, and professional interpreting services.

Aquinas professional interpreters are native speakers, certified, court accredited, experienced, especially trained and/or qualified to provide the best services.

We guarantee our quality interpreting services. If a client is not satisfied with our interpreter's performance, we do not charge the client for the interpreter's services.

Our clientele includes the following:

Canadian Federal Court; Canadian Tax Court; BC Courts: Supreme Court and Provincial Courts; Public Prosecution of Canada; Immigration & Refugee Board of Canada; BC Ministry of Justice; BC Crown Counsel Offices; BC Probation Offices; BC Ministry of Children and Family Development; BC Human Rights Tribunal; Vancouver Police Department; Surrey RCMP; Surrey Pre-Trial Services Centre; BC Worker's Compensation Appeal Tribunal; BC Government Employees Union; Arbitration & Mediation Offices; Small, Medium and Large Legal Firms; Medical & Physiotherapy Offices; Insurance Companies; Small, Medium and Large Corporations; Non-Profit Societies; and Private Individuals.

Aquinas Institute has provided quality interpreting services in high profile media cases in the B.C. Supreme Court to the full satisfaction of the justices, lawyers and all involved parties.

Aquinas Institute provided conference interpreting services in the French language at the 9th World Down Syndrome Congress, which was held on August 24-25-26, 2006 at the Vancouver Convention and Exhibition Centre.

Aquinas Institute is committed to providing accurate translation services. Only certified translators are assigned to handle translation services. If there is no certified translator in the requested foreign language, only an experienced translator is assigned to handle the translation service.

We provide professional translation services for the following written materials:

Academic Certificates, Diplomas & Transcripts; Advertisements, Movies and Scripts; Affidavits, Estate Documents and Wills; Birth, Marriage & Divorce Documents; Business Documents, Letters & Proposals; Handbooks. Manuals and Websites; Immigration Documents; Legal, Medical and Technical Documents; and Literary Books.

Aquinas interpreters and translators are governed by the Code of Ethics of the B.C. Courts, the Code of Ethics of the Society of Translators and Interpreters of British Columbia, the Aquinas Code of Ethics, and the Standard Code of Business Ethics.


AQUINAS COURT-JUDICIAL-LEGAL INTERPRETER TRAINING

Since 2006 Aquinas Institute has been offering its well-known training trademark Court-Judicial-Legal Interpreter Training in British Columbia to interpreters and to the general public.

The Aquinas Court-Judicial-Legal Interpreter Training is the fastest way, the most practical way and the most economical way to learn court interpreting. This concise yet comprehensive training will give the student everything that he/she needs to become a professional interpreter in court-judicial-legal settings. THE NEW GRADUATE IS READY TO INTERPRET IN ALL TYPES OF COURT HEARINGS.

New graduates have interpreted in trial cases in BC Provincial Courts and/or BC Supreme Court to the full satisfaction of justices/judges, crown counsels, defense counsels, court staff, clients, witnesses, media and the gallery audience.

For additional information and/or to register for the Court-Judicial-Legal Interpreter Training, please send an email to info@aquinasinstitute.ca.


AQUINAS CHARITIES AROUND THE WORLD

Aquinas Institute has given hope and work to many people from diverse cultures. We are one of the best paying agencies and the fastest paying agency to interpreters in British Columbia and, perhaps, in Canada.

Aquinas Institute provides interpreters for legal aid cases as a part of its charitable commitment to the community.

Aquinas Institute is committed to sharing its acquired resources with the needy around the world.

Aquinas Institute offers annual scholarships to needy university students in Canada and the Philippines. In 2014 our goal is to give away $10,000.00 scholarships grants.

Aquinas Institute gives donations to local, national and international charities. In the past years, AIC supported Snehalaya, a shelter home for street kids in Guwahati, India; Hogar de Nazareth, an orphanage in Mexico; the Paul Lawton Memorial Scholarship in Kingston, Ontario; parishes; and youth groups in Vancouver and the Lower Mainland.

For more information about Aquinas Charities, please see www.aquinasinstitute.ca.


HISTORICAL ROOTS

Aquinas Institute was originally registered with the Government of British Columbia as a sole proprietorship by Teresita Nixon in December 1997; however, it was only four years later in 2001 that it was able to start its business operations. Aquinas Institute operated as a charitable organization from 2001 to 2005. The talents, time and efforts of its personnel were used to raise funds, which, in turn, were donated to local, national and international charities.

Aquinas Institute received the Papal Blessings of Pope Benedict XVI in 2006 through its spiritual adviser, Msgr. Pedro Lopez-Gallo, P.A. and the Papal Blessings of St. Pope John Paul II in 2000 through Fr. Paul Pang, one of the members of its voluntary advisory board.

Aquinas Institute is dedicated to the Holy Family - Jesus, Mary and Joseph. It is committed to carry out its mission in ways that draw inspiration from the examples of the works of St. Thomas Aquinas, St. Pope John Paul II, St. Anthony of Padua, St. Therese of Lisieux, and Blessed Teresa of Calcutta.

In its early establishment, Aquinas Institute had a voluntary advisory board, which included Msgr. Pedro Lopez-Gallo, P.A.
(President, Matrimonial Tribunal of the Catholic Archdiocese of Vancouver); Fr. Nicolas Tumbelaka (Priest, Archdiocese of Vancouver); Lukose Cheruvalel, SDB (Founder, Snehalaya, Guwahati, Assam, India); Fr. John Gibson (Rector, Seminary of the Archdiocese of Bo and Sierra Leone, Africa); Fr. Paul Pang (Director, World Overseas Chinese Apostolate Commission, Rome, Italy); Dean Francisco G. Reyes (Retired Dean, Faculty of Engineering, University of Santo Tomas, Manila, Philippines); Ms Filomena Sison (Lay Advisor).

Aquinas Institute organized and/or sponsored free, highly successful religious events for groups of 300-800 participants from the Catholic community in Vancouver and the Lower Mainland. These events included Advent Retreats, Lenten Retreats, Holy Hour & Conference for the Youth, and The Seminar on the Holy Eucharist.

In October 2004, Aquinas Institute released its first publication, a special, beautiful and timeless 250-page book entitled "Meditations on the Holy Eucharist", which was granted the Nihil Obstat by Rt. Rev. Msgr. Pedro Lopez-Gallo, P.A. and the Imprimi Potest by Most Rev. Raymond Roussin, S.M., D.D., Roman Catholic Archbishop of Vancouver. The 2005 Revised Edition of the "Meditations on the Holy Eucharist" was sold in Canada, the United States and Europe. This book received "Honorable Mention" as a semi-finalist in the Religion Category at the 2005 Independent Publisher Book Awards held on June 3, 2005 in New York. There were 2,175 books submitted from 20 countries for this annual competition.

Aquinas Institute was incorporated under the name of Aquinas Institute Corporation in September 2005 and officially started its business operations as a corporation in January 2006. Msgr. Pedro Lopez-Gallo remains as the spiritual director of Aquinas Institute.

Interpretation & Translation Services

Aquinas Institute was established with a zero budget in a home based office in Richmond, B.C. With one regular staff, it has been generating annual gross income of approximately $150,000.00 for the past two years.

Aquinas Institute is the specialist in court-judicial-legal interpreting services. It has the largest scope of over 250 languages and dialects in which it can provide interpreting services. It also has a very large database of over 3,500 linguists. Clients, especially courts, have relied on Aquinas Institute to provide them the most competent interpreters to interpret in trials of high profile media cases heard in the BC Supreme Court or in BC Provincial Courts. Our ability to provide the most qualified interpreter on short notice is something that our clients appreciate the most. If you are looking for an interpreter in any rare language, chances are we have the interpreter for your language needs.

AQUINAS COURT-JUDICIAL-LEGAL INTERPRETER TRAINING

The Aquinas Court-judicial-Legal Interpreter training has gone a long way since it stated in 2006. It has become a trademark of the company. Referrals to the training come from the Society of Translators and Interpreters of British Columbia, the BC Courts and the graduates themselves. Its new graduates are accepted by the courts to interpret in trial cases upon completion of the training. Their quality interpreting services have been appreciated by the presiding judges, crown counsels, defense counsels, court staffs, witnesses, clients, the media and the gallery audience.

Testimonial:

Excerpt: "The book is remarkable for the multicultural perspective of its meditations on the Eucharist and for its open grouping of saints, bishops, priests, religious, and lay people as authors including young people and children. The articles themselves range from the theological (like the dogmatic and inspirational reflections one might hear from the pulpit on the Solemnity of Corpus Christi), to the personal (like the testimonials from the lay core of Eucharistic Devotion). To emphasize the personal and community immediacy of the Mass and Eucharistic Devotion, the editors wisely include photos of many of the authors, and of parish scenes, as of priests offering the Holy Sacrifice of the Mass and communicants receiving the Eucharist, and other photos of monstrances and Blessed Sacrament tabernacles. The articles and photos in combination form a portrait of the Catholic Church and of everyday religious observance, with the Eucharist at the centre, which brings me to the authors' purpose in writing this book. I imagine this purpose to be to inspire a sense of awe and trembling for the Eucharist again, a renewed devotion to the Blessed Sacrament based on spiritual conversion, a turning back to God, and on Eucharistic thinking, as when God is again the centre of human experience."
"Eucharistic Thinking" written by Paul Matthew St. Pierre in The B.C. Catholic newspaper, Nov. 1, 2004 issue, page 14.

Excerpt: "Bearing an appropriate cover design honouring the eucharist, this volume richly illustrated is a fitting tribute to the current Year of the Eucharist promulgated by John Paul II.........
With its wide coverage of contributions from prelates to pastors, pupils, parents, religious and teachers, the volume will have wide appeal and serve to deepen eucharistic understanding and devotion. Bishop Albert LeGatt of Saskatoon commends the volume as "very fruitful spiritual reading for all those wishing to make the eucharist the centre of their life and prayer."..........
The section on testimonials opens with a moving account by Rev. Lukose Cheruvalel, SDB, a Salesian missionary, of his eucharistic ministry to street kids at a centre called Snehalaya (Abode of Love), Guwahati City, Assam, India, where his close ministry partners are the Sisters of the Missionaries of Charity. Cheruvalel considers the eucharist to be the real symbol of the unity of Christ and the church, the "source and summit" of the whole Christian life. Cheruvalel is aware that "Both in the Eucharistic celebration and in my service to the poor, it is a question of the presence of Christ.......In my service of ..........restoring self-dignity to kids with low self-esteem, giving love to kids who have hardly received or given love, the power of the Eucharist to transform and heal becomes the solid foundation on which hope for the future is built up."
In her testimonial, Teresita Nixon attributes her father's recovery from a massive heart attack to the daily presence of the holy eucharist in his room which 'gave him a quick recovery, new energy and a new life."
With its inspiring content and multitude of local and international scenes, this commemorative volume should win wide public attention."
"Aquinas Institute honours current Year of the Eucharist" written by Rev. James W. Gray, O.S.B. in the Prairie Messenger newspaper, February 9, 2005, page 11.

"The Aquinas Institute of Vancouver has produced a very rich work in its publication "Meditations on the Holy Eucharist". It includes as foundational text the Holy Father's Encyclical "Ecclesia De Eucharistia" wherein Pope John Paul II sets out the rich mystery of the Eucharist as source and summit of the Church's faith and life. The Eucharist is indeed this mystery of faith that draws us constantly and ever deeper into the mystery of Christ himself in His paschal mystery, into His redemptive Death and Resurrection. The rich series of essays, sermons and reflections in this publication gathered from a wide variety of contributors helps unfold this mystery of the Eucharist. Calling us to a faith-filled celebration of the Eucharist and to reverential Eucharistic devotion and adoration, the articles in this book offer a very fruitful spiritual reading for all those wishing to make the Eucharist the center of their life and prayer."
Bishop Albert LeGatt of Saskatoon

"The Meditations on the Holy Eucharist" is a special book published by the Aquinas Institute in the Archdiocese of Vancouver, Canada. It is a beautiful collection of Eucharistic reflections and essays by bishops, theologians, priests, religious nuns, lay people and children. The Encyclical on the Holy Eucharist by Pope John Paul II and writings by Saint Josemaria Escriva are among the contents of the book. The articles are not only high inspiring and very informative but they are also well written in various languages. The book is a true richness of which we must take advantage of and disseminate as widely as possible. Absolutely recommended reading for devotees and those who are interested in knowing more about the Holy Eucharist - the Centre of our Catholic Faith."
P. Jose Abel Castillo Castillo, Secretary-General
