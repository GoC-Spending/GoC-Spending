Service Name:
Akita Equtak Drilling Ltd
Inuvik-based Akita Equtak Drilling Ltd. is the premier oil and
gas drilling contractor in the Western Arctic, providing
conventional petroleum drilling services as well as
directional, horizontal and under-balanced drilling.

Incorporated in 1983 by the Inuvialuit Development Corporation
and Akita Drilling Ltd., this dynamic company combines top
technical expertise and experience with an extensive wealth of
northern knowledge and community support. It's a powerful
combination-and it's the reason that senior oil and gas
producers in the North have come to rely on Akita Equtak for
all their drilling needs.

A Priority: Safety and Environmental Protection
Akita Equtak is committed to ensuring that its operations are
environmentally responsible and in compliance with all federal,
territorial and local environmental protection laws. Through
its environmental management programs, training and strict self-
monitoring, the company stays on top of issues and concerns
when they arise-and moves quickly to address them.

"Safety First" is Akita Equtaks #1 Priority. All operations are
geared to safe work practices.

Service Name:
NTCL
NTCL is a pan-Arctic marine operator, providing cost-effective,
reliable and comprehensive marine transportation and related
services throughout Northern Canada and the Arctic. It is a
principal transportation link for the movement of bulk
petroleum products and dry cargo to many communities, oil and
gas exploration sites and defense installations across the
North.
By northern Canadian standards, NTCL is a very long established
company. It has provided marine services throughout the
Mackenzie River Watershed since 1934 and along the Western
Arctic coast and islands since 1957. Arctic service was
extended to Alaska in 1963, when the Company was the first to
transport a drill rig by barge to Alaskas North Slope. On-
shore technical and fabrication services are available at
NTCLs shipyard facility.
In 1987, NTCL established a marshalling and packaging service
out of Montreal, which is operated through its subsidiary,
NorTran Inc. Eastern operations also include two supply
vessels serving the offshore resource industry off Canadas
east coast. And most recently, eastern operations were
expanded to include contract work within the Great Lake through
the Companys subsidiary, NT Husky.
NTCL expanded its freight services in late 2002 with a new
division, NT Global, which offers international freight
forwarding services and logistics management and consulting.
