Company Profile


Company History
Since 1971, the focus of the firm has remained the same:
consulting services for people and the environment. Our goal as
consultants is to contribute to the preservation and enhancement
of socially viable communities, improving corporate environmental
responsibility and supporting organizations to manage transition
and change. Our consulting services contribute to the quality of
life in the communities where we work by minimizing adverse
social and environmental impacts; planning for the delivery of
community and consulting services which are essential to healthy,
livable municipalities; improving corporate environmental
performance through consulting services; and assisting
organizations to develop management strategies which maximize
their effectiveness.

DPRA provides a range of consulting services in six key areas:
Consultation and Communication; Environmental and Community
Studies; Training; First Nation Studies; Management Studies; and
International Projects. DPRA staff have training and consulting
services experience in such diverse areas as:

- stakeholder consultation;
- environmental planning and assessment;
- socio-economic impact assessment;
- natural resources management;
- GIS and information systems;
- organizational analysis and management studies;
- strategic planning;
- public consulting services;
- program evaluation; and
- planning for the needs of special populations including
Aboriginals and older adults.

DPRA's Approach
We have the vision and expertise to take your project from the
initial concept through detailed development to approval and
implementation. DPRA's approach to all assignments involves
ongoing communication and careful collaboration with our clients.
We frequently deal with complex problems and have consistently
demonstrated our ability to produce creative, practical and
cost-effective solutions. Our ultimate objective is to assist our
clients with the implementation of their projects, and we are
confident in our ability to generate the consensus and commitment
necessary to succeed. We recognize the value which our clients
place on objective, expert advice and we are prepared to stand
behind our recommendations and assist you with obtaining the
approvals to proceed.

Our Clients
DPRA has been retained by governments, crown corporations, private
organizations and community groups. Our clients include over 20
federal and provincial departments in Canada and abroad; more
than 100 municipalities; over 50 First Nations communities; the
United Nations Development Program; the United States
Environmental Protection Agency; Ontario Hydro/Ontario Power
Generation; Atomic Energy of Canada Limited, and major companies
such as Canadian National Railway, Chrysler Canada, Harley
Davidson of Canada, A&P Food Stores, International Minerals and
Chemicals, Canadian Waste Services, Connaught Laboratories, Black
and MacDonald and Co-Steel LASCO.

Technology profile

DPRA has an
established reputation for excellence, innovation and
unparalleled client service.

The Environmental Information Systems (EIS) group was established
at DPRA to support on-going activities with the applications and
advantages of current information technologies. The goal of the
EIS group is to provide comprehensive services to the
environmental community that enhance their ability to understand,
utilise and apply the information they use in daily management,
analytical and operational activities.

Staff of the EIS group have over twelve years experience in the
management, administration, design and development of information
systems, related to environmental issues, concerns and
applications. Resources available include complete systems
life-cycle analysis and design, user needs assessments, database
design, development and implementation, the application and
training of Geographical Information Systems (GIS) technology.

Geographic Information Systems (GIS) and Information management
systems available through DPRA and affiliated firms include:
Arc/Info, Arc/View, MapInfo, MicroStation, ESRI Map Objects, ESRI
Internet Mapper; database software including MS Access and
Oracle; programming languages including C++, Visual Basic, HTML
and SQL; and dynamic web development applications and search
engines.
