Company Profile


CPCS is an international professional services firm that has been providing specialized infrastructure development advice to public and private sector clients since 1969. Specializing in mobilizing private investment in public infrastructure and services, we develop strategies and programs that ensure sustainable infrastructure service delivery and promote economic growth. We are currently the third largest company in Canada for consulting projects funded by International Financial Institutions - a significant portion of our work has been implemented in challenging international markets.

CPCS has been honored with the World Bank's Award of Excellence, the Canadian Award for International Cooperation, and a Canadian Award for International Development (Best Transport Services Project).

CPCS provides advisory services across a broad spectrum of infrastructure sectors, including: Ports, Rail, Urban (transit and property development), Power, Marine, Multimodal Logistics and Roads. Our team brings international expertise in the areas of transaction structuring, public-private partnerships (PPP), financial and economic modeling, legal and regulatory reform, operations, engineering and social and environmental advisory. This strong combination of in-house commercial and technical expertise is what makes CPCS different and has enabled us to successfully complete over 900 projects in 80 countries.

We are an employee-owned, independent company. We have no corporate affiliations that would compromise our ability to provide independent advice to our clients regarding downstream project work. We have established an active and successful transaction advisory practice that advises our clients on the value of professionalism and transparency and we have brought those principles fully on board in our own dealings with clients and partners alike.

North American Practice:
In 2006, the North American Practice was formally created as a division of CPCS and operates with its own dedicated team. This has allowed CPCS to better respond to the specific needs of clients in North America and to develop a depth of experience specific to this market. The Practice continues to draw on CPCSs Global experience and experts as needed.

In a short period of time, CPCS North American Practice has established itself as market leader. We continue to bolster our presence and team in this market in response to a growing volume of work. Since 2006, we have completed over 50 assignments for both public and private sector clients. Public sector clients include municipal, provincial and federal governments as well as agencies. Private sector clients have included port operators, Class 1 Railways and other logistics providers as well as private debt and equity investors.

Product / Service / Licensing

Service Name:
Public-Private Partnerships  Promoting Commercial Development
As the demand for infrastructure increases, governments are recognizing the important role that the private sector can play in construction, operations, maintenance and financing. The challenge is in structuring public-private partnerships that maximize investor interest while ensuring government priorities are met. We work closely with governments through all stages of the transaction process including project identification, due diligence, deal structuring and marketing, preparation of bid documents, management of the bid process, and negotiations. We have the experience and expertise to create the right financial conditions and arrangements necessary to attract top-quality investors and develop structures for project financing, through vehicles such as Build Operate Transfer (BOT) and Build Own Operate (BOO).

Service Name:
Planning, Economics and Modeling  Improving Operations and Services
Our unique blend of skills and expertise enables us to provide a comprehensive range of planning and development services for infrastructure projects and programs. We have prepared business plans to optimize the commercial potential of infrastructure projects around the world. We have also completed a wide range of feasibility studies and master plans in the transport sector. We use a variety of tools including financial analysis, costing, market studies, competition studies, asset valuation, and economic analysis to examine the potential and sustainability of infrastructure projects. We are well-suited to identify the full range of financial, economic and social costs and benefits that are needed to inform government decisions.

Service Name:
Institutional Reform  Strengthening the Public Sector
The success of our work in developing infrastructure and improving service often depends on the extent to which public institutions can implement and support new approaches. We assess the capacity of government institutions to perform key public sector roles, especially policy, strategy and regulation. We diagnose organizational and human resource strengths and weaknesses. We design new or strengthened institutional arrangements to fit the country context and recommend financial solutions to enable them to attract appropriate staff. We provide governance, management, organizational development, staffing, training and adjustment services to implement these plans, drawing on our global network of specialized and experienced consultants.



Service Name:
Legal and Regulatory Services  Developing Appropriate Frameworks
Working with governments and the private sector we make sure legislation, policy and regulatory structures are appropriate for the particular market and country where private sector participation in the transport sector is desirable. We ensure that legislative and policy frameworks provide the right incentives for private sector investments and that regulations balance both the investors and the consumers interest. We provide the full range of economic, legal, technical, safety and logistics expertise required to develop new regulatory regimes and governance structures. We also provide assistance in capacity building and training.

Service Name:
Investor Support  Informing Investment Decisions
Investors need a thorough understanding of the real opportunities and risks of potential infrastructure investments. We work with the private sector to carry out necessary feasibility studies, planning, and financial analysis to determine a projects full commercial potential. We assess the entire range of risk factors, whether they are market, technical or financial in nature, and advise on the appropriate bid strategy. We provide expert and independent reviews of market projections and business plans for investors and financial institutions. Having represented governments and investors in privatization transactions, we bring a wealth of insight from both sides of the table to each of our advisory mandates.

Service Name:
Environment and Social Safety Net  Recommending Sustainable Solutions
We develop strategies to provide essential infrastructure services to remote communities. We develop innovative strategies to involve local communities in the management and maintenance of infrastructure. Using participatory approaches we measure the impact of proposed developments on the poor and on the environment. We work with government and developers to maximize the beneficial impacts and minimize the harmful impacts of infrastructure projects.

Service Name:
Engineering Consulting - Development and Maintenance of Infrastructure
CPCS offers a wide range of engineering services for the development, rehabilitation and maintenance of infrastructure. We specialize in assessing infrastructure and developing rehabilitation and maintenance plans. CPCS proposes engineering solutions that are soundly supported by the finances and economics of the project. Our engineers work closely with our economists to ensure that infrastructure development is both technically sound and cost effective.
