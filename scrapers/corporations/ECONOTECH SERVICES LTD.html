Company Profile


Supporting the pulp and paper industry since 1972, Econotech helps with your toughest problems: from specialized chemical analysis of pulp (woods & non-woods), pulping liquors, and other process chemicals (smelt, ash, condensates, lime), to physical and optical testing of pulp or paper, species analysis, contamination identification (scale and plastics), and confidential pulping and bleaching studies.

Product / Service / Licensing

Service Name:
Microscopy
Pulp and Paper Microscopy: species identification-domestic,
foreign, exotic and non-wood; weighted average fiber length
(WAFL); fiber coarseness; sclereids per o.d. gram of pulp; fiber
curl factor and kink count; cooking uniformity; cell wall
thickness and fiber diameter in wood or pulp; deinked pulp-ink
particle count and area or sticky count and area; contaminant
identification; paper defects investigation.

Wood and Chip Testing: moisture content; length and thickness
classification; bulk and basic density; species breakdown; wood
brightness; percent bark and rot.

Air Sampling and Testing: total particulate; asbestos in bulk
samples, fiber counts, on-site sampling.

Service Name:
Pulp & Paper Testing (physical & optical)
Pulp Treatment: two PFI mills, two Valley beaters and a Domtar
latency removal mixer; flat screening; Pulmac shive analysis;
Bauer McNett fiber classification; Optest Fiber Quality
Analyzer-WAFL, coarseness, kink and curl indices; fiber length
index (DeMontigny); freeness measurement; handsheet preparation
(white water recirculation available).

Physical Testing: burst, tear, tensile, caliper, bulk, density,
fracture toughness, fold, internal bond, porosity (Sheffield or
Gurley), smoothness (Sheffield or Gurley), stiffness (Taber or
Gurley), softness (Gurley), coefficient of static friction,
concora, flat crush, ring crush, K & N ink absorption, water
absorption, Hercules size test, water retention value, water
vapor transmission rate, zero span and short span tensile,
stretch, TEA and MOE, Brecht Imset tear, Parker print surf,
Dennison wax pick, wet tensile testing, elongation, Cobb test.

Optical Testing: brightness and brightness reversion, opacity,
Hunter and CIE color coordinates, light scattering and absorption
coefficients, luminance factor, fluorescence, whiteness or
yellowness, tristimulus values, dirt count and identification,
ink particle, sticky counts and area determination, gloss.

Printability Testing: Vandercook proof press, MB Print
indicator, print density, mottleness, print through, IGT pick
test.

Fluff Properties Analysis: fluffing in hammermill, fiber damage
ratio, hardness index, nep or nodule count, resiliency and
absorption capacity and time, specific volume.

Other Services: permanence testing; sanitary product testing,
tissue towel and diaper testing; British Standard Sheet Machine
gridplate wire recovering; filler and additive evaluations.

Service Name:
Environmental Analysis
Effluent Monitoring: general water quality parameters including
BOD, COD, ammonia, total Kjeldahl nitrogen, phosphates,
alkalinity, acidity, pH, conductivity, turbidity, phenolic
substances, AOX, anions (chloride, sulfate, nitrate, nitrite,
fluoride, etc.), cations (sodium, calcium, magnesium, potassium,
etc.), cyanide, color, oil and grease, reactive silica and more;
trace level metals including chromium, lead, vanadium, iron,
zinc, manganese, titanium, and many others by ICP or AA; trace
level organics such as resin and fatty acids, PCBs, chlorinated
phenolics, formaldehyde, surfactants, methanol, chloroform,
volatile fatty acids (formic, acetic, buytric, etc.), chlorinated
acetic acids, polynuclear aromatic hydrocarbons, terpenes.

Soils, Sludges, Waste Materials: the majority of the above
effluent tests may also be applied to a wide variety of solid,
semi-solid and multiphased materials; other tests include:
1-hour aqueous extractions and 24-hour leaching per special waste
procedures, moisture, ash content, extractable organohalogen
(EOX), particle size analysis, infrared qualitative
identificaiton, and other tests; commercial solvent mixtures can
be quantified including mixtures of glycols and alcohols.

Air Samples, Industrial Hygiene: respirable quartz, organic
solvents, caustic mist, sulfuric acid mist, formaldehyde,
peroxide, total silica, oil mist, asbestos, total particulate in
air and others; mill particulate emissions including total dust
fall, anions or cations in dust fall or high volume filters.

Service Name:
Process Chemical Analysis
Roundwood, Chips & Hog Fuel: salt (chloride) content,
DCM-solvent extractives (pitch control), hot water extractives,
calorific value, ash content, carbohydrates, metals and elemental
analysis, potential turpentine or tall oil.

Smelt, Green & White Liquor: A-B-C titration, trace metals,
reduction efficiency, anions (hydroxide, carbonate, chloride,
sulfide, sulfate, sulfite and thiosulfate).

Black Liquor: carbohydrates, high heating value, organics
(sulfated ash), inorganic ratio, elemental CHNO, metals by ICP or
AA, total solids, boiling point rise, swelling, tall oil content,
UV lignin, viscosity profile and black liquor oxidation, anions
including: sulfur, chloride, sulfide, sulfite, sulfate and
thiosulfate.

Condensate: sulfide and methyl mercaptans, dimethyl sulfide and
dimethyl disulfide, methanol, Granatelli total reduced sulfur.

Pulp: carbohydrates (galactan, glucan, mannan, arabinan, xylan),
BOD, COD, aldehyde and carboxyl groups, pentosans, alpha, beta
and gamma cellulose, total (TOX, extractable (EOX) and adsorbable
(AOX) organic halogen, copper and kappa numbers, chloride,
DCM-solvent extractables (pitch), metals by ICP or AA, anions,
hexavalent chromium, viscosity, Klason lignin, total sulfur, pH,
extractable non-ionic surfactant.

Process Closure: full range of process and non-process elemental
analyses of all inputs and outputs for material balance and
corrosion studies.

Other Process Streams Analyzed: lime, limestone and reburned
lime; sulfite liquors; tall oil, spent acid; digester recovery
and other area scales.

Service Name:
Pulping
Examples of Studies and Applications: optimization of low kappa
pulping (EMCC, RDH) parameters necessary for production of ECF
and TCF pulps; evaluation of new fiber sources, including exotic
or non-wood fiber supplies; screening of pulping additives prior
to mill implementation; general pulping studies with different
chip blends to develop new fiber properties; layered cooking of
chips to determine economic benefits of chip thickness screening;
development of process parameters for mill modernization or
design of green field mills; evaluation of mill chip supplies for
pulp yield and strength; production of bleached and unbleached
pulp samples for sales staff; mechanical pulping simulations and
testing (TMP, CMP, CTMP).

Pulping Simulations and Other Capabilities: kraft batch and
continuous digester cooks, Kamyr EMCC, Kamyr MCC, Beloit RDH,
Bauer M&D cooks, kraft bomb digester cooks, soda, soda-AQ,
organosolv, ORGANOCELL, ALCELL, ASAM, acid sulfite, alkaline
sulfite, NSSC, green liquor cooks, high-yield soda ash cooks,
additives (AQ, polysulfide, surfactants), exotic or non-wood
fiber supplies or species, evaluation of mill chip supplies,
chipping, chip classification, bulk chip packing density, basic
wood density.

Service Name:
Bleaching
Examples of Studies and Applications: ECF and TCF bleaching
conditions for kraft and sulfite pulps; low-AOX and dioxin-free
bleaching sequences; peroxide and xylanase applications for
increasing ECF production capacity; recycling of office waste and
newsprint involving deinking and bleaching; dissolving pulp
bleaching, optimization and preparation of client samples;
bleaching options in modernizing and designing new bleach plants;
impact of bleach plant modification on costs, pulp strength and
effluent properties; bleaching of low-kappa EMCC and RDH pulps to
optimize strength and brightness; quantification of increased
bleaching costs caused by inter-stage carryover.

Bleaching Simulations Available: oxygen delignification, ozone,
hydrostatic oxygen (Eo), peroxide reinforcement (Eop), peroxide,
peracetic acid, performic acid, monopersulfuric acid, dimethyl
dioxirane (DMD); chlorinations (Cd, Dc or D100), chlorine
dioxide, hypochlorite, Monox-L, extractions, caustic
purifications; hydrosulfite, formamidine sulfinic acid (FAS),
borohydride; enzyme treatments, chelations.

Metals Analysis
Service Name:
Fiber Quality Analyzer
Service Name:

Technology profile

Econotech, founded in 1972, has
expertise in chip testing, pulping, bleaching, pulp and paper
testing, dissolving pulp evaluation, microscopy, environmental
analysis and testing of process samples and liquors.

Environmental Group areas of expertise include evaluating and
assisting in solving toxicity problems. We analyze effluents,
condensates, pulps, black liquors and wood samples; testing
encompasses trace level organics, general water quality
parameters, effluent monitoring and nutrient analysis. The
Environmental lab is accredited by the Canadian Association for
Environmental Analytical Laboratories (CAEAL) and the Standards
Council of Canada (SCC) which meets or exceeds ISO 25.

The Process Chemical Group combines a knowledge of process
chemistry with analytical skills to perform both routine
evaluations and specialized tests, from black liquor calorific
value to pulp carbohydrate constituents. We can analyze process
stream samples from pulping, bleaching, recausticizing, recovery
and paper machine areas of a mill. We follow TAPPI, CPPA, SCAN,
ASTM and other standard methods.
