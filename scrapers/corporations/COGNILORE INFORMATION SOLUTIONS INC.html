Company Profile


In an increasingly technology driven world, applications are
becoming more complex and the amount of information available
has grown dramatically. The management and development of an
organization's knowledge assets has become a key issue.
CogniLore excels at the development of electronic publishing,
content management, knowledge management, and document
management systems that allow our clients to make critical
knowledge available to their users. Whether for internal or
commercial purposes, CogniLore develops automated systems that
reduce the content publishing lifecycle and increase the
accuracy and availability of critical knowledge assets.

Using a variety of standards based approaches to content and our
advanced content publishing, searching, and access control
technologies, CogniLore can rapidly convert legacy systems from
any format to a controlled, intuitive Internet based content
collection completely under the owner's control. When your
needs change from the development of a collection of web pages
to a true online publishing system, CogniLore is your most
effective partner in getting the job done right the first time.

Electronic Publishing Services
CogniLore excels in the production of electronic titles using a
variety of technologies and standards to produce content for
delivery via CD/DVD-ROM, Internet, Intranet, and other
electronic media. CogniLore has produced titles for legal,

volume (500MB and greater) systems. Our unique access control
technology allows our clients to manage client access to content
down to the finest level of content granularity as determined by
the content owners.

Service Name:
Data Conversion Planning and Production
CogniLore works with our clients to plan and execute conversion
of electronic content from a legacy format (including Folio
VIEWS, older HTML, Interleaf, database tables, word processing
documents, SGML, and proprietary formats) to new formats
including XML, SGML, X-HTML, SCORM and PDF. CogniLore
specializes in the development of publishing systems for large
(500 MB and greater) scale data conversion.

Technology profile

The CogniLore JackKnife Electronic Publishing System allows your
organization to take control of its electronic assets by
defining client access to the finest levels of document
granularity and invoking powerful and rapid context based
searching capabilities. These features allow your users to
easily and accurately locate the precise document components
they need while giving content owners exacting control over the
type of content that can be viewed on a user by user basis.
CogniLore's JackKnife system consists of two powerful components
that work together to create extensive yet intuitive content
collections of any type, the Access Control System (ACS) and the
Simple/Easy XML Indexing System (SEXI). When your
organization's needs go beyond creating a collection of web
pages you have entered the domain of electronic publishing
systems. JackKnife is your solution for creating controlled,
secure, and intuitive electronic collections accessible through
cutting edge Internet technologies.

CogniLore Access Control System (ACS) - The core function of the
ACS system is to manage access to sub segments of a complete
document collection. The CogniLore Access Control system uses a
document's (or collection of document's) hierarchical
organization to selectively manage access to granular components
within this hierarchy. In this fashion, a content owner can
determine which sections of a document or a document collection
a client is able to view, and can easily restrict access to
components of a document at any level of granularity (section,
chapter, paragraph, sentence, etc.) based on the desired
restrictions and target users (i.e. to meet a certain security
level; to sell segments of content for commercial purposes; or
to customize a user's view of a collection of documents). A
user's profile contains his subscription information (subset)
which limits his view of the content collection, ensuring that
the user is not able to access these components, and, in fact,
can be completely unaware that these components even exist
within the collection. The ACS system features a "Table of
Contents" interface that allows the user to easily browse
through the hierarchy of a content collection select the
documents they wish to view. The system also features an
intuitive subscription capability that makes creating user
collections as easy as checking off the components to which that
user (or user group) should have access. Content displayed by
the ACS can be in whatever web format is most appropriate for
the content, including HTML, XML, scripts, Flash media, images,
and typically any other type of content that can be displayed in
a web browser. This powerful ability to control segments of
content based on user authentication lends itself to many and
diverse uses. The ACS system has already been used in very
broad fashion: from integration in e-learning systems that allow
users to create customized and personalized courseware to
segmenting non-confidential and secure information and only
allowing authenticated users to access the appropriate
information. Other applications of the technology allow users
to commercially purchase access to selective segments of content
or integration with a document management system that allows
users to access the documents for which they are responsible.
Other product features include:
Easily control access to content based on the structural rules
you define for your collections.
Easily create new user subscriptions by checking document
segments to which a user will have access.
The ability to personalize documents by creating notes that
attach to document segments. These notes remain static from
session to session and can be easily identified through a note
icon shown in your subscriptions Table of Contents.
Access tracking allows a user to easily identify components
which they have previously accessed.
Access history allows a user to easily see a list of the
previous 10 documents they have viewed.
Fully customizable interface allows the site manager to
determine how the content will be presented.
Easily supports any type of Internet based document.
Designed for multi language support.
Database driven easily allows for the integration of new
features based on client requirements.
The CogniLore Simple/Easy XML Indexing System (SEXI) - This
powerful XML search and retrieval system integrates with the ACS
to allow users to easily search for context specific content
with a XML document collection. Using CogniLore XML based query
language, users can query XML documents for the contents of any
indexed tag set, resulting in rapid and highly precise search
results. By creating easy to use and intuitive web based query
forms, users can rapidly identify the terms with which they wish
to construct their queries without the requirement to remember
complex Boolean search terms and constructs. The result is
rapid identification of the documents that are most pertinent to
their query. The SEXI system is ACS aware, ensure that the
results that are returned are only within the document subset
available to end user. However, the SEXI system can also also
be implemented independent of the ACS, allowing you to integrate
XML querying into any XML based system for rapid document search
and retrieval.

collection managed by our own JackKnife
Electronic Publishing Technologies.

CompliSource - CogniLore produced 8 CD based
titles and created and hosted an Internet version
of these titles for CompliSource's US Regulatrory
Compliance titles. These titles were sold and
accessed by over 80 Fortune 500 clients from
across the US. Each title, hosted on CogniLore's
servers, contained hundreds of thousands of
Financial Regulations and opinions on Mortgage,
Auto, and Private loans accessed by the legal
departments of such clients as IBM, Toyota, Chase
Bank, Bank One, BMW, and GMAC.
