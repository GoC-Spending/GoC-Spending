Company Profile


MPB Technologies was started in 1977 by Dr. Morrel Bachynski and seven other employees when the Research and Development arm of RCA Canada closed its doors.

The Company grew and eventually diversified to include works in Communications, Space, Fusion Technology, Electromagnetics, the Forest Industry, Robotics, and Lasers. The company won numerous awards along the way including the Financial Post Canada Enterprise Award for Best Start-up Business Plan, three Canada Awards for Business Excellence, and the Canada Export Award.

The company has an international reach with a global customer base of blue chip companies in the industrial, research, defense and telecommunications sectors.

Headquartered in Montreal, Quebec, MPB Technologies is the parent company of MPB Communications Inc., and The Electronics Test Centres in Kanata and Airdrie.

MPB Communications is a leading supplier of innovative, high performance fiber laser and fiber amplifier subsystems to the international high tech industry. For the telecommunications industry, MPBC provides state-of-the-art subsystems to extend the reach of optical fiber spans. For the laser industry, MPBC is the innovator of visible fiber lasers for biomedical and industrial applications.
See MPBCommunications.com

The MPB Space Photonics group, also part of MPB Communications, has been developing innovative materials for terrestrial and space applications, pressure/stress/temperature sensors to gauge environmental impact, imaging spectrometers, and a reconfigurable all-terrain multi-mission microRover.
See MPB-Space.com

The Electronics Test Centres in Kanata and Airdrie have been Canadian leaders in compliance testing since 1988.
See ETC-MPB.com

Service Name:
Electronic Test Center
The Electronics Test Centres are located in Kanata, Ontario and Airdrie, Alberta.

Located just outside Ottawa, the ETC Kanata Laboratory boasts
- 5 Anechoic Chambers,
- a High Voltage Lab,
- HIRF Test Facilities, and
- an Open Air Test Site for Compliance and Custom testing
and have
- NARTE-Certified test engineers,
- and Controlled Goods Registation

The Electronics Test Centre is multi-accredited and has extensive experience in MIL-STD, RTCA, SAE, DEF-STAN and commercial testing for CE marking of MILCOTS equipment.


The Airdrie Electronics Test Centre has been operating since 1966 as a one-stop compliance test lab.
Committed to quality assurance and customer service, ETC provides trustworthy testing services to the electronics, telecommunications, wireless, instrumentation, sensing devices, medical, military, aeronautical and automotive industries.
Experienced technologists and specialists at home in a world-class laboratory provide consulting, testing, engineering evaluation, and documentation services for product introduction to North American, European and Global Markets.

Success stories:

Designing, developing and manufacturing the
world's first undersea branching multiplexors put
into operation March 2, 1992 in the optical fibre
Trans Atlantic Telecommunications System (TAT-9).
Designing and developing the control system,
feedback system, data acquisition, various
diagnostic systems and a high mW r-f power
launching system for Canada's only major facility
for research in fusion energy located at the
Centre canadien de fusion magnetique in Varennes,
Quebec.
Leading a consortium of Quebec organizations in a
five year Telerobotics Development Systems (TDS)
Program which developed three complete advanced
prototype telerobotic systems, a macro-robot for
live hydro electric line maintenance, a micro
robot for micro fabrication, and a research
robot.
Developing innovative, high perfomance lasers
including ultra stable, long-life sealed CO2
lasers for demanding scientific applications, the
world's first commercially available Er3+ fibre
lasers, a family of excimer lasers, including the
world's smallest excimer, and unique tunable
solid-state laser systems.
Designing and developing special space payloads
for microgravity experiments. Payloads have been
developed for space station, space shuttle,
suborbital rockets and KC-135 aircraft missions.
Designing and developing microwave/MMwave
radiometers for remote sensing and upper
atmosphere research, rain and cloud radars and
advance next generation SAR.
