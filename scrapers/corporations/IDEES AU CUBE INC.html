Company Profile


Idées au cube is one of the largest audio-visual production and corporate events firms in Quebec, a specialist in graphic and multimedia production for museums, companies, public and para-public agencies, and world-class associations.

Idées au cube designs and produces turnkey solutions. This means assuming end-to-end responsibility for the production, from initial concept to final delivery. As a company duly registered in the Canadian governments repertoire of video providers, Idées au cube would like to take this opportunity to inform you of its wide range of services. Our firm has successfully carried out mandates in the areas of video, events and multimedia for various federal departments (Justice, Defence, Canadian Heritage, Environment...). We believe we have the ability to meet the highest requirements in terms of both quality and efficiency.

With more than 35 years of active experience in the field of communications, Idées au cube has produced an impressive array of videos of all types, as well as a host of multimedia communication tools, many of which have garnered industry awards.

For more information, please visit www.id3.com.

Product / Service / Licensing

Product Name:
Design the Future (immersive multi-sensory show)
For the Biosphere, Environment Museum (Montréal)

Idées au cube's mandate was to create an immersive, multi-sensory experience that reaches the mind, body and soul. Everything was designed to allow the visitor to reconnect with nature and discover how it offers innovative solutions to tackle today's environmental challenges.

As the show starts, the visitor stands at the center of an imposing ring, inclined at a 5° angle, which surrounds a central pillar reminiscent of a nucleus. Projections on the ring, pillar and floor are enhanced by a captivating soundtrack and various testimonies on the relationship of people with nature. Atmospheric effects (wind, snow, mist) add a sensory layer to the experience.

Optimum use of space and the technological requirements of the project were both daunting challenges. In addition to coordinating 22 projectors, LED lighting effects, sensory effects and 24-channel sound spatialization, Idées au cube and its collaborators developed a custom-made 360° HD video capture system.

NUMIX Prize 2016 (Cultural Production/Museum Production)


Product Name:
The Val-Jalbert Epic (immersive multi-sensory show, interactive models and exhibition space)
For the Village historique de Val-Jalbert (Saguenay-Lac-Saint-Jean)

As part of a total revamping of the pulp mill site in Val-Jalbert, the information system designed by Idées au cube consists of three main components.

An exhibition room, serving as a vestibule, sets the tone and reminds the visitor he is in a place of work, a place of industrial production at the heart of a company village.

Interactive models tackle all of the educational content related to the site and pulp mill operations in a playful, yet informative way.

An immersive show focuses on the human aspects through short vignettes, like so many era snippets. The viewpoint of the different characters weaves a meaningful collective story.

The trail forms a cohesive ensemble that stimulates reflection, fosters learning and sparks wonder.

Grand Prix du tourisme québécois 2014 (Tourist Attractions, 25 000 to 100 000 visitors)
Télé-Québec Audiovisual and Multimedia Award 2014 (Société des musées québécois)


Product Name:
Northern Lights (sound and light show)
For Canadian Heritage (Ottawa)

Projected on the Parliament's facade in Ottawa, Northern Lights is a 32-minute show complemented by an interactive cube offering 55 minutes of entertainment.

Northern Lights takes us on a voyage to discover the history of Canada. The starting point is the 1916 fire that razed Parliament, sparing only its impressive library, memory of our country. From there, the show goes on to cover the key characters and watershed moments that shaped the evolution of Canada. Projected on a 470-foot wide surface that reaches a height of 30 floors at the center, the spectacular images are accompanied by a hypnotic soundtrack, light effects and lasers.

In the summer of 2015, this wondrous adventure supported by a large-scale technical display attracted an average of 5000 people a night, for a total of 268 000 spectators!

Grand Pinnacle Gold 2015 (Best Event Video - over $1,5M)
NUMIX 2016 Finalist (Cultural Production/Immersive Production)
