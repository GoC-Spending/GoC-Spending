Company Profile


CVMR Corporation is the holding company for a number of CVMR® companies.

The company was founded in 2006, although some of its subsidiaries and affiliates date back to 1986. It is engaged in mining, metal refining and development of a unique series of vapour metallurgy processes and technologies, including: CVMR® Carbonyl Technology (used in refining of nickel, iron and cobalt), CVMR® Iodide Technology (used for refining ultra pure titanium and vanadium), CVMR® Chlorination Technology (used in refining of Coltan, rare Earth Elements, titanium and vanadium) and CVMR Phosphorous Tri Fluoride Technology (PGE refining). It produces Nickel, Cobalt, Iron and other metals and alloy powders in various sizes and morphologies, including nano-powders. CVMR®s Nano-Metal-Powders are used extensively by various manufacturers in North America, Europe and the Far East.

CVMR® has extensive investments in fully-integrated mineral assets, worldwide. Its refining processes are unique to the Company, environmentally neutral and quite cost effective. These technologies are mostly developed in house, at CVMR®s laboratories and pilot plants. The Centre for Research and Development of CVMR® Corporation and its Head Office are in Toronto, Ontario, Canada.

The company, as whole, spends about 10% of its annual income on R&D --roughly about $37 mil.-- in 2012. It holds numerous patents for refining of Nickel, Cobalt, Molybdenum, Iron, Chromium, Tungsten, Zirconium, Titanium, Niobium and Tantalum. CVMR®s unique technologies can be used to concentrate Platinum Group of Metals (PGM), Gold, copper and Rare Earth Elements (REE) by first recovering the base metals such as iron and nickel and to produce highly purified elements in various forms.

The company is privately held and does not amalgamate its income from various countries. As a private company CVMR is not obliged to divulge its exact annual income. CVMR is a 20% shareholder in Jilin Horoc holding company in the province of Jilin, People's Republic of China. As of 2012 its shares in Jilin Horoc were estimated at be worth about U.S. $650 mil.

Technology profile

Vapour metal refining processes (e.g. the "carbonyl process" for refining base metals, such as Nickel) can be applied to many metals in the transition group (Nickel, Cobalt, Iron, Manganese, Tungsten, Molybdenum, Chromium, etc.) as well as Beryllium and metals in the platinum group (Platinum, Palladium, Osmium, Irridium, Ruthenium, Rhenium and Rhodium)

The CVMR process delivers very pure metal and significantly lower operating cost than traditional processes. The CVMR process can produce pellets, net shapes, coatings, powders and foams, according to market needs, by simply changing the process decomposition parameters - without altering the separation/extraction/refining steps.
