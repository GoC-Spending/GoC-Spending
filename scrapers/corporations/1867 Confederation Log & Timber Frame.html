Product Name:
BUILDINGS, LOG

1867 Confederation Log & Timber Frame manufactures a broad variety of custom log and timber frame residential and commercial structures from their manufacturing facility, and head office located in Bobcaygeon, Ontario, Canada.

Our homes are custom designed by our design and drafting department to suite each customers specific requirements. Each detail is approved before the home is manufactured. Our extensive product research and state-of-the-art technology ensures that our customers experience an airtight, energy efficient, and low maintenance log structure at an affordable price. In September 2008 an 1867 Confederation Log & Timber Frame became the first log home in Canada to receive an Energy Star rating, and has been awarded the Log Home Guide Seal of Approval, a distinguished claim for excellence in quality and design.

An 1867 Confederation Log & Timber Frame home is crafted from either Northern White Pine, or Western Red Cedar, both species combining durability with rustic appeal. The squared log timbers used in 1867 Confederation Log & Timber Frame homes are dry seasoned measuring 6x12, and 8x12.

Technology profile

1867 Confederation Log & Timber Frame is recognized as a leader in the log home industry. Superior customer service, quality craftsmanship, and thirty-one years in the business make them the manufacturer of choice for discriminating purchasers.

Log homes manufactured by 1867 Confederation Log & Timber Frame homes come with a fifty year materials warranty, and proven construction techniques that maximize the energy efficiency of the homes (and reduce the amount of movement which is natural to logs). White Pine, from northern Ontario, or Western Red Cedar is used to manufacture the homes.

Customers can choose one of the companys award winning designs, or develop their own floor plans with the help of the in house design department.

1867 Confederation Log & Timber Frame homes are experienced at exporting homes all over the world. They have homes in Japan, South Korea, Austria, Switzerland, Germany, Spain, Italy, Venezuela, South Korean, and Japan.

The company has dealers all across Canada and the United States, who receive thorough training in Bobcaygeon when they join the dealer network, and are perpetually updated as techniques, and components are changed and developed. In addition to a North American dealer network, 1867 Confederation Log & Timber Frame also has dealers in Turkey, Greece, Italy, and Japan.

In 2009 1867 Confederation Log & Timber Frame celebrated its thirtyth year in the log home industry. The company has marked the occasion by erecting a new model home showcasing many different architectural and structural features that are now available.

The superior quality and craftsmanship of 1867 Confederation Log & Timber Frame has earned it the Log Home Guides Seal of Approval, the homes are also recognized and approved by CMHC, and most importantly our customers are backed by unparalleled customer service.

1867 Confederation Log & Timber Frames standard kit is a closed in shell, that means your structure will be secure when the package is erected. The log portion of each home manufactured by 1867 Confederation Log & Timber Frame is assembled in the factory prior to being shipped. This is to ensure the accuracy of the extremely tight tolerances of the logs, and pre-drilled holes that accommodate such things as electrical wiring, hardwood dowels around door and windows, or thru-bolts. The logs are numbered so that they can be easily be erected on site as they are unloaded from the truck. Erection of the logs does not require a crane.

Key / Major clients:

1867 Confederation Log & Timber Frame has been providing homes, cottages, and commercial buildings to our customers since 1979. We pride ourselves on the quality of our product, and our customer service.

Success stories:

1867 Confederation Log & Timber Frame has provided structures for various commercial and retail customers. From a ski resort (including chalets and main lodge) in Japan, to daycare centres in Canada and the United Kingdom, to picnic shelters and gazebos along the Trent Severn Waterway in Ontario, Canada.

International capital projects:

1867 Confederation Log & Timber Frame provided chalets, and central buildings for the Cupid Valley Ski Resort in Japan. Our homes have been shipped to the U.S.A., Argentina, Japan, Austria, Germany, France, United Kingdom to name a few countries. A craftsman from 1867 Confederation Log & Timber Frame is available to instruct and supervise a crew of local builders in the erection of a building overseas.
