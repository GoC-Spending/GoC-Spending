Product / Service / Licensing

Service Name:
Soccer Specific Training
We offer the following Programs:
Ball Mastery
Focus on improving the four key technical aspects of soccer: Receiving, Retaining the ball, Running with the ball and Releasing the ball.

Speed Strength and Conditioning
We will assess individual movement mechanics both with and without the ball, in order to identify specific abilities requiring improvements. We integrate speed, agility, quickness, balance, strength, rhythm and co-ordination in an innovative and fun manner.

Futsal
Technical / Tactical training specific to the game of futsal

Brazilian Sand Agility
We will integrate speed, agility, quickness, balance, strength, rhythm and co-ordination in an innovative and fun manner with and without the ball.

Brazilian Sand Skillz
Focus on improving the four key technical aspects of soccer while training in the sand: Receiving, Retaining the ball, Running with the ball and Releasing the ball.

1v1 & 2v2
Players are taught and coached in offensive and defensive 1v1, 2v1 and 2v2 game situations. Offensively they will learn to use combination play and deception to beat their opponent. In defense players will learn the foundations of good individual and second man defending.

Team Training
Programs will be tailored to the specific teams requirements after consulting with the coach of the team.

Pre Season Conditioning
We will assess individual movement mechanics both with and without the ball, in order to identify specific abilities requiring improvements. We integrate speed, agility, quickness, balance, strength, rhythm and co-ordination in an innovative and fun manner.

Plus 20 Elite
Players who are selected to participate in the +20 program will be challenged to unleash their ultimate athletic potential through technical, tactical and physical training. Players will be exposed and introduced to a number of new training methods that will expand their knowledge of the principles of attacking and defending soccer.Players will be asked to maintain a very high standard on and off the training field and be provided with a very in depth Home Strength, Balance and Technique Program which will ensure the players are staying sharp and fit throughout the program.

Plus 20 Elite Goalkeeper
Goalkeepers who are selected to participate in the +20 program will be challenged to unleash their ultimate athletic potential through goalkeeper specific technical and physical training.

March Break Camp
During our fun five day training camp, players will be exposed and introduced to a number of new training methods that will expand their knowledge of the principles of play that are applied in attacking and defending soccer as well as learning and refining individual techniques.

University Prep Camp
A detailed look into what is required of players to play at the university level in the United States. Players will be exposed to various tactical games emphasising both attacking and defending principles of play.

Summer Camp
During our fun five day training camp, players will be exposed and introduced to a number of new training methods that will expand their knowledge of the principles of play that are applied in attacking and defending soccer as well as learning and refining individual techniques.

Personal Training
Player's looking for individual training can send an email to sanjeev@parmarsportstraining.com to organize training sessions specific to the topic of their choice. All individual training will be conducted by Kevin Nelson or Sanjeev Parmar

Sector information

Unique applications:

In 2010 Sanjeev was the recipient of the prestigious Ottawa Business Journal Forty Under 40 award. Sanjeev received his CSA A License in the summer of 2007 and became one of the youngest coaches in the country to have achieved the highest coaching level for soccer in Canada. Sanjeev has been an Ontario Soccer Association Staff Coach / Course Conductor since 2003. In October 2010, Sanjeev was selected by the Ontario Soccer Association to participate in the first ever FIFA Futsal Course for Coaches. He is also certified with his NCCP Level II. Sanjeev has added to his academic credentials by completing his Masters Degree in Human Kinetics at the University of Ottawa in December 2004. Presently, Sanjeev is a part time professor at the University of Ottawa in the Human Kinetics department and a technical consultant for the Louis Riel Sports Etude Program where he is responsible for the technical development of the soccer players.

Key / Major clients:

amateur players, professional players, amateur teams and professional teams

Success stories:

ERIN MCLEOD, Canadian National Team & Washington Freedom, Womens Pro Soccer League
November 29, 2009: My name is Erin McLeod and I am a keeper for the Canadian National Team as well as the Women's Professional soccer team, Washington Freedom. I had the privilege of working with Sanjeev's +20 program born in 1996/97 for a number of weeks in Ottawa. I can honestly say that I have never seen so much talent for such a young group of girls. Their technical ability alone is better than any girls I have seen at that age anywhere in the USA and Canada. Sanjeev's focus to technical detail along with Kristina Kiss's focus on tactics allows for an incredible learning environment. This group had homework assignments every week, two girls per session were in charge of the team warm up, they shake all the coaches hands before and after practice, they have prepared resumes for possible universities; this is a team of professionals determined to be better people and players. Sanjeev and all of his coaching staff expect the most out of their players every session and this passionate group consistently delivers. I was able to run a few warm up drills for the entire group as well as work with the keepers. I noticed immediately that regardless of who is running the drills this group is eager to learn and listen, a group of girls like this is why people coach in the first place. They train like it is their career and I expect for most of them one day it will be.

ELLIOT SIMMONS, Ottawa Fury Academy, U11
December 10, 2008: Elliot has supplemented his regular club training with Parmar Sports for the past three years. He has just spent a succesful period of time training with Arsenal FC U11 boys in the UK and has recently received additional training/trial offers from West ham Utd and Chelsea FC . Having spoken to scouts/coaches from these three clubs, it is very apparant that the most important attributes they are looking for in young players between 8-12 is natural ability and mastery of the ball, ie First touch and overall technical ability etc. Other advanced tactical aspects of the game as it gets faster through the ages cannot be achieved unless these fundementals are in place first . Sanjeev's huge influence on Elliot over the years cannot be underestimated and his attention to technical detail has really made a difference, and played a significant role in Elliot being identified for these trial opportunities. If you take your game seriously, Put your trust in Sanjeev, I don't believe there is a better technical coach in the Ottawa area. We look forward to many more years of your programs.

NESS TIMMONS, Canadian National Team Coach for World University Games 2003 and University of Cape Breton Women's Soccer, Nova Scotia
October 12, 2003: "Sanjeev makes the speed, agility, and quickness sessions sport specific. He has a way of taking the athletes to that next level. That explosive power is what we want in all our players and Sanjeev has the knack to bring it out."

International capital projects:

Parmar Sports Training Inc. has developed a great relationship with Youth Football Club Rurka Kalan in Punjab, India and Birtil Sinclair Soccer Academy in Trinidad and Tobago since 2008.

Sanjeev travels to India at least once a year to work with YFC and was involved in a recent project in September 2010. He helped develop an instructional DVD in Punjabi which covered 25 session plans for coaches coaching 12 - 15 year old players.

In association with the Australian Sports Commission and the Sports Department of Punjab, India, Gurmangal Das, president of Youth Football Club Rurka Kalan invited Sanjeev Parmar to Punjab, India to run a six day Grassroots Coaches Training Program. The program was organized from December 15 - 20 at the YFC academy in Rurkakalan, India. The program attracted 25 coaches from around various villages and cities in the state of Punjab. Coaches came from with different personal coaching backgrounds with the intention of learning and growing as coaches.

We are continuously collecting soccer cleats and would love to collect them from you. The equipment will be dispersed to under priviliged children participating in the YFC satellite projects in the neighboring communities.
