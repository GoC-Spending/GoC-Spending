Company Profile


Innovative Steam Technologies (IST) engineers and manufactures Once-Through heat recovery Steam Generators (OTSGs) / 'boilers' to recover the heat from the gas turbine exhaust stream. IST also designs and manufactures Fired Once Through Steam Generators for Enhanced Oil Recovery applications.

ISTs Combined Cycle/ Cogeneration and Gas Turbine Injection Applications
In general OTSGs can be used by most electricity power plants and steam generating centers, various industrial complexes (semiconductor fabs, pulp & paper mills, textile manufacturers, glass manufacturers, etc.), mining corporations, large centers
requiring stable and flexible electricity (hotels, office buildings, hospitals, universities, sports complexes, factories, etc.), and even organizations that require large amounts of steam such as desalination plants.

IST is the only proven manufacturer of once through heat recovery steam generators (OTSG) in the world. IST has numerous advantages over conventional drum-type heat recovery steam generators including the following:

* Simple operation - steam drums and complex control systems are eliminated making the OTSG capable of remote attended or unmanned operation.
* Simple and economical site installation that minimizes site costs and associated project risks.
* Capable of dry operation, therefore a bypass stack is not required and the steam system can operate independent of the gas turbine.
* More efficient due to elimination of a bypass stack and associated leakage as well as blowdown losses.
* Chemical injection to the steam drum is not required, therefore this system is eliminated.
* Due to minimal instrumentation and drainability, a building enclosure is not required in severe climates. This eliminates a costly enclosure from the project.
* Lowest installed and life cycle costs.

Installed & life-cycle benefits of our OTSG boiler make it much more efficient & less expensive than drum-type boilers.

The above characteristics make the OTSG ideal for the following applications:
* Heat recovery in combined cycle and cogeneration plants.
* Heat recovery utilizing the waste heat from natural gas pipeline compressor drives.
* Heat recovery for steam injection for gas turbine NOx control, power augmentation and cooling purposes.
* Heat recovery & steam for pulp and paper mills, textile mills, manufacturing complexes, etc.
* Heat recovery for barge and marine based combined cycle plants

IST currently has 200+ OTSGs spread across six continents. Our technology has been examined and accepted by many customers including global players such as the following:
* GE Power Systems, USA
* NEL Power, UK
* Calpine, USA

* Alstom (ABB Power Generation), Puerto Rico

Our size range currently handles gas turbines from 5 to 75 MW, plus LMS100 for combined cycle and cogeneration applications and the entire gas turbine range for steam injection/cooling applications

ISTs Fired OTSGs for Enhanced Oil Recovery Applications
In addition to ISTs traditional products IST is now designing, manufacturing and installing OTSGs for key oil producing markets such as Alberta, California, Oman, Venezuela and Texas.

We produce different sizes of units with varying levels of thermal capacity.

Technology profile

IST designs and manufactures Once-Through Steam Generators (OTSGs), for the power generation sector and for Enhance Oil Recovery.

Power Generation Sector
The OTSGs are simple, continuous flow boilers that convert feedwater into high purity steam. The OTSGs are best suited technically and economically for combined cycle power
generation, cogeneration and gas turbine power augmentation applications.

The OTSG has many notable features mainly, full dry running capabilities, which eliminates the need for a bypass stack and damper. The OTSG requires significantly fewer parts and the installation is accomplished faster and at a much lower cost than the typical drum-type HRSGs, due to the relatively higher amount of factory assembly and testing that is completed prior to shipment. The system has a once through flow path; therefore no steam drums or blowdown systems are required. The OTSG demonstrates a significant improvement over the natural circulation drum-type units. It offers high availability, proven experience, and cost savings benefits.

Enhanced Oil Recovery
Fired Once Through Steam Generator
- Generate saturated steam with quality 300 MMBtu/hr
- Boiler designed to ASME Section I and American Petroleum Institute (API) specifications
- Piping designed to ASME B31.1
- Operating pressures up to 2500psi

Radiant Module
- Cylinder diameter from 30 ft
- Cylinder length about 3x diameter
- Single-circuit or multi-circuit design
- Multi-circuits can be either parallel-pass or circuit-by-sector design
Internally insulated with ceramic fibre blanket, modules and refractory. Casing is carbon steel.

Convective Module
- Consists of a serpentine finned tube module
- Tubesheets at the ends are carbon steel with refractory installed on the hot side
- Intermediate tubesheets may be necessary for larger convective sections
- Single circuit or multi-circuit design
- Multi-circuits can be either parallel-pass or circuit-by-sector design

Fuel
- Natural gas, oils, process and bi-product fuels are suitable

Unique applications:

Gas Turbine Injection (GTI)
This process consists of injecting steam into the head end of the combustor of gas turbines, reducing unabated NOx emissions; and into the compressor discharge, increasing mass flow and power output.

GTI delivers one of the lowest cost per kW* for incremental power, especially when considering the higher capital and installation cost of building a larger plant.

Gas Turbine Injection has been proven to be a highly reliable method of improving gas turbine performance.

Delta Hudson  Strathmore, Alberta, Canada 
Encana Power,
Cavalier Power Station

Project Description
The Cavalier Power Station was built in 2001to provide power for the Calgary area. The 228 MW combined cycle plant is optimized to
achieve a schedule of daily start-stops, without requiring additional capital investment or loss of operational efficiency.

IST was contracted to supply four (4) OTSGs for the Cavalier Power Station. The four (4) OTSGs were built with HP and IP steam circuits, and also included SCR and CO systems for emissions control. The plant also features four (4) x 43 MW LM6000 gas turbines and two (2) x 28 MW steam turbines.

The customer selected OTSGs because they are ideally suited for cycling applications due to the elimination of drums and other thick walled pressure parts. The thin walled tube design and high-grade tube material permits regular start-stop operation without the thermal stress concerns usually associated with drum-type HRSGs.
OTSGs are capable of extremely fast startups and are typically able to
supply full steam loads within 60 minutes. Since OTSGs start from a dry condition, there is no requirement to slowly heat the water contained within drums. The fast startup of OTSGs allow both gas turbines to reach full load and SCRs to achieve their minimum operating temperature much quicker than traditional drum HRSGs.

Project Description
The Pulrose Power Station was constructed to provide the Isle of Man with its own source of economical power allowing the Island to be less dependent on diesel engines and cable power supplied from England. In addition to efficiency, the size and visual appearance of the plant was critical due to the location within a populated tourist area and limited space availability to house the generating equipment. The steam generation equipment was selected to provide the plant

ISTs two (2) OTSGs were built with HP and LP steam circuits and they also included exhaust ducts which combined with the three (3) diesel engine exhausts streams to form one main plant stack.

GTI delivers one of the lowest cost per kW* for incremental power, especially when considering the higher capital and installation cost of building a larger plant.

Gas Turbine Injection has been proven to be a highly reliable method of improving gas turbine performance.

Success stories:

Delta Hudson  Strathmore, Alberta, Canada 
Encana Power,
Cavalier Power Station

Project Description
The Cavalier Power Station was built in 2001to provide power for the Calgary area. The 228 MW combined cycle plant is optimized to
achieve a schedule of daily start-stops, without requiring additional capital investment or loss of operational efficiency.

IST was contracted to supply four (4) OTSGs for the Cavalier Power Station. The four (4) OTSGs were built with HP and IP steam circuits, and also included SCR and CO systems for emissions control. The plant also features four (4) x 43 MW LM6000 gas turbines and two (2) x 28 MW steam turbines.

The customer selected OTSGs because they are ideally suited for cycling applications due to the elimination of drums and other thick walled pressure parts. The thin walled tube design and high-grade tube material permits regular start-stop operation without the thermal stress concerns usually associated with drum-type HRSGs.
OTSGs are capable of extremely fast startups and are typically able to
supply full steam loads within 60 minutes. Since OTSGs start from a dry condition, there is no requirement to slowly heat the water contained within drums. The fast startup of OTSGs allow both gas turbines to reach full load and SCRs to achieve their minimum operating temperature much quicker than traditional drum HRSGs.

NEL Power  Isle of Man, UK
Manx Electricity Authority,
Pulrose Power Station

Project Description
The Pulrose Power Station was constructed to provide the Isle of Man with its own source of economical power allowing the Island to be less dependent on diesel engines and cable power supplied from England. In addition to efficiency, the size and visual appearance of the plant was critical due to the location within a populated tourist area and limited space availability to house the generating equipment. Because of the tight schedule for the combined cycle plant, the two (2) OTSGs, gas turbines and steam turbines were installed after the construction of the building. The plant (2x2x1) has a full dry run capability, eliminating the requirement for a bypass stack and diverter valve.

The steam generation equipment was selected to provide the plant
with safe and reliable operation for 365 days a year, fast startups, small footprint, and a simple cycle dry run capability at the most cost competitive price.

ISTs two (2) OTSGs were built with HP and LP steam circuits and they also included exhaust ducts which combined with the three (3) diesel engine exhausts streams to form one main plant stack.
