Company Profile


Flint Original Hospital Felt had its origin in 1989 when David
Flint, the owner of Flint Saddlery, was asked by concerned
professional equestrian people (English and Western disciplines)
whose horses are used many hours daily to find a solution to
such problems as scalding, fibrosis and ulceration resulting in
the horses being unusable for days. David experimented with
many different pad materials currently on the market but none
solved the problem. One day while visiting a friend in the
hospital who had been bedridden for six months, David remarked
that he must have bedsores. The friend explained that there
were no bedsores because of the special hospital felt pad that
was under him. The mill that produced the pad was contacted,
modifications to the milling process were discussed and the
first Flint Original Hospital Felt pad with its many
therapeutic properties was produced. In fact, Flint Saddlery,
has an exclusive agreement with the mill for this felt product
which is grey in colour. The grey colour makes it easy to
distinguish the Flint Original Hospital Felt from other common
polyester orthopaedic or sports hospital felts.

Flint Original Hospital Felt is not the polyester orthopaedic
or sports hospital felts that are found in most tack stores and
most commonly used in sports and equestrian equipment. Flint
Original Hospital Felt contains no coatings or finishes to
prevent air circulation and dissipation of heat and moisture
from the horse's body. The fibre loops mesh with the horse's
coat to prevent slippage under the saddle. Due to the high
content of special fibre, not found in sports felt, Flint
Original Hospital Felt offers excellent protective and
therapeutic qualities.

The Flint Original Hospital Felt pad has excellent shock
absorption value and the main advantage is its ability to absorb
and wick both moisture and heat away from the horse's body. The
short fibres work with the horse's coat resulting in much less
slippage. Bacteria won't grow in the pad. You will find that
your horse is much drier under the pad after a ride. These
qualities are retained after extended use and enhanced by
frequent washing.

Flint Saddlery has also developed Two-Piece Western/Ropers
cinches and Two-Piece English and Australian girths with
removable felt pads for easy washing. Exclusively developed by
Flint Saddlery with the narrow-built horse in mind are the
Two-Piece Western Contoured Cinch® and the Two-Piece English
Contoured Girth® also with removable felt pads for easy washing.
The removable felt pad is easy to keep clean and exchangeable
which helps to prevent the spread of skin diseases from horse to
horse when working several horses a day.

Other fine equestrian products that offer the Flint Original
Hospital Felt therapeutic properties are the Western, English
and Australian saddle pads and Orthopaedic saddle pads,
under-blanket pads, breast collars and training surcingles.

David Flint is constantly experimenting and subsequently
up-dating his equestrian products. His commitment to top
quality and innovation is evident in the complete line of Flint
Saddlery equestrian products.

Flint Saddlery is currently exporting to Australia and the
United States.

Flint Saddlery is exhibiting at the CAN-AM Show, Spruce Meadows,
Alberta, in Spring and in Fall of each year.

In addition to trade shows, we publish a quarterly Newsletter
which is distributed via direct mailing to our current
customers. If you would like to receive this Newsletter please
contact us.

We also manufacture in addition to our line of Original Hospital
Felt equestrian products, equestrian products with Neoprene.
Our equestrian products with Neoprene are trademarked as "FOR
WINNERS ONLY"!

Product Name:
ORIGINAL HOSPITAL FELT EQUESTRIAN PRODUCTS
How and why was the Flint Original Hospital Felt pad developed?
David Flint did not set out to manufacture felt pads but in 1989
he was asked by concerned professional equestrian people
(English and Western discipline) to help them solve the problem
of their horses getting sore backs from being ridden long hours
every day.
David spent about a year trying all the various pads on the
market, even importing several from Europe. The sore back
problems persisted. Then he discovered a special felt pad being
used in the hospital bed of a friend. He contacted the mill and
made up a sample pad. It helped the horses. He worked with the
mill on some changes to their felting process to make the
product better for use with horses. The final product worked
well and became the only pad many professional equestrian people
now use. Other customers heard of the value of the pad and
David began custom making the pad for them. Thus a new sideline
to his regular saddle making business began from his willingness
to help the professional equestrian people.

What makes the Flint Original Hospital Felt pad different from
all those other "hospital felt" pads?
What other manufacturers call "hospital felt" is really
orthopaedic or sports felt. It is made up of long polyester
fibres which will slip on the horse's back, generate heat and
has hardly any absorption, wicking or heat dissipation values.
Flint Original Hospital Felt is made up of a very high
percentage of short man-made fibre with the same qualities as
natural fibre together with excellent absorption, wicking and
heat dissipation properties. It requires a special
manufacturing process to make this felt, resulting in a raw
product cost considerable greater than orthopaedic felt. We
know of no other pad manufacturer that uses this grade of felt,
in fact, Flint Saddlery, has an exclusive agreement with the
mill for this felt product.

How can I tell the difference?
Visually, it is tough. However, there are two simple tests you
can perform. Rubbing your knuckles hard on orthopaedic or sports
felt will generate a considerable amount of heat. A similar
rubbing on Flint Original Hospital Felt will result in hardly
any heat generation. Holding a flame to the edge of other felt
pads will cause the pad to melt, revealing that it is really a
type of plastic. The same flame test will cause the Flint
Original Hospital Felt pad to burn, similar to a natural fibre.
The easiest way to ensure you are getting the Flint Original
Hospital Felt pad is to look for the Flint Saddlery Bucking
Bronco Logo ®.

How will a Flint Original Hospital Felt pad help my horse?
The pad has excellent shock absorption value and the main
advantage is its ability to absorb and wick both moisture and
heat away from the horse's body. The short fibres work with the
horse's coat resulting in much less slippage. Bacteria won't
grow in the pad. You will find that your horse is much drier
under the pad after a ride. Remember after the Flint Original
Hospital Felt pad was developed, endurance riders, professional
packers and outfitters have reported instant success when
switching to the Flint pads.

How do I keep the felt pads clean?
They wash beautifully. We recommend vacuuming off all the horse
hair and washing the 1 pads in the triple loaders at the
laundromat. The 1/4" and 1/2" saddle pads, cinch, girth and
breast collar pads can be washed in the machine at home. Wash
in luke warm water and machine dry at very low heat. (High heat
won't harm the felt but it will cause the nylon wear strips on
the pads to wrinkle and shrink.) Washing actually enhances
performance and increases loft. NEW PADS SHOULD BE WASHED
BEFORE USE.

ENGLISH / DRESSAGE SADDLE PADS
The durable, shock absorbent saddle pads are manufactured from
Flint Original Hospital Felt in 3/8 thickness. The pads are

The Flint Original Hospital Felt is at its best when used in
the saddle pads David makes for Western riding. Its many
features may be reviewed on the first page and the Questions &
Answers section. Western/Ropers pads come in three
thicknesses (1", 1/2", 1/4") determined by usage. The Western,
Ropers and Packers saddle pads are covered on the wear area with
heavy duty natural or black canvas. For a dressier pad, 100%
cotton heavy duty cloth in a South-western Design may be
specified. All under blanket pads come without wear pads and
are designed for use under a Flint pad or Navajo blanket for
cleanliness and additional protection. Advise of special sizes
for under blanket pads or if wear leathers are needed. For
Western/Ropers Orthopaedic saddle pads, please refer to
Orthopaedic pads.
