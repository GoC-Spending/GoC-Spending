Company Profile


Can-Eng is one of North America's leading manufacturers of
industrial heat processing furnaces and related equipment. Since
its formation in 1964, the company has gained an international
reputation for design innovation, quality workmanship and a
commitment to service.

Can-Eng offers its customers proven furnace building expertise
start to finish. From system design and construction through
follow-up support, the company maintains comprehensive in-house
capabilities to provide equipment which reflects the diverse
needs of all its clients in the heat treating industry.
The people at Can-Eng have the depth of experience to produce a
custom engineered product that can benefit even your most
demanding applications. This depth of experience has given Can-
Eng the opportunity to export their products to various
industries throughout the world.

Can-Eng is affiliated with ASM International and the Metal
Treating Institute (MTI). Can-Eng is ISO 9001:2008 certified.

Product Name:
Can-Eng Mesh Belt Atmosphere Furnace System
Highly respected for their reliability, the Can-Eng Mesh Belt
furnace is designed for neutral hardening, carburizing, carboni-
triding and austempering. Low operating costs are achieved by
use of recuperative burners, low atmosphere consumption and
part preheating using waste heat from the belt return. Standard
sizes available to handle from 250 lb/hour to 7500 lbs/hours.

Can-Eng systems are usually supplied as totally automated, turn-key heat treating lines which include computer controlled loading systems, pre-wash stations, hardening furnaces, quench systems, post washer stations and tempering furnaces. Auxiliary equipment such as dephosphating systems, blackening systems, bin handling, atmosphere generators and full SCADA capabilities are also available to complete the system automation.

System Benefits:
Continuous production which delivers superior part quality
High capacity, external belt loading designs that improve throughput efficiency while reducing part mixing and damage
Specific design features to minimize possibility of foreign material
Proven field reliability and maximum up-time performance

Maximum fuel efficiency through innovative design and energy recuperation
Fully automated SCADA systems with enhanced reporting and data collection capabilities
Neutral and case hardening capabilities
Standard production capabilities from 250 lbs/hr up to 7500 lbs/hr also show in metric throughout

Product Name:
Can-Eng Quenching and Descale Technologies
Can-Eng has developed a broad spectrum of quenching expertise in a wide range of areas, including immersion, water spray, PAQ (precision air quench), molten salt, lead bath, bismuth bath, oil, polymer, fluid bed, and forced air cool.

Our fundamental understanding of heat transfer as applied to quenching has allowed Can-Eng to offer the broadest quenching technologies available.

From the world's largest immersion water and oil quench (500,000 liters), to the precise high velocity cooling of the PAQ, Can-Eng is a recognized leader in quenching technology for ferrous and non ferrous metals.

Can-Eng can also supply high pressure descale as required prior to rotary forging, or prior to tube quench and temper.
Can-Eng can provide:
Immersion Quench Tanks up to 500,000 Liters in Oil, Polymer, Water
Spray Quenching of Bars and Tubes
Restrained Mass flow Drill Tube and Bar Quenching
Restrained Roller Spray Quench for Plates
Austemper in molten salts for Upper and Lower Bainitic Transformations
Marquenching
Conventional and Hot Oil Quenching
Precision Air Quench (PAQ) of critical aluminum automotive components
Forced Air Cooling for Martensitic Stainless
Lead Bath, Molten Bismuth for Patenting
Fluid Bed Quenching
Water Shower Curtain Technology

Can-Eng Quenching and Descale Technologies
Can-Eng has developed a broad spectrum of quenching expertise in a wide range of areas, including immersion, water spray, PAQ (precision air quench), molten salt, lead bath, bismuth bath, oil, polymer, fluid bed, and forced air cool.

Our fundamental understanding of heat transfer as applied to quenching has allowed Can-Eng to offer the broadest quenching technologies available.
