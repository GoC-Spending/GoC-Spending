Company Profile


StreetSense was formed in 2007 to provide personal safety and self-defense training for the corporate sector, government, schools, law enforcement, and the public. When I started the company my goal was to provide my clients with the skills and knowledge to reduce their risk of being targeted for crime and violence. While this is still my goal, how I do this has evolved, and expanded astronomically.

I’ve radically altered my approach to how I teach crime and violence prevention. What I’ve discovered is that the traditional approaches for reducing incidents of criminal activity and violence aren’t working; at least not very well. What’s needed is for training to provide the average person with the ability to recognize, profile, assess and mitigate threats to their safety across all environments.

As I developed the StreetSense program, I thought a great deal about my work as a correctional officer. Every day I work amongst some of the most violent and unpredictable members of society. Fear, intimidation, and violence are a part of the lexicon of my workplace. Yet surprisingly there are far fewer instances of officers being targeted for violence than you think. This has everything to do with our ability to accurately assess the intentions of those around us, and take proactive action to either de-escalate the situation, or neutralize the threat. It also has a lot to do with how we train to mitigate threats.

The StreetSense curriculum is designed to provide my customers with these same skill sets, without the need to spend 25 years in prison acquiring them. Intelligence based, StreetSense programs teach clients Proactive Personal Safety (PPS), and how to quickly and accurately profile individuals in their environment and determine if they pose a threat. Learning to identify the pre-incident indicators that are always present prior to crime or violence occurring provides clients with an increased array of response options. Coupled with reality based Aggression Control Tactics (ACT) they are also well prepared to deal with whatever life throws at them.

I’ve been involved in the combat arts for most of my life. I’ve also been fortunate to have learned from some of the best martial artists in the field. This is a big part of the problem with teaching people how to respond physically to threats to their safety. Most people won’t spend years studying the combat arts. Statistics indicate that only 1/1000 people who begin training reach black belt level. Aggression control tactics training is a solution.

Simple, effective, and devastating is how clients refer to this program. Training focuses on situational awareness, target hardening and behavioural profiling. It teaches clients how crime and violence actually occur. Training is concept based and designed to provide a full spectrum of response options. We are not going to change the fact that there will always be a segment of the population that sees the rest of us as potential targets for crime and predation. What can change is how you look at the world, and your place in it. You don’t have to live in fear, but you can use it to keep you and your family safe.I am a veteran correctional officer with over twenty five years of experience working in a maximum security facility. I have been involved in the combat arts since 1970 and my training has been both eclectic and practical. I’ve deployed in many capacities during my security career from working as a doorman in some pretty sketchy bars, to teaching hand to hand combat to close protection operatives.
Working in an occupation where violence is a regular occurrence has taught me a few things about what does and does not work in situations where violence happens fast, and your life could be on the line. These experiences have had a strong influence on the way I teach and consult for my clients.

Service Name:
Workplace Violence Prevention
Violence is endemic in our culture. In order to reduce instances of workplace violence you must first acknowledge that you are at risk.Many businesses face the challenges of safely conducting operations in potentially hostile environments. Safety and security are important concepts as your personnel may be exposed to a wide variety of threats and risks during the course of their day to day activities.

During the past two decades many people have lost their lives, suffered injury, or been exposed to crime, violence and trauma while going about their regular work duties. This highlights the importance of investing in specific training designed to increase employee’s ability to recognize threats to their safety and security, and increase their chances of survival if they are place in harm’s way.

The business community is often required to deploy employees to varying types of workplaces, and some are higher risk than others. This requires a serious commitment by the organization to address the safety and security of its personnel. In this context workplace violence prevention training is deemed to be a valuable support tool to organizations in regards to their duty of care to their deployed personnel.

StreetSense Inc. has developed a Workplace Violence Prevention program (WVP) designed specifically to assist organizations in training their personnel to increase their personal safety and security while both within the workplace, and in their private lives.

Safety and Security are essential to the orderly operations of business regardless of the organizations mandate. Properly training and equipping personnel is part of the duty of care for every organization. This includes all pre-deployment medical and psychiatric screening, workplace violence prevention training, and ensuring a tested and functioning security management framework is in place.

WVP training is crucial for anyone working in the modern business environment. The curriculum provides comprehensive coverage of the relevant issues and is designed to safely simulate the types of situations employees may encounter. The program stresses proactive security, and teaches participants how to avoid high risk situations, escape and evade, and survive.

The course utilizes classroom theory, hands on training, and is complemented by simulations of real-life situations such as angry/violent customers, employees, criminality, assaults, and weapons related incidents. After each exercise and simulation experienced trainers will debrief the group on the behaviour of individual participants and of the group as a whole. The aim is the highlighting critical problems and issues that emerge during training and of improving personal and group functionality under difficult conditions.

Service Name:
School Violence Prevention
The StreetSense School Violence Prevention program teaches students, and faculty how to recognize, profile, assess, and mitigate risk in an educational environment. My background teaching reality based self-defence enhanced by my work as an correctional officer has made me acutely aware of how crime and violence happen in the real world.

The StreetSense curriculum teaches student's and faculty the skills to utilize the personal safety triangle (situational awareness, target hardening, and behavioural profiling) to identify the pre-incident indicators that are always present prior to crime and violence occurring. The curriculum is reality based and utilizes a variety of training modalities to ensure participant success.

I provide training, education and consulting for both the Federal and Provincial government, Universities, Colleges, as well as many private schools and school boards. My eclectic background in security and personal protection has given me a unique opportunity to teach my clients how to utilize Proactive Personal Safety concepts to reduce their risk of being perceived as a potential target for criminals and predators. StreetSense is a contributing author to the National Workplace Violence Prevention Institutes magazine, and I regularly write a column for Law Enforcement Today magazine.
