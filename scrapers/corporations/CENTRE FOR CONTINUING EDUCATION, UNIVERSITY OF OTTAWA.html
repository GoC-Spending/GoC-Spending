THE RIGHT WORD
"The difference between the almost right word and the right word is really a large matter--it's the difference between the lightning bug and the lightning."
Mark Twain

Do you know the difference between a phone that rings continuously and one that rings continually? Should you ask a co-worker to be discrete or discreet about a personal matter? Does the bus go further up the road or farther or does it not matter? Many words in English look or sound alike, yet choosing the wrong one can blur, or even reverse, your intended meaning.

You don't have to have a big vocabulary of exotic words to write well. In fact, strong writing often uses simple, everyday words. You do have to use the words you know precisely. Recognizing the nuances and connotations as well as basic meanings of words will make you a more flexible and confident writer.

This fast-paced and energetic one-day course will ensure your writing is professional and precise. It will also expand your working vocabulary by enabling you to use more of the words you already know with confidence.

MANAGING THE PEOPLE-SIDE OF MULTIPLE PROJECTS AND PRIORITIES
In this workshop, you will experience and dissect a successful project climate and learn to create the foundation for it. You will learn to recognize the warning signs and symptoms of project teams that need attention and to resolve many of the situations that adversely impact projects. Project environments are often complex, and the need to deal with people is a largebut sometimes overlookedpart of the challenge of managing them. In our exploration of the process of working in projects, we learn about team dynamics, complacency, and conflict. We identify ways of getting and keeping support for your project and dealing with changes, all while attempting to produce results and respecting the triple constraint that guides good project management.

Service Name:
MICROSOFT PROJECT 2010 HANDS-ON
Managing a project without project management software is like accounting without spreadsheets. This two-day workshop will teach the attendee to automate each process of project management: a) in the planning phase, using Microsoft Project to create work breakdown structures, estimate time and cost, and schedule the project; and b) in the execution phase, using Microsoft Project to view project status, compare progress against plan, track actual expense versus budget, balance resource usage, and create powerful detail and roll-up reports.

This workshop is totally hands-on with all learning exercises performed on the computers provided. Familiarity with Windows and basic knowledge of project management is an asset.

Service Name:
PERSUASIVE SPEAKING FOR EXECUTIVES
Persuading decision-makers such as high-ranking officials, power-brokers, and political authorities requires strategic thinking and complex situation-specific plans. Often, calls to persuade appear when we least expect them. Winning executives and aspiring senior executives are prepared in advance to deliver oral presentations that convince even the most scrutinizing decision-maker.

Our powers of persuasion are all too frequently called on. If a program needs to change focus or direction, then those affected by the change will need to be convinced of the need for it. If a new policy must be developed, then persuading someone in a position of authority is often the first step. We are always expected to stand up and deliver strategies, ideas, and options in an engaging as well as convincing manner. This workshop will help you with both the art and the science of persuasion. It will prepare you not only to persuade senior decision-makers with strategically planned and prepared oral presentations but also to feel comfortable doing so.

Service Name:
SELF-KNOWLEDGE AND THE WORKPLACE
Self-knowledge, in the context of this workshop, is understanding the important link between what you believe and what you do. Self-knowledge, as a strategy, is an applied approach to assessing your strengths and weaknesses, and their impact in your working life. Emphasis is on your day-to-day interaction with the people who have the greatest influence on your morale, motivation and career development/job satisfaction.

In this workshop, participants will have the opportunity to develop insights into their current reality by completing a number of self-discovery tools and comparing their responses with established benchmarks. Exploring your profile and the implications inherent in how you interact with others increases your awareness of what connects you to others. As a result, it enables you to understand your power to manage and shape the creation of your desired future.

In addition to self-discovery, the workshop will enable individuals to determine their unique strengths and development areas. Working with their peers as learning partners, they will prioritize their development areas and create an action/learning plan. Success indicators will be established to assist participants in transferring the knowledge acquired back on the job.

Service Name:
TURNING DIVERSITY INTO A TANGIBLE ASSET
Discussions about diversity usually focus on how to increase it or how to be sensitive to it. Instead, this workshop focuses on why diversity is such an asset, and how the manager can capitalize on it to build stronger teams and organizations. Diversity provides its value from the variety of experiences, skills, knowledge, motivations and perspectives naturally present within a group of people. This interactive workshop helps leaders understand how to identify these assets and, in doing so, to build more effective work environments that engage employees, are more efficient at problem solving, create more satisfied customers, and increase productivity.

Participants will develop an appreciation for the multifaceted elements of diversity and learn a concrete analytical approach to leverage diversity to drive organizational performance.
