Company Profile


Tremain Media Inc. began in 2001 as the sole proprietorship Read-TV Productions. Its first release, a literacy-based DVD was produced by Leah Tremain as the thesis project for her Masters in Education, thus establishing the companys fundamental link between education and video production.

In 2006 the Read-TV proprietorship, brand, intellectual properties and all its assets were purchased and folded into the newly-formed enterprise, Tremain Media Incorporated.

Tremain Media was incorporated with two shareholders - Leah Tremain and the University of Victorias Innovation and Development Corporation. In 2011 Tremain Media Inc. purchased all shares owned by University of Victorias Innovation and Development Corporation (now UVic Industry Partnership) thus terminating the firms relationship with the University. Tremain Media is now under the sole ownership of Leah Tremain, M.Ed.

The primary area of focus for Tremain Media is producing educational material in partnership with educational institutions, not-for-profits, foundations and charities. Tremain Media retains ownership of a number of titles and sells the material to schools, institutions, post-secondary institutions, not-for-profits, libraries and homes across Canada and the US. Tremain Media has three educational distributors and have sold over 68,000 of their educational DVDs across Canada.

Tremain Medias success in the educational market has caused clients to request their services in producing exclusive material, thus engaging Tremain Media as a client-focused, work-for-hire company, thereby enabling an expansion of scope and services. Currently, Tremain Media produces a range of educational and promotional content in a variety of media. The company produces film and television content, web- and social media sites, books, advertising and a broad range of print and web-based materials. Tremain Media content has aired on NBC-Universal, Versus and NBC Sports. Additional offerings include promotional, educational media for a variety of government and corporate clients, including the Ministry of Children & Family Development, BC Hydro, Transport Canada, YVR and Alberta Education.

Tremain Media engages pre-production, production and post production sub-contractors and consultants on a project-by-project basis. Tremain Media has two full time staff members and up to 23 sub-contractors and consultants.

Tremain Media has become an expert in time-lapse and digital monitoring services, with cameras in operation throughout Western Canada, amassing over one million photos to date. The cloud-based visual data system on which the time-lapse is run is unparalleled in the industry. Tremain has recently developed a complete Canada-only cloud solution of their monitoring and time-lapse for the Canadian Federal Government. The company also creates apps and custom software and develops data systems for remote site offices.

Tremain Media built READTV: Render Engine's Daily Timelapse Video. This is their proprietorial cloud-based software engine that powers their timelapse services.
Tremain Media built RESCU: Render Engine's Satellite Caching Unit. This system allows clients to access visual data via constricted satellite services.

Distribution of employees:

We have a core team of contractors, specialists and consultants that have been with Tremain Media Inc. for over five years.

Unique applications:

Time-lapse allows a client to document and showcase construction, deconstruction and remediation projects in a condensed format. Our time-lapse system is powered by Render Engine in the cloud. Render Engine is Tremain Medias proprietorial automated daily deliverable system.

We utilize a variety of high-end HD outdoor rated cameras in secure housings. Once a client sees the quality of the picture, and the ease from which one can control the PTZ camera, we are often asked to set-up a camera for secure on-site monitoring.

We've had cameras up at an urban remediation project and at a remote turbine replacement project at one of the tallest earthen-filled dams in the world. At the end of the ten month turbine replacement project we amassed over 8 million photos. Our data manager has written scripts exclusive to Tremain Media that stitch these photos together in number of formats and sizes. We are also able to provide time-lapse within a time frame that you choose. For longer projects we typically supply monthly time-lapse updates, a final full time-lapse and a finished documentary of your project.

Our newest services include a Canada only, secure, real-time, high definition, construction site video monitoring with remote access capabilities. It allow senior management to track on-site activities without the need to travel to the site. Additionally, time lapse video is included in daily reporting and included in a post construction video that chronicles key on-site activities.

In remote locations we employ our power specialist to set up a power source to the cameras  and, if needed, configure satellites to monitor the site and transfer photos to our back-up system. On-site and off-site backups ensure we capture all mission-critical moments of your project.
