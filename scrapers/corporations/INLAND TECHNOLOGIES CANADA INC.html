Company Profile


Since 1990, the Inland group of companies, headquartered in Truro, Nova Scotia, has specialized in the management of airport glycol collection and recycling services. With operations in North America, Europe and Asia, our primary area of expertise lies in providing a cost-effective means of recovering and reprocessing glycols collected from military and civilian airport de-icing and anti-icing operations.

Inland is both an equipment manufacturer and a service provider, and as such, we are able to provide services ranging from building and operating turnkey facilities to delivering a component of the glycol recovery process.

One of our key manufactured products is the patented Glycol Concentrator. This technologically advanced, cost-effective system removes water from spent aircraft de-icing fluids using very little energy. We also have designed and developed our own glycol recovery unit [the Glyvac™] for the effective pick up of spent aircraft de-icing fluid from ramp, our own distillation plant, and our own drain bloack containment system [the GlycolGuard™]. These innovations allow us to provide closed loop recycling services at our client sites.

Inland Technologies has grown to become one of Canada's largest independently owned specialty waste management companies, processing hundreds of millions of litres of waste annually. While spent glycol management is our primary focus, we also offer a range of aviation ground services including aircraft de-icing, snow removal, and runway rubber removal.

We are committed to delivering the highest level of customer service and environmental protection through an investment in on-going research and development and will continue to broaden our product line by taking into consideration each customer's requirements and needs.

Product / Service / Licensing

Product Name:
Environmental Products and Services
Product Name:
Inland Airport Environmental Services and Glycol Concentrator Technology.
Inland Technologies Inc. has developed glycol and water treatment systems designed specifically for the wide ranging requirements of airlines and airports. The service offers a total waste management approach involving site analysis, efficient collection, storage, treatment and disposal of recovered de-icing fluids and contaminated storm water. Inland's state-of-the-art glycol recycling technology provides maximum re-utilization of spent de-icing fluids.

The further water treatment process ensures regulatory compliance. The glycol concentrator provides an extremely cost efficient method to recycle the spent ADF into a marketable product for reuse again at the airport or for outside vendors. The process is based on a distillation unit with vapour recompression, and unique heat recovery systems for energy efficiency. Inland's extensive experience in this field permits airports and airlines to meet regulatory requirements for airport storm water discharge and realize the cost benefits of having a recycled product.

Product Name:
Inland's Modular Oily Waste Treatment Process & Environmental Management Systems
Inland's proven oily waste treatment process incorporates a modular designed system to recover waste petroleum liquids at low cost and produce recycled alternative fuels suitable for heavy industry. The unique design permits rapid, low cost construction and installation including controls and ancillary equipment processes. Processes are modified and added to consider waste volumes, waste types and local regulatory issues.

Additional processes can be incorporated to treat sludges, greases, soils, used oil filters and solvent recovery. The successful management petroleum based wastes from a region or country will require not only proper recycling but also the infrastructure that supports it. Inland's solutions include; assisting the development of relevant regulations; promoting and supplying proper storage containers; efficient collection/transportation systems; consulting and the sale of recycling products.

Product Name:
MEG Concentrator
Inland has designed a developed a patented MEG Concentrator, used in regenerating MEG [Mono-Ethylene Glycol]. The application is designed to prevent hydrate inhibition in offshore gas plants. The MEG Concentrator is suited to small to medium installations. We have also designed and developed a MEG distillation plant suitable for small to medium scale operations.

Product Name:
Glyvac™ - Glycol Recovery Vehicle
Inland designs and manufactures our patented Glyvac™ glycol recovery vehicle for the fast and efficient recovery of spent aircraft de-icing fluid from airport tarmacs. Compared to other recovery vehicles, the Glyvac™ offers a number of features that drive operational efficiency including a large holding tank, quick off-loading, low maintenance costs and up to a 40% fuel savings over similar vehicles.

Product Name:
GlycolGuard™
Inland designs and develops the GlycolGuard™ drain block system. Because of its innovative design, our drain block is highly effective in preventing unwanted runoff during de-icing operations or during other situations where containment is essential.The unit has a shallow basin, which allows effluent to be easily vacuumed using a recovery vehicle or hose without removing the grate. The unit can also be manufactured with a removable polypropylene filtration sleeve for removal of trace hydrocarbons from the stormwater and added environmental protection.

The GlycolGuard™ drain mechanism is unique in the industry in that it drops down to open. This feature allows the drain to open even if completely clogged with snow and ice. Because of the design, sediment build-up is prevented as water flow helps keep the surface area free of debris.

Service Name:
Aircraft De-icing Fluid Recovery
Complete service for the collection of spent Aircraft De-icing Fluid. Includes Airport environmental monitoring and regulatory
compliance for storm water release from airport de-icing areas. Inland incorporates the Glyvac™ - Glycol Recovery Vehicle and the GlycolGuard™ drain block to offer winter hub airports of various containment programs.

Service Name:
Aircraft De-icing Fluid Recycling
Recycling of spent Aircraft De-icing Fluid for aircraft de-icing or as glycol base stock. Inland utilizes our Glycol Concentrators and the Starcevic Distillation System™, along with various filtration and reverse osmosis systems to recycling spent de-icing fluid.

Inland provides a variety of management services supporting airport environmental compliance including stormwater management, lagoon management, liaising with public treatment plants, testing and compliance management, and managing equipment and systems such as anaerobic digesters and engineered wetlands.

Service Name:
Aircraft De-Icing/Anti-icing
Aircraft safety is our primary concern. Inland's de-icing service brings together highly-experienced, well-trained staff, and state-of-the-art equipment and systems to ensure aircraft are de-iced safely and efficiently.

Our de-icing operators quickly become specialists as they complete our extensive training program. We first cover the basics of our in-house de-icing methodology and also complete each of our customers' specific training programs in a classroom setting. This is followed by individual testing to ensure each operator has a thorough understanding of the curriculum and client requirements.

Once certified on the equipment, our operators are taught proper driving techniques, airport driving patterns, and proper pad (or gate) de-icing techniques as well as the use of safe zones for the equipment to ensure the safety of everyone involved.

Each step of the de-icing operation is carefully documented. We use customized software to ensure each aspect of the de-icing process is logged and safely managed. We also use advanced software to ensure the time in the de-icing queue is minimized and wait times are as close to non-existent as possible. Our equipment is also technologically driven with on-board fluid blending and forced air/air injection systems to limit the amount of fluid needed to effectively de-ice aircraft.

Inland also uses advanced equipment, systems and work practices and systems to reduce the volume of aircraft de-icing fluid used. This translates in a direct chemical savings for our clients, but also a reduction in the level of environmental management required.




Service Name:
Runway Rubber and Paint Removal
Inland owns and operates sophisticated high pressure paint removal equipment that also recovers contaminants in a single pass. Our Stripe Hog® equipment is efficient [one person operation], uses no chemicals, and quickly removes and recovers rubber and paint on a single chassis without damaging pavement surfaces.

Service Name:
Waste Mangement/Enviromental Consulation
Since the early 1980's Inland Technologies has specialized in general waste management. Our current areas of specialization include: Industrial wastewater treatment; Site remediation of hydrocarbon impacted soils, as well as other contaminants; Waste oil recycling
and Hazardous waste management.
