Company Profile


Integra is committed to delivering superior Waste to Energy solutions as well as alternate energy modalities with above-standard environmental benefits to industries, governments and communities In North America and beyond.
Waste and energy are the two main components of the worlds sustainability/environment crisis. Integras hybrid energy-from-waste technology addresses these issues by producing green energy to electricity and by virtually eliminating the need for landfill, both of which contribute to the significant reduction of greenhouse gas.
Integra is committed to delivering environmentally-responsible solutions that reduce greenhouse gases, methane and CO2, through the advancement of Integra Global Green Energy Inc. Canada cutting edge technology, remediation, conversion, and alternate energy platforms.
"Municipal Waste Streams" are considered a designated renewable resource that can be recycled into electricity using the Integra W2E system. It is estimated that for every ton of MSW put into landfill, there is an equivalent of 1.5 tons of greenhouse gases being emitted.
By diverting waste from landfills/biomass and eliminating long-distance hauling, a large amount of greenhouse gas emissions are cut out of the waste disposal equation.

Product / Service / Licensing

Out-License (Offering):
Energy from Waste/Biomass Power Plants
How Integra's system works:
The Integra system converts energy from one form to another. The operating process can be summarized as follows:
1. Waste is transported to the facility where it is prepared. It enters the system's primary chamber, which vaporizes the energy-laden waste at high thermal thresholds and starts an energy cascade that results in the formation of fuel gases much like natural gas.
2. These reformed gases enter a second chamber where they are
thermodynamically balanced to supply thermal units to the boiler system which provides the steam for the turbine generators.
3. The treated soil and/or other materials are recovered, verified and
classified for reuse.
4. The technology generates billions of British Thermal Units of thermal
energy per day. During the conversion cascade, this energy is harnessed by means of electrical co-generation.
The Integra system is a continuous feed conversion that allows for greater operational efficiency and an extremely high conversion capacity. Each system has the capacity to process 1,000 tons of characterized processed waste and produce 60+ MWH of power.
Recovered energy is used to co-generate electricity that may be used locally, or sold to the power grid. Therefore, the capital costs for an integrated energy-from waste Integra system can be recouped in one to three years, based on a number of factors such as the size of the facility, tipping fees, carbon credits and the operating efficiency ratio.
The system is modular and can be easily upgraded to increase capacities. It is very efficient and requires little fuel to destroy the waste material put through it, making the technology even more profitable by reducing added fuel costs. T
