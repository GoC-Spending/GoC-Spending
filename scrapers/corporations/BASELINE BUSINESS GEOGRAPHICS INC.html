Product Name:
MapInfo Product Line
MapInfo Professional® is a powerful Microsoft® Windows®-based mapping and geographic analysis application from the experts in location intelligence. Designed to easily visualize the relationships between data and geography, MapInfo Professional helps business analysts, planners, GIS professionals  even non-GIS users  gain new insights into their markets, share information-rich maps and graphs and improve strategic decision-making.


MapInfo® Vertical Mapper works seamlessly within MapInfo Professional® to help you display, manage and interpret grid-based continuous spatial information. If you want answers from location-based data, Vertical Mapper will find them, creating grids, or continuous surfaces of information, from point data by estimating values between known points. Vertical Mapper helps turn your data investment into compelling, meaningful information to give your business the upper hand.


MapXtreme® is the leading software development kit (SDK) for integrating location intelligence with existing business systems. It allows developers to build custom mapping applications, provide tailored views of geographic data and automate and augment business processes.

Technology profile

A Geographic Information System (GIS) is a computer system that
creates, stores, manipulates, analyses and displays multiple
layers of data, the data being linked to actual points on the
surface of the earth. GIS data is held in tables, the tables
containing attributes (such as a city name) and a geographical
reference (such as a latitude and longitude). Some tables may
contain 'base' maps of a country, others may contain population
census data, while others may contain region information such as
municipal boundaries or superstore catchment areas. When a
number of tables are opened at once they can be viewed, analysed
and manipulated in order to extract useful information. For
example the above tables could be used to determine the best
municipality in which to site a glass recycling plant. By using
a GIS the task of analysing data, looking for trends, finding
the best location for a new facility, balancing sales
territories and numerous other tasks is made much easier.

Business Mapping offers a new way to organize, visualise and
analyse business data by using maps and the geographic
relationships that already exist within the data. Data from
throughout your organization can be merged into a single
coherent view in a form which is easily understandable. The data
thus becomes powerful information, revealing patterns and trends
that are otherwise hidden. This new visualisation is becoming
increasingly crucial to decision making at all levels of your
enterprise.
