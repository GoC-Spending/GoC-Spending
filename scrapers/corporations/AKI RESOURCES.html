Company Profile


Kishk Anaquot Health Research has been operating since 1994 providing a variety of government and non-governmental clients, communities, universities and others advice on human service, governance and community development issues. Now operating under the business name AKI (EARTH in Algonquin) Resources, the company has expanded to include comprehensive sustainability planning and programming. The group of companies offer services related to community and program development, public health administration, research, training, facilitation as well as evaluation and performance measurement.

The company principal is:
* a visionary with a sound understanding of human health and how to create it;
* a superior leader serving public health venues, government departments and non-governmental organizations;
* able to quickly grasp and clearly articulate advanced concepts;
* able to think strategically and focus efforts to generate solutions;
* able to resolve critical challenges related to program efficacy and performance measurement with a question driven approach;
* able to work effectively with strategic partners.

The principal and team are able to bring thoughtful leadership, critical analysis and generative, solution orientation to address the needs of challenging and rapidly changing public policy environments seeking to achieve their desired goals.

Key / Major clients:

The National Collaborating Centre on Aboriginal Health; the Solicitor General of Canada; the Canadian Coalition for Global Health Research; The World Bank; Carleton University; Canadian Aboriginal AIDS Network; Canadian Population Health Initiative at the Canadian Institute for Health Information; The Aboriginal Healing Foundation; Canadian Heritage; Healthy Babies Healthy Children with the Province of Ontario; various departments within Health Canada [Childhood and Youth Division, Medical Services Branch; Health Promotion and Protection Branch, Population Health and Epidemiology] National Aboriginal Health Organization; Kitikmeot Board of Education; Somerset West Community Health Centre; Canadas National Forum on Health: Determinants of Health Working Group; Canadian Centre on Substance Abuse; Direction de la Santé Publique Régie régionale de la santé de des services sociaux de Montréal-centre; Pauline Jewitt Institute of Womens Studies, Carleton University; Canadian Medical Association; Department of Community Health Sciences, University of Manitoba; Royal Commission on Aboriginal Peoples; Community Health Programs Division, Medical Services Branch; Kitchener Waterloo Hospital; Department of Health Studies, University of Waterloo

Success stories:

Clients have used my services primarily for policy direction and program evolution. In some cases, the works have resulted in publication such as this sample from the National Collaborating Centre for Aboriginal Health http://www.nccah-ccnsa.ca/Publications/Lists/Publications/Attachments/87/Urban_Families_EN_web.pdf or the work has resulted in program evolution and policy direction http://www.ahf.ca/downloads/final-report-vol-2.pdf. In another case, the comprehensive sustainability planning conducted with Centretown Citizens Ottawa Corporation, was adapted and integrated with the corporations over all strategic plan. Similarly, in a community engagement process to reinforce internal accountability, a new vision and accountability framework was developed for the Kitigan Zibi Anishnabeg (See http://kzadmin.com/NewsDocuments/469_KZA_VisionDoc.pdf andhttp://kzadmin.com/NewsDocuments/468_KZA_AccountabilityDoc.pdf). In the realm of governance, some of our work on Measuring What Matters has directed the performance measurement strategies of Aboriginal Affairs and Northern Development Canada. In addition, several other national initiatives have responded to the evaluative recommendations made by the company over a two decade period that are too numerous to mention here.
