Swiftsure Spatial Systems Inc.

Company profile

Swiftsure Spatial Systems Inc. is a spatial systems consulting firm that was incorporated in British Columbia in 1996. The goal of Swiftsure is to help clients to define and use information systems that manage all their dataincluding spatial, cultural, historical, and multi-temporalin a unified, secure, web-enabled environment.

Swiftsure believes that the internet is only effective if based on open international standards. Swiftsure Spatial Systems has been active in the harmonization of spatial standards both within Canada and internationally.
Swiftsure are members of the OGC, the ISO SQLMM data management for evolving SQL, and ISO TC211. Swiftsure's goal is to provide clients with expertise to design information systems that manage all their data-including spatial, business, cultural, historical, and multi-temporal - in a unified, secure, web-enabled environment.
The staff of Swiftsure Spatial Systems are known worldwide for:

- Unparalleled expertise in modeling the temporal aspects of
spatial systems, "Temporality" includes source management, updating, historical data modeling, and multi-temporal data modeling.
- Gathering requirements for cutting edge, complex systems for intelligence analysis,
- Designing infrastructure to support enterprise system,
- Designing and guiding research programs for Universities, research centres and government agencies, and
- Managing the timely delivery of challenging prototypes and testbeds

Our team has served a wide array of corporate and government clients, including BC Lands, BC Environment, Geographic Data BC, Public Works and Government Services Canada, Information Data
Corporation (IDC), Intergraph Corporation, Integrated Spatial
Systems Inc. (ISSI), Statistics Canada, the Canadian Centre for Marine Communication, Sierra Systems, MDA, Rosettex Corp, Northrup Grumman- TASC, and the US Army CERL.

Swiftsure personnel have worked on joint research with
faculty at University of Victoria and Université Laval and served on the board of the GEOIDE Network Center of Excellence.

Swiftsures Spatial Systems' two founders, Gail Kucera and Henry
Kucera, offer a combined 46 years of experience in spatial
information management. Our offices are located in Victoria, BC.

Technology profile

Swiftsure Spatial Systems Inc. specializes in analysis and
design, i.e., determining what a system will do, and defining how
it will do it. To this end, we offer the following consulting
services:

 Requirements analysis
 Systems analysis
 Data model design
 Functional design
 System architecture design
 Test design and documentation
 Workshops and tutorials

The staff of Swiftsure Spatial Systems is known worldwide for
unparallelled expertise in modeling the temporal aspects of
spatial systems. Our projects include:

· Design and deployment of the award winning Canadian National Forest Information Systems (NFIS)
· User requirements survey and use case analysis of BC's TRIM
data users.
· Design of a migration path from a file-based TRIM distribution
system to a spatiotemporal data warehouse on a commercial
database platform.
· Object modelling to define open spatiotemporal data models for
the Mercator Alliance.
· Analysis of temporal requirements for the BC Road Network
Database.
· Harmonization of the SAIF, S-57 and DIGEST data models for
Mercator I.
· Critique and harmonization of the TC-211, OGC and SQL/MM
standards processes.
· Design of test cases for transaction-based updates for US Army
CERL.
· Workshops on temporal models and data standards in the context
of client requirements for Natural Resources Canada and
Statistics Canada.
· Development of a standards-based operational meta-content
framework and model for the BC Ministry of Environment Lands
and Parks.
· Development of the data architecture for distributed
information management for the BC Crown Lands Registry System.
· Development of the meta-content schema for the Canadian
Department of National Defence Data Warehouse Prototype.
· Development and delivery of a distributed multi-tier system for
terrain analysis and decision support for US Army Topographic
Engineering Centre.
· Creation and support of the National Health Surveillance System
Spatial Data Warehouse for Health Canada.
· Re-design of a Department of Fisheries and Oceans database for
managing salmon escapement data, to spatially enable the
database and for Year-2000 compliance.
· Design of the Research Strategy for the second phase of the GEOIDE Centres of Excellence Program
· Developing the research and business plan for the BC Centre for Advanced Remote sensing, Modelling and Simulation.
· Assisting Rosettex Technology and Ventures Group in the design of advanced research and deployment of cutting edge spatial technologies.
· Provide requirements analysis and program design capabilities to the US National Technology Alliance.
