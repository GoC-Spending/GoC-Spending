Company Profile


Bibliofiche is a library services company that serves library and information service centres in Canada and internationally. Services include support of open source library and digital archive management, asset management, textbook management, and online database management and authentication systems. Bibliofiche is also a library supply and library furniture supplier.

CERF Directory of educational Websites
CERF is an authoritative guide to safe educational Websites that provides access to resources for over 350,000 unique educational topics that have been selected by educators and catalogued by librarians. The directory provides international perspectives covering social studies, literature, languages, mathematics, science, religion, current events and professional development.

Service Name:
OPALS - Open Source Automated Library System
The OPALS Open-source Automated Library System is a powerful cooperatively developed, Web-based, open source program providing Internet access to information databases and library collections. Many schools, churches, businesses and Union collections with ILL needs are successfully using OPALS. There is no need to install software or purchase expensive computer hardware for this powerful Internet accessed system. There is no charge for software and usually nothing to install onto your workstations.

Bibliofiche supports OPALS with reasonably priced subscription technical support - update fees and Internet hosting services customized to meet the needs of small and large libraries.

Service Name:
DSpace - Open Source Digital Archive Software
DSpace preserves and enables easy and open access to all types of digital content including text, images, moving images, mpegs and data sets. Bibliofiche supports DSpace with reasonably priced services, support subscription fees, consultation, customization and Internet hosting services for small and large libraries.

Sector information

Key / Major clients:

Bibliofiche has served school, public, academic and special libraries in Canada, the US and around the world since for over 35 years. The company also supports open source library and information management systems. OPALS is one of only a few successful open source integrated library management systems that has been implemented in and used by 2000 libraries. Twenty one (more than half) of New York State cooperative educational school library systems use this technology. In addition, because of the software's Unicode capabilities, libraries can now catalogue their collections in Arabic, Chinese, Cyrillic (Russian), Hebrew and more. Libraries use these services in Africa, Asia, Europe and North America and many contract with Bibliofiche to host and manage these systems for them (SaaS).

Bibliofiche also supports systems that integrate and authenticate online databases and permit institutions to create and manage digital archives.
