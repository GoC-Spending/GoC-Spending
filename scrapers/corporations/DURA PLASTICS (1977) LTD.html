THE DURA GROUP

The Dura Group consists of two operating companies, DURA PLASTICS (1977)
LTD. hereafter called DURA PLASTICS, and DURA TEXTILES LTD. hereafter
called DURA TEXTILES.

DURA PLASTICS is comprised of a warehousing and distribution company,
and a PLASTICS RECYCLING DIVISION, while DURA TEXTILES is the converting
company.

DURA PLASTICS was founded in 1959 and commenced operations that year.
DURA TEXTILES was established in 1979 and commenced operations at that
time.

PLASTICS WAREHOUSING, DISTRIBUTION DIVISION, and DURA TEXTILES moved
to the groups own building which was purchased in February 1995 in
order to consolidate operations and increase efficiencies.

DURA PLASTICS warehouses polyvinyl chloride (PVC), polyurethane (PU),
ring metal, mechanisms and hardware, metal accessories for the stationary
trade, and paper products for lamination.

The PVC line includes calendared film in both rigid and flexible, as
well as calendared, extruded and cast expanded supported and unsupported,
and coated supported products. The products range from .002 gauge to
160 gauge and up to eighty-four (84) inches wide. The products are
sold to a variety of industries including heat sealers, bookbinders,
tent and awning manufacturers, sign manufacturers, silkscreeners, furniture
manufacturers and upholsterers, marine boat manufacturers and the marine
industry, blister and vacuum forming industries, etc.

PLASTICS PRIMARY CONVERTING equipment includes one sixty (60) inch
face embossing machine with a library of thirty-one (31) embossing
rollers, and one seventy-six (76) face multiply laminating embossing
machine with an additional five (5) embossing rollers.

PLASTICS SECONDARY CONVERTING equipment includes two (2) re rollers,
one (1) powdering machine, one sixty (60) inch Judelsohn razor-blade
slitter with a two thousand pound capacity, one sixty (60) inch slitter
duplex take-off, and two (2) computerized sixty (60) inch sheeter-slitters.

TEXTILES MACHINERY includes two (2) electronic quilting machines with
a library of fifty-six (56) quilting-embossing plates, two (2) Judelsohn
balony knive slitters, one (1) Cameron razor-blade-sheer-score duplex
take-off slitter-rewinder, one (1) guillotine, one (1) leather/composite
splitter, two (2) leather/composite skivers, six (6) heated binding
machines, two (2) cementing machines, two (2) twelve (12) inch sheeter/slitter/strip-cutters,
one sixty (60) inch beam press, one (1) clicker, one (1) sixty (60)
inch surface rewinder, numerous rewinders of different widths, numerous
narrow-width slitters-rewinders.

Plastic Extrusion machinery includes two 3 inch extruder line and one
1 inch extruder line, as well as various secondary processing machinery
with an in-house machine shop. Dura produces many of its own dies.

Founded in 1959, Dura Plastics (1977) Ltd. has grown into a vertically
integrated plastic distributor and converter in Canada.

The company has a diversified inventory of products and services as
follows:

Plastic Vinyl in Rolls for Industrial and Consumer use.
Plastic packaged Tablecloths and Plastic Tablecloth material in rolls.

Placemats
Soft Clears for table coverings, windows, marine boat tops, and café
enclosures.
Slitting, Sheeting, Die-Cutting, Coating and Laminating facilities.

Rigid packaging material in rolls and sheets for vacuum, and blister
forming, etc...
Leatherettes for chairs, restaurants, marine boats, automotives.
Polyurethane for Industrial as well as for clothing.


Worldwide shipments available.

EXTRUSION DIVISION

Dura Plastics purchased the extrusion operations of GINN BROS. LTD.
on February 1, 2000 which had been operating since 1944.

The primary manufacturing lines are as follows:

Two three (3) inch Extruder Lines
One two and one half (2 2) inch Extruder Line
One one (1) inch Extruder Line
The company processes PVC, Olefin, POLYETHYLENE, POLYPROPYLENE and
Nylon

The profile extrusions are varied and have both industrial and consumer
use. Some examples are: Furniture Strapping, Weed Eater Cording, Pipings,
Brade Linings, etc.
