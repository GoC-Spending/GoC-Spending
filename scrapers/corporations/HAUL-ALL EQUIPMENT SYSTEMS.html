Company Profile


Haul-All Equipment specializes in the design and manufacture of
waste and recycling systems including storage containers,
collection vehicles and transfer vehicles. Sub-sectors: Waste, recyclable & compost containerization, collection and transfer.


Products: Containers for waste, recyclables and compost; animal-proof, bear proof containers; collection/compaction bodies for refuse, recyclables and compost; modular transfer stations.


Company Description: Haul-All Equipment Ltd., has historical roots going back over 75. They have been designing and manufacturing side-loading collection vehicles for over 35 years. Today, Haul-All manufactures a complete line of material management equipment for the containerization, collection and transfer of waste, recyclables and compost.

Product Name:
The Haul-All Collection System
An economic study published in Resource Recycling states that, "whether your objective is maximizing diversion of running a cost effective recycling program, a well implemented drop-off program can yield the best economic results." The equipment used for the economic study was designed and manufactured by Haul-All.

Recognizing the volatile market for recyclables, Haul-All designed an operationally successful program by controlling collection costs without sacrificing service levels. The Haul-All Recycling System has all the advantages of a curbside program, including high diversion and user convenience, at savings up to 70%. Key features of the Haul-All System are:

- aesthetic, user friendly containers, strategically located to maximize convenience and awareness;

- flexible routing that maximizes vehicle and container utilization;

- light weight compaction bodies that use simple hydraulics, maximizing payload capacity; and

- high compaction collection vehicles for bulky light weight material.

These features lead to high material quality and volumes.

Product Name:
The TRANSTOR System for urban, rural and regional transfer
Cost-effective transfer of waste and/or recyclables is achieved using the TRANSTOR System for Urban or Rural Transfer. The TRANSTOR System is well used and has proven to be competitively successful for over 15 years. Operating savings of up to 50% are achieved through:

- flexible routing that maximizes trailer utilization; and fast turn around times.

The TRANSTOR System is a modular transfer station serviced by a transfer trailer. The TRANSTOR is a self-tipping hopper that can transfer up to 52 cu yards (40 cu metres) of material in approximately five minutes. This rapid transfer eliminates the down time associated with push pit and compactor transfer stations, increasing trailer utilization and reducing operating costs. In addition, the Transtor can load any open top or compacting trailer making it fully compatible with existing push pit transfer stations. This allows low cost expansion when regional expansion strategies are implemented or existing transfer stations become over utilized.

Sector information

Total plant size:

Haul-All Equipment Systems (Waste Management Equipment), Sure Flame Products (Construction Heaters), One4Haul (Mobile Garage and Portable Eco-Structures), Custom Manufacturing

Unique applications:

Region of Peel - Transfer System Program
Haul-All Transtors are the cornerstone of the Regions CRC (Community Recycling Centre) drop-off stations. Designed to maximize diversion after the closure of the Regions Brittania landfill, the 5 CRC systems are expected to divert over 40,000 tons (44,800 tonnes) per year from local landfills.
- TS500 Transtor units are used to contain and transfer municipal solid waste, yard waste, drywall, cardboard, mixed recyclables and tires. The TS500 units were selected for several reasons including the ability to provide fully sealed, closed containment of wastes and the speed of trailer loading using the unique ability to high side load collection trailers.
- To ensure efficient transportation, 100 cubic yard (76.5 cubic metre) high compaction transfer trailers are fully integrated into the Transtor system. These trailer units can stand alone to be loaded using a hydraulic power package or they can be dumped conventionally by the transfer tractors PTO system. Depending upon commodity, transport loads from 20 to 30 tons (22 to 33 tonnes) are common. This equates to packing loads up to 7 times higher than conventional roll-off containers.

Key / Major clients:

- National, Provincial and State parks
- Cities, Municipal Districts and Rural communities
- Private landowners, community groups and retail businesses

Success stories:

If you build bins, they will recycle...

With the passage of Pennsylvania's Act 101 in July 1988, all communities with more than 5,000 residents were required to recycle. As curbside collection programs emerged within Pennsylvania's urban communities, many rural communities launched voluntarily local drop-off recycling programs using mobile roll-off containers.

According to Dan Grow, Solid Waste and Recycling Coordinator of Schyukill County (Pottsville, Pennsylvania), the system was very inefficient. "The major problem with the roll-off system was density. We weren't hauling good volumes of materials," says Grow. Other problems included high contamination rates due to the larger openings on the roll-off containers and not enough convenient drop-off locations. To remedy these problems, Schuykill and eleven other counties across Pennsylvania turned to Haul-All's (Lethbridge, Alberta) integrated recycling system.

With the mobile roll-off system, Schuylkill County handled approximately 1,200 tons per year. In 2001, the first year following the switch in equipment, that figure increased to almost 1,800 tons. "With this system, you double your load efficiency," says Grow.

(Resource Recycling, 02/04 - Service in the Sticks - Andrew Santosusso Jr.)

Testimonial:

"Possibly the best feature of the animal-proof bin system, [said Goin], is the fact that it works. Over the years, few animals are found in the town due to garbage being an attractant. Personally, I think everywhere from Bragg Creek to Whistler should have a system like this. They've shot a lot of bears in some areas, but we don't get bears that are habituated here."

(Rocky Mountain Outlook - 10/18/07, Dave Whitfield - Canmore)
