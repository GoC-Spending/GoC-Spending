Company Profile


1 PROFILE

ISONORM CONSULTING GROUP INC is an international consultancy firm, incorporated in Canada in 1993 by the founding president Gerard Blin to respond to the market needs in terms of consultation and training services in Q.S.E. management (Quality, Safety, Security, Salubrity, Health and Environment) within the framework of a program for managing the changes inside the private and public organizations.

The two senior associated consultants, Gerard Blin and George Laszlo are recognized by their unique practical experience in the Quality matters in North America. They are accumulating more than 70 years experience in bilingual management (EN-FR) of the operational processes of many organizations with several distinct, economical and cultural contexts (USA, Canada, Quebec, France, Tunisia, Algeria, Venezuela, Argentina, India, China).

ISONORM CONSULTING GROUP INC is associated with MANAGEMENT M PLUS INC in Canada and with ISONORM EUROCONSULTING and PERFORMANCE QSE s.a. in France. This international networking configuration allows us to supply top class consulting and training services with a multi-disciplinary approach at the current local rates.

2 MISSION

The mission of ISONORM CONSULTING GROUP INC is to respond, worldwide where safety allows, to the needs of the small and medium enterprises, the laboratories and the institutions in the four following areas of the continuous improvement:

2.1 OPTIMIZATION OF THE OPERATIONAL PERFORMANCES as per the following guidelines and tools of the Project Management: Lean, Six Sigma, Process and Activity Based Costing;
2.2 CERTIFICATION OF SYSTEM related to the Q.S.E. management (Quality, Safety, Security, Salubrity Health and Environment) and the accreditation projects of the laboratories;
2.3 CERTIFICATION OF PRODUCT for export purpose, in particular the projects of certification/homologation/labelling/marking relevant to the future Free Trade Agreement between Canada and the European Union;
2.4 SUSTAINABLE DEVELOPMENT including the programs of Sustainable Forest Management relevant to the ligneous and non-ligneous resources, the programs of reduction of the industrial environmental footprints (energy, carbon and atmospheric polluting emissions, REACH, MASE / UIC, LEED, etc.) and the programs of the Social Economy (Eco-Responsibility, Agenda 21 de Rio, ISO 26000).
 
3 OFFER

We offer a wide range of multidisciplinary services which contribute to maintain the continuity in business and the growth of the enterprises:

3.1 Consultancy (feasibility studies, risk management analysis, first and second party audits of diverse management systems (legal and/or normative compliance);
3.2 Coaching of the Project Management aiming the optimization of the performance, the certification of system, the certification of product and the sustainable development;
3.3 Training of the managers, trainers and employees concerned by the current project;
3.4 IT solutions specialized in certified or accredited Management systems.

4 APPROACH
The management of the change defines itself as the way of facing, of answering and of reacting to the changes. ISONORM CONSULTING GROUP INC proposes a holistic but pragmatic approach of the Project Management derived from the «Total Quality Management and Six Sigma» approach of the American Society for Quality (ASQ) and the «Lean» approach of Toyota that we are customizing for each customer culture on the basis of the following guidelines:
4.1 Preeminence of the Customer's needs and expectations;
4.2 Excellence in Business by the perceived quality of products and services;
4.3 Responsible commitment of the top management and the workforce.

We differ clearly from other large Consulting Engineering or Accounting firms by:
4.4 Our structure adapted for the cultures of the smallest organizations;
4.5 Our bias for the preponderance of the «human» capital;
4.6 Our sense of Ethic in business and ultimately the results obtained thanks-to our hands-on contributions in the field, when required.

We distinguish ourselves finally by our capacity to use (or supply and integrate):
4.7 The present technologies of information available at the customer site;
4.8 Our own electronic documentation and workflow system, IDMS (Isonorm Documentation Management System) particularly affordable;
4.9 Several management softwares specialized in the certified systems and used currently in the large companies.

1 OPTIMIZATION OF THE OPERATIONAL PERFORMANCES

1.1 Example #1 of real-life experience in Canada

Project manager:
G. Blin as Director of the Canadian operations and Source inspection reporting to the President and CEO of VELAN-RATEAU s.a. (France), a subsidiary of ALSTOM GROUP located at La Baule (France).

Mandate:
To contribute to the optimization of the quality performance of the nuclear valves class 1, a critical pressure part of the French nuclear PWR power plants.

a) Improving the technology transfers (design, R&D and manufacturing) from VELAN inc (Canada) toward VELAN-RATEAU s.a. (France) in order to facitate the changes management which was necessary to be accredited by the French Nuclear Authorities as a stand-alone national supplier;
b) Replacing and improving the prior surveillance by BUREAU VERITAS NORTH AMERICA;
c) Assuring the quality and the quantity of the nuclear valves manufactured and exported to VELAN-RATEAU s.a.

Realization:
To achieve the corporative mandate to the full satisfaction of the employer and the concerned French Authorities (ALSTOM, FRAMATOME and EDF) without failure during 6 years.

1.2 Example #2 of real-life experience in Canada

Dedicated consultant:
G. Blin as a Senior Consultant and Trainer of ISONORM CONSULTING GROUP INC assigned to the project of the CSSS DE LA VALLÉE-DE-LA-GATINEAU, at Maniwaki (Quebec)

Mandate:
To supply the training in Risk Management in the Health sector, required by the Head office of the Health and the Social Services Center (CSSS in French) with the FMEA/AMDEC method.

Realization:
To win the competitive bidding process, to deliver the services and to achieve the mandate to the satisfaction of the Client (Letter of recommendation available upon request).
 

2 CERTIFICATION OF SYSTEM

2.1 Example #3 of real-life experience in Canada

Project manager:
G. Blin as Manager, Quality Assurance of COMBUSTION ENGINEERING CANADA INC.

Mandate:
To support the management of changes required to maintain the current certification of the Sherbrooke plant as per the ASME code and CSA Z299 -1 and -2 and introduce the new emergent standard ISO 9001 1987 governing the quality management systems of the pressure parts of the thermal and nuclear power plants.

Realization:
To achieve the corporative mandate within the allocated budget and time and obtain the certification few months after the publication of the new ISO standard.

2.2 Example #4 of real-life experience in Algeria

Dedicated consultant:
G. Blin as a Senior Consultant of ISONORM CONSULTING GROUP INC (Canada) and Head of mission assigned for the international project of the ALGERIAN CENTER OF QUALITY CONTROL AND PACKAGING (CACQE IN FRENCH).

Mandate:
To supply the expertise, the training and the support for the management of the changes required by this project of certification and renovation of several laboratories with a team of five (5) Canadian engineers selected and hired by myself within my own business network for the duration of the project of the Canadian International Development Agency (CIDA).

Details:
The CACQE is an important Algerian public institution which is responsible to assure the salubrity (healthiness) of the imported products (foods, cosmetics and other goods) including the control of the drinking water by means of a network of 20 laboratories of physico-chemical and microbiological analysis (4 main regional laboratories of Oran, Algiers, Constantine, Ouargla and 16 secondary laboratories locally).

ISONORM CONSULTING GROUP INC has accompanied the multiple changes necessary to improve the managerial capacity of the staff, the competence of the employees and the performance of the operational processes including the integration of all the requirements of the following contractual documents into a unique management system applicable to the entire lab network:

• Requirements of ISO 17025, ISO 9001, ISO 14001, OSHAS 18001;
• Relevant Laws and regulations of Algeria;
• Specific requirements of the Canadian International Development Agency (CIDA) which was financing 40 % of the project under the administrative control de CRC SOGEMA INC (Québec);
• Specific requirements of the Trade Ministry of Algeria which was financing 60 % of the project mainly for the renovation of the infrastructures.

Our approach was a mixt of the Deming cycle (Plan/Do/Check/Action), of the Six-Sigma model (Define/Measure/Analyze/Improve/Control), of the Project Management phases (Project charter/Project plan/Project execution & monitoring/Project Close Out) within a framework of management of the changes to assure the mutual trust and communication, employee loyalty, organizational culture, and the leadership of the directors.

Realization:
To win the competitive bidding process, to deliver the on-site and online services and to complete the mandate within the allocated budget (almost 1 million $ CAD) and time to the satisfaction of the four clients [CIDA, CRC SOGEMA INC, Trade Ministry of Algeria and CACQE] including the training of 250 employees, the calibration of a huge quantity of measuring instruments, the renovation of the infrastructures, the preparation to the accreditation process of the laboratories by ALGERAC (the future national accreditation body of Algeria in the course of being founded).

The targeted objective and results were successfully confirmed by the independent certification of the competence of the four (4) regional laboratories as per the International standard ISO 17025 by the International Certification Body SGS in 2005.

3 CERTIFICATION OF PRODUCT

3.1 Example #5 of real-life experience in Canada

Dedicated consultant:
G. Blin as a Senior Consultant of ISONORM CONSULTING GROUP INC (Canada) assigned for the project of the CONSEIL DES APPELLATIONS AGROALIMENTAIRES DU QUÉBEC (CAAQ).

Mandate:
To supply the expertise and the training requested by the General Manager of the CAAQ in 2005 in order to rationalize and to facilitate the management of the changes which were necessary to face the increasing demand for the labelling of the agro-food products of Quebec.

Details:
The apposition of a logo (labelling) credible and controlled by a local recognized authority is considered from now on, as an effective marketing tool by the worldwide agro-food industry and as a good protection against the poor controlled imported products from foreign countries.
 

The delivered services were as follows:

• To realize a first study in order to draw up an inventory of possible laws, regulations, administrative practices and form of self-regulation which were in existence or in preparation in the organization.
• To create a new documentation configuration compatible with the requirements of the international standards for the Certification/Accreditation, on the basis of our own system IDMS (Isonorm Documentation Management System);
• To supply a first draft of the Quality Management Manual of the CAAQ activities and propose subsequent amendments according to the comments of the General Manager;
• To supply a first draft of the missing documents to assure the full compliance with the requirements of the international standard ISO 17011: 2004 for the accreditation of the certification bodies.

Realization:
To win the initial competitive bidding process, to deliver the ordered services to the satisfaction of the General Manager (references available upon request) and to contribute consequently to the future restructuring of the CAAQ in two new entities to better answer the pressing needs of the agro-food industry of Quebec.

• CAEQ for «Comité d’Accréditation en Évaluation de la Qualité» in French, a new Quebec competent authority for the assessment of the competences of the Product Certification Bodies;

• CARTV for «Conseil des appellations réservées et des termes valorisants» in French, a new Quebec competent authority on behalf of Quebec’s Ministry of Agriculture, Fisheries and Food (MAPAQ in French), for the accreditation of the Food Product Certification Bodies, the recognition and the monitoring of reserved designations and logos.

3.2 Example #6 of real-life experience in France

Dedicated consultant:
G. Blin as a Senior Consultant of ISONORM CONSULTING GROUP INC (Canada) assigned to the project of TRAD-Y-SEL s.a. Guerande (France)

This enterprise exploits the sea salt collected by evaporation of the ocean water and distributes its product in France by means of several local distribution networks.


Mandate:
To realize a feasibility study for a «green certification» of the sea salt in order to facilitate the identification of the changes which are necessary to increase the profitability of the exploitation without jeopardizing the traditional and secular means of collection.
 

Realization:
To supply an expert opinion answering the expressed needs of the general management how to increase the sales figures.

The conclusions of the study have allowed to eliminate the ORGANIC certification in this case, and to provide guidance to access to the international export with a certified product in the framework of the following labelling program:

• Obtaining the «IGP» label for the Protected Geographical Indication;
• Implementing a traceability system;
• Obtaining the labels «certified BRC» and «Nature et Progrès».

4 SUSTAINABLE DEVELOPMENT

4.1 Example #7 of real-life experience in Canada

Dedicated consultant:
G. Blin as a Senior Consultant of ISONORM CONSULTING GROUP INC (Canada) assigned to the project of the QUEBEC COUNTY REGIONAL MUNICIPALITIES (MRC DE LA-VALLÉE-DE-LA-GATINEAU), Maniwaki (Québec).

Mandate:
To supply the expertise, the training and the support for the management of the changes which are essentials for the realization of this project required by a community of 17 municipalities including Maniwaki and a First Nation reservation (Kitigan Zibi Anishinabeg);

The project consists of the implementation of a new Sustainable Forest Management (SFM) on the Quebec Intra-municipal Public Lands (TPI in French) in compliance with:

• The Quebec regulations applicable to the forests of proximity;
• The Sustainable development policy of the MRC;
• The Public participation including the First Nation community of Kitigan Zibi Anishinabeg.

Realization:
To win the competitive bidding process, to deliver the services and to complete the mandate to the satisfaction of the MRC DE LA-VALLÉE-DE-LA-GATINEAU.

The pursued objectives and obtained results were confirmed by the active participation of the Algonquian community to the project and by a first forest certification delivered by SAI GLOBAL according to ISO 14001-2004 (reference available upon request).
 

The next phase proposed for the development of the forest ligneous and not-ligneous resources within the framework of the S Development policy of the MRC, has been limited to the initial awareness session of both communities on the basis of the Sustainable Forest Management as per CSA Z809-2008 because of the actual constitutional problem not solved on the status of the First Nations.

4.2 Example #8 of real-life experience in Canada

Dedicated consultant:
G. Blin as a Senior Consultant of ISONORM CONSULTING GROUP INC (Canada) assigned to the project of ABITIBI CONSOLIDATED INC, renamed lately PRODUITS FORESTIERS RÉSOLU, Montreal (Quebec).

Mandate:
To supply the expertise, the training and the support for the management of the changes required by this major certification project of the Corporate Forest Managers of the Head office and the Executive Managers and employees of the 3 canadian pilot plants

The project was consisting in the facilitation of the achievement of the corporate objectives related to the environmental and sustainable management of the Canadian public forests, measured by the multiple and independent forest certifications while contributing in a proactive way to the continuous improvement of each distinct forest regulations applied in each Province.

Details:
The delivered services during two years were as follows:

 Training the corporative forest managers and the concerned employees of the pilot plants of Ontario, Quebec and Newfoundland including the introduction of a systematic internal audit program in each plant;

 Coaching the implementation of a new Forest Environmental Management System (FEMS) in each province on the basis of the requirements of the following reference documents:

• ISO 9001/ISO 14001/ISO 19011;
• The systems of traceability from the forest to the plant;
• «Criteria and indicators of Sustainable Forest Management in Canada» of the Canadian Council of Forest Ministers (1997);
• The distinct Forest Laws and Regulations of Quebec, Ontario and Newfounland.

Realization:
To win the competitive bidding process, to deliver the required services for a successful final certification and to achieve the mandate to the satisfaction of the Corporative Forest Management of ABITIBI CONSOLIDATED INC (reference available upon request) relatively to the first phase of the certification of its Forest Environmental Management System (19 % of the total certified forest area in Canada in 2001 resulting from the contributions of our dedicated consultant).

To contribute to the strategic planning of the next phase to upgrade the certification program for the other Sustainable Forest Management requiring the participation of the public (social acceptance) as CSA Z809 (standard of the Canadian Forest Authorities) and FSC (standard of WWF and Greenpeace).

2.2 Example #4 of real-life experience in Algeria

Dedicated consultant:
G. Blin as a Senior Consultant of ISONORM CONSULTING GROUP INC (Canada) and Head of mission assigned for the international project of the ALGERIAN CENTER OF QUALITY CONTROL AND PACKAGING (CACQE IN FRENCH).

Mandate:
To supply the expertise, the training and the support for the management of the changes required by this project of certification and renovation of several laboratories with a team of five (5) Canadian engineers selected and hired by myself within my own business network for the duration of the project of the Canadian International Development Agency (CIDA).

Details:
The CACQE is an important Algerian public institution which is responsible to assure the salubrity (healthiness) of the imported products (foods, cosmetics and other goods) including the control of the drinking water by means of a network of 20 laboratories of physico-chemical and microbiological analysis (4 main regional laboratories of Oran, Algiers, Constantine, Ouargla and 16 secondary laboratories locally).

ISONORM CONSULTING GROUP INC has accompanied the multiple changes necessary to improve the managerial capacity of the staff, the competence of the employees and the performance of the operational processes including the integration of all the requirements of the following contractual documents into a unique management system applicable to the entire lab network:

• Requirements of ISO 17025, ISO 9001, ISO 14001, OSHAS 18001;
• Relevant Laws and regulations of Algeria;
• Specific requirements of the Canadian International Development Agency (CIDA) which was financing 40 % of the project under the administrative control de CRC SOGEMA INC (Québec);
• Specific requirements of the Trade Ministry of Algeria which was financing 60 % of the project mainly for the renovation of the infrastructures.

Our approach was a mixt of the Deming cycle (Plan/Do/Check/Action), of the Six-Sigma model (Define/Measure/Analyze/Improve/Control), of the Project Management phases (Project charter/Project plan/Project execution & monitoring/Project Close Out) within a framework of management of the changes to assure the mutual trust and communication, employee loyalty, organizational culture, and the leadership of the directors.

Realization:
To win the competitive bidding process, to deliver the on-site and online services and to complete the mandate within the allocated budget (almost 1 million $ CAD) and time to the satisfaction of the four clients [CIDA, CRC SOGEMA INC, Trade Ministry of Algeria and CACQE] including the training of 250 employees, the calibration of a huge quantity of measuring instruments, the renovation of the infrastructures, the preparation to the accreditation process of the laboratories by ALGERAC (the future national accreditation body of Algeria in the course of being founded).

The targeted objective and results were successfully confirmed by the independent certification of the competence of the four (4) regional laboratories as per the International standard ISO 17025 by the International Certification Body SGS in 2005.

3 CERTIFICATION OF PRODUCT

3.1 Example #5 of real-life experience in Canada

Dedicated consultant:
G. Blin as a Senior Consultant of ISONORM CONSULTING GROUP INC (Canada) assigned for the project of the CONSEIL DES APPELLATIONS AGROALIMENTAIRES DU QUÉBEC (CAAQ).

Mandate:
To supply the expertise and the training requested by the General Manager of the CAAQ in 2005 in order to rationalize and to facilitate the management of the changes which were necessary to face the increasing demand for the labelling of the agro-food products of Quebec.

Details:
The apposition of a logo (labelling) credible and controlled by a local recognized authority is considered from now on, as an effective marketing tool by the worldwide agro-food industry and as a good protection against the poor controlled imported products from foreign countries.
 

The delivered services were as follows:

• To realize a first study in order to draw up an inventory of possible laws, regulations, administrative practices and form of self-regulation which were in existence or in preparation in the organization.
• To create a new documentation configuration compatible with the requirements of the international standards for the Certification/Accreditation, on the basis of our own system IDMS (Isonorm Documentation Management System);
• To supply a first draft of the Quality Management Manual of the CAAQ activities and propose subsequent amendments according to the comments of the General Manager;
• To supply a first draft of the missing documents to assure the full compliance with the requirements of the international standard ISO 17011: 2004 for the accreditation of the certification bodies.

Realization:
To win the initial competitive bidding process, to deliver the ordered services to the satisfaction of the General Manager (references available upon request) and to contribute consequently to the future restructuring of the CAAQ in two new entities to better answer the pressing needs of the agro-food industry of Quebec.

• CAEQ for «Comité d’Accréditation en Évaluation de la Qualité» in French, a new Quebec competent authority for the assessment of the competences of the Product Certification Bodies;

• CARTV for «Conseil des appellations réservées et des termes valorisants» in French, a new Quebec competent authority on behalf of Quebec’s Ministry of Agriculture, Fisheries and Food (MAPAQ in French), for the accreditation of the Food Product Certification Bodies, the recognition and the monitoring of reserved designations and logos.

G. Blin as a Senior Consultant of ISONORM CONSULTING GROUP INC (Canada) assigned to the project of TRAD-Y-SEL s.a. Guerande (France)

This enterprise exploits the sea salt collected by evaporation of the ocean water and distributes its product in France by means of several local distribution networks.
