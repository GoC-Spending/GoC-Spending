Success stories:

SELKIRK FIRST NATION:
In 2013, the Selkirk First Nation embarked on a cultural orientation program with the aim of informing their non-aboriginal business partners on the historic significance and appreciation of Northern Tutchone Culture and Heritage. The program includes a DVD, booklet, and community digital signage network with screens in the local administration office, store and the Minto Mine. PDS is providing project support via deployment of the digital screens, software/content development, as well as video and print production. The Digital Signage network will also display relevant community information, news, and weather.

YUKON GOVERNMENT:
With the move to a new location, the Motor Vehicles Branch of the government's H&PW Department needed a solution to better engage clients by reducing perceived wait-times while providing helpful information as they wait to see an MV representative. Our solution included a 'Take-a-number' queuing system, 55in LED screen, media player, CAT5/6 8-port video distribution, a Content Management server, as well as a 1 day training course for MV and Road Safety staff to access the network to create and schedule messages in real time. PDS also worked with H&PW staff to develop a Content Management strategy that conforms to their current branding and visual identity criteria. PDS currently provides a technical support and Content Management service for the department.

NORTHWESTEL:
Provision of hardware/software solution to enable Northwestel Cable to effectively manage their ad channels and community TV channels from a central web-based location. Solution involved installation, training, configuration and ongoing support using Scala suite of software. (Content Manager, Designer, and Player)Locations for NWTEL ad channels include:

•Whitehorse, YT – Ch 21
•Yellowknife, NT – Ch 6
•Fort Nelson, BC – Ch 8
•High Level, AB – Ch 22

The same solution has been ported to Northwestel Retail stores in Whitehorse, Yellowknife, Fort Nelson and High Level.

YUKON HOSPITAL CORPORATION:
The Yukon Hospital Corporation was looking for a means to communicate with two key groups; Visitors and Staff. The Yukon Hospital Foundation was looking for innovative new revenue streams to enable it to invest in state-of-the-art medical equipment. Both organizations achieved their objectives and received multiple ROI from a single investment by purchasing our Digital Signage solution. An important benefit was scalability..the ability to start small (one hospital) and eventually expand to multiple locations and additional monitors without risking the initial investment. It was also important to have a system that would allow a single media player output two separate and independent channels of information. One channel delivering staff info in the cafeteria while another channel delivered visitor/patient messages and advertising to lobby and ER locations. This resulted in considerable cost savings.

YUKON COLLEGE:
The college needed a more efficient means to reach students, staff and visitors with timely information on classes, events, and services. They wanted a system that would allow multiple users to create, post, and schedule their own content across departments while ensuring all messages met visual standards as set out in their branding manual. They also had a requirement to accomplish this without interrupting the current flow of information, which was a major problem using their old PowerPoint based solution. This was accomplished using our Digital Signage software solution with templates and messages.

WHITEHORSE AIRPORT:
With the decision to remove static signage as a result of airport expansion, an alternate method of advertising was proposed to replace the aging products. PDS deployment of a Digital Signage network met multiple requirements including a less cluttered environment, consistent look-and-feel for content, instant and dynamic messaging, and current value-added content such as news and weather. Installation of a Scala-based software/hardware solution allows advertisers to target a specific demographic at the airport in both arrival and departure locations. This installation is all about getting the right message to the right people at the right time, in the right location!

LITTLE SALMON CARMACKS FIRST NATION:
First Nation self governments and communities face a plethora of challenges these days. One of the biggest hurdles is ensuring communications to their citizens and other stakeholders is efficient, effective,and immediate. PDS supplied a Scala-based digital signage network allowing department managers to streamline their messages to specific screens/locations while giving them complete control over scheduling. PDS configured the network to ensure an approval process is in place so all messages are cleared before being displayed. The system was put in place to reduce the clutter of community billboards while reducing the amount of printing done on a daily basis.
