Company Profile


Founded in 1993, Immersion Corporation is a recognized leader in developing, licensing, and marketing digital touch technology and products. Using Immersions advanced touch feedback technology, electronic user interfaces can be made more productive, compelling, entertaining, or safer. Immersions technology is deployed across automotive, entertainment, industrial controls, medical training, mobility, and three-dimensional simulation markets. Immersions patent portfolio includes over 700 issued or pending patents in the U.S. and other countries.

Immersion develops, licenses, and markets haptic technologies that bring the sense of touch to the digital world. We help to create levels of realism that allow training environments, digital controls, gaming, and virtual interaction to be more engaging, intuitive, entertaining, or productive.

Product / Service / Licensing

Product Name:
MicroScribe Systems
MicroScribe® systems are lightweight and easy-to-use portable CMM articulated arms and digitizers. To measure any object, users simply collect data points using the stylus. MicroScribe portable CMMs allow the end-user to create everything from basic to complex geometry and/or accurately compare CAD data to a physical part. By interfacing directly into leading graphic arts, reverse engineering, and metrology applications, and many more, MicroScribe systems become a very powerful tool with a very small learning curve. More information at www.immersion.com/digitizer/

Product Name:
TouchSense(R) Tactile Feedback for Touchscreen Displays
Using Immersion's TouchSense® actuators, control board(s), and firmware, tactile feedback can be added to touchscreens to provide the sensation of pressing physical buttons. Results can include unmistakable confirmation, improved interactivity and usability, and powerful differentiation. More information at:
www.immersion.com/touchscreen/


Product Name:
The VibeTonz(R) System
Immersion's VibeTonz® System for mobile handsets includes VibeTonz Mobile Player that enables playback of sophisticated vibro-tactile effects and the VibeTonz SDK suite of tools and APIs that enable development of touch-enabled applications and media. VibeTonz vibro-tactile sensations, in combination with sight and sound cues, make mobile content more realistic and fun and operation more intuitive. More information at www.immersion.com/mobility

Product Name:
Endoscopy AccuTouch(R) System
The Endoscopy AccuTouch System includes three types of endoscopic procedures: flexible bronchoscopy, and upper and lower gastrointestinal flexible endoscopy. Endoscopic procedures are some of the most commonly practiced medical procedures today, but the motor skills necessary to perform these procedures are difficult to train and assess. The Endoscopy AccuTouch Simulator is a realistic, computer-based system for teaching and assessing motor skills and cognitive knowledge, enabling novices and experienced physicians to practice in a safe environment. Using real-time computer graphics, including anatomic models developed from actual patient data and a robotic interface device, force is transmitted through the flexible scope to provide tactile sensations mimicking the actual feel of a procedure. More information at www.immersion.com/medical/products/endoscopy/

Product Name:
CathLabVR System
The CathLabVR System allows clinicians to practice endovascular procedures such as PTCA, stenting, and cardiac pacing. Endovascular procedures require careful attention to interpretation of the fluoroscopic image as well as the subtle feel transmitted through guide wires, catheters, and other interventional devices. This Immersion Medical simulator, which simulates the look and feel of actual procedures, helps clinicians to develop skills prior to performing on patients and to maintain those skills throughout their professional practice. More information at http://www.immersion.com/medical/products/endovascular/index.php

Product Name:
LapVR(TM) Surgical Simulator
The LapVR(TM) Surgical Simulator provides a platform for virtual reality laparoscopy training. The surgical simulator comes packaged as a complete system with selected software module(s) and height adjustable cart, monitor swing, foot pedal, camera, and tools. The systems hardware and software interface simulates laparoscopic surgery and provides virtual reality training in the essential skills required of laparoscopic procedures. Individual training modules that include several virtual cases for procedural skills, such as running the bowel and adhesiolysis; general surgery, such as laparoscopy cholecystectomy; and OB/GYN surgeries, such as tubal ligation and ectopic pregnancy are available. An Essential Skills module that trains on basic skills and an Administration module that lets training administrators set task parameters, create and assign courses, and review and report on user performance, are also available.

Product Name:
Virtual I.V.
The Virtual I.V. is a comprehensive and fully interactive self-directed learning system for training intravenous catheterization. Powerful 3D graphics provide visual realism, while a state-of-the-art force feedback device accurately simulates the sense of touch for a truly immersive experience. Virtual patients respond with bleeding, bruising, swelling, as well as other patho-physiological reactions. Over a dozen pieces of virtual equipment can be used to perform the medical procedure. More information at www.immersion.com/medical/products/vascular/

Product Name:
Impulse Engine
The research-quality Impulse Engine joystick supplies realistic force-feedback effects for creating virtual-reality environments. It allows applications to track user movements and respond with high-fidelity tactile sensations. Its cable-drive capstan transmission provides smooth drive torque with minimal compliance and friction. The result is unmatched realistic simulation of textures, liquids, gravitational fields, and biological materials, to name a few possibilities.
Two or more Impulse Engines can be interfaced within a single system to create exciting multiuser force feedback experiences from games to group participation in simulated surgeries.


Product Name:
SoftMouse(R) 3D Mouse
The SoftMouse positioning device offers unparalleled ease of integration and comfortable two-handed operation for 3D photogrammetry systems used for mapping, GIS surveys, strategic military planning, and other applications. More at http://www.immersion.com/industrial/3Dmouse/



Product Name:
Impulse Stick(R)
The Impulse Stick is a robust force feedback joystick suitable for industrial applications and high abuse environments. It delivers high-fidelity, realistic tactile feedback and comes with all the electronic components and driver software needed to integrate the device into your system. More at http://www.immersion.com/industrial/joystick/impulse_stick.php

Product Name:
TouchSense(R) PR-1000 Programmable Rotary Module
Immersion's PR-1000 TouchSense® Programmable Rotary Module enables a flexible, intelligent design solution for today's user interface and an alternative to mechanical switches, encoders, potentiometers, and other types of electromechanical input devices. The programmable PR-1000 can emulate the physical characteristics of a mechanical rotary switch, such as barriers, detents, and other properties, which contribute to ergonomics and user satisfaction. And it can also supply the benefits of an electronic control: flexible programmability, usability, design, and cost savings. More at http://www.immersion.com/industrial/rotary/products/

Product Name:
Wheel Electronics
For arcade driving games and simulators, it's all about realism - and especially the feel of the wheel. Immersion arcade wheel electronics integrate with off-the-shelf active arcade steering wheels using Immersion gaming drivers. This combination opens the door to fast and efficient development of powerful and realistic force feedback effects. More information at www.immersion.com/gaming/arcade_sim/products/wheel.php

Product Name:
TouchSense(R) Technology for Gaming
Next-generation TouchSense(R) Vibration for console video games matches the realism expected of next-generation high-definition graphics and high-fidelity. Hardware and software components are used both to deliver current dual-motor system performance and provide additional operating modes to extend the range of possible tactile effects. Compared to current dual-motor systems, next-generation TouchSense(R) vibration technology supports:
- Richer, and even more realistic and complex force feedback effects. This is possible by supplying a much broader frequency range and by exerting more precise control over the vibration actuator. Easy-to-use authoring tools allow developers to fully take advantage of these capabilities.
- Faster response/lower latency for a tighter synchronization with onscreen graphic and audio events.
- Lower battery consumption for equivalent sized motors; less weight (a convenience for implementing motion control); and deployment in the same or less space.
- Backward compatibility for tactile effects in games designed for current dual-motor systems.
- No increase in cost, and in many cases, lower cost, by reducing two motors to only one actuator.


Product Name:
CyberForce(R) Tactile Feedback System
The CyberForce® system is a force feedback armature that not only conveys realistic grounded forces to the hand and arm but also provides six degrees of freedom positional tracking that accurately measures translation and rotation of the hand in three dimensions. The CyberForce system is an option designed to work with the CyberGrasp(TM) exoskeleton, the award-winning, lightweight, force-reflecting system that fits over a CyberGlove(R) data glove and adds resistive force feedback to each finger. More at http://www.immersion.com/3d/products/cyber_force.php

Product Name:
CyberGrasp(TM) Exoskeleton
The CyberGrasp(TM) system is an innovative force feedback system for your fingers and hand. It lets you "reach into your computer" and grasp computer-generated or tele-manipulated objects.
The CyberGrasp device is a lightweight, force-reflecting exoskeleton that fits over a CyberGlove(R) data glove (wired version) and adds resistive force feedback to each finger. With the CyberGrasp force feedback system, users are able to feel the size and shape of computer-generated 3D objects in a simulated virtual world. More at http://www.immersion.com/3d/products/cyber_grasp.php

Product Name:
CyberTouch(TM) Force Feedback Option
The CyberTouch(TM) system is a tactile feedback option for Immersion's wired CyberGlove instrumented glove. It features small vibrotactile stimulators on each finger and the palm of the CyberGlove system. Each stimulator can be individually programmed to vary the strength of touch sensation. The array of stimulators can generate simple sensations such as pulses or sustained vibration, and they can be used in combination to produce complex tactile feedback patterns. Software developers can design their own actuation profiles. The CyberTouch tactile feedback option is essential to anyone serious about using their hands to interact with objects in a virtual world. More at http://www.immersion.com/3d/products/cyber_touch.php

Product Name:
CyberGlove(R) II Wireless Data Glove
The new wireless CyberGlove II system is a fully instrumented glove that provides up to 22 high-accuracy joint-angle measurements. It uses proprietary resistive bend-sensing technology to accurately transform hand and finger motions into real-time digital joint-angle data. More at http://www.immersion.com/3d/products/cyber_glove.php

Product Name:
VirtualHand(R) for V5 (CATIA)
Use Immersion Corporation's new CAA V5-based software, VirtualHand(R) for V5, together with one of our CyberGlove(R)-based hardware products to reach in and touch your CATIA designs. You can intuitively evaluate the ergonomics and design of digital prototypes by using your physical hands to interact with CATIA models. More at http://www.immersion.com/3d/products/virtualhand_for_v5.php



Product Name:
VirtualHand(R) SDK
The VirtualHand SDK is a complete development solution for adding hand-motion capture, hand-interaction, and force feedback to simulation applications. It simplifies simulation for developers in mechanical CAD, R&D, or 3D e-commerce to "hand-enable" their applications. More at http://www.immersion.com/3d/products/virtualhand_sdk.php


Product Name:
VirtualHand(R) for MotionBuilder
VirtualHand for MotionBuilder, a plug-in for Immersion's CyberGlove(R) II Wireless Glove, lets motion-capture studios easily acquire, edit, and blend motion animation in AutoDesk's MotionBuilder real-time capture software. More at http://www.immersion.com/3d/products/virtualhand_for_motionbuilder.php

Success stories:

BMW iDrive controller
Leading auto manufacturer, BMW, has licensed Immersion's TouchSense technology to create the automotive industry's first intuitive information and control system called iDrive. This system allows drivers to access numerous operations such as climate, entertainment, and navigation with a single control. ALPS Electric, the world's largest electronics components supplier, manufactures the iDrive Controller and is a preferred supplier of TouchSense-enabled controls.

Vascular Access Medical Training Simulator
The CathSim® AccuTouch® System was introduced in 1998 and over 700 systems have been installed worldwide since that time. Customers report the system supplies a highly realistic learning environment that teaches the proper technique and sequencing for IV access. It exposes students to a wide variety of cases, and in that way, improves student confidence and understanding. In addition, the system has brought cost savings, due to less need for supplies, and instructor time savings, because more independent learning is possible. Case studies detailing use of the system at the following organizations can be found at www.immersion.com/medical/products/vascular/case_studies.php:
- Children's Hospital of Pittsburgh
- Indiana University School of Medicine
- Madisonville Community College
- Montgomery County, Maryland  Fire Department
- University of Medicine and Dentistry of New Jersey

Bronchoscopy Medical Traning Simulator
The Endoscopy AccuTouch® System with bronchoscopy modules was introduced in 1999, and since that time hundreds of customers have installed the system into their training programs. They report that the system provides faster learning and more realistic experiences, no-risk training, and therefore improved patient safety, increased student satisfaction, and the ability to act as a qualification tool for clinical procedures. Some of their experiences can be found at www.immersion.com/medical/products/endoscopy/bronch/case_studies.php, including from:

Lower GI Medical Training Simulator
The Endoscopy AccuTouch® System with lower GI modules was introduced in 1999, and since that time, hundreds of customers have integrated the system into their training programs. They report that the systems force feedback action (haptics) makes the procedures feel real, and that its realistic learning environment helps reduce the time needed for residents to gain proficiency. The system allows independent learning in nonthreatening situations, and promotes patient safety through developing the skills needed to perform colonoscopies faster. Some of our customers experiences are documented at www.immersion.com/medical/products/endoscopy/lgi/case_studies.php, including those from:
- Philadelphia Gastroenterology Training Group

- University of Aberdeen

Endovascular Medical Trainint Simulator
Users of the Endovascular AccuTouch® System report that the system provides a very safe, controlled environment in which criterion-based training can be established to control the learning process. They further believe that the system helps reduce the learning curve and could minimize complications and the costs associated with them. The systems cases progressively increase in difficulty to expand learning and help transfer good technique to real procedures. Two doctors who used the system in a training program hosted by Medtronic, a co-developer of the system, have reported on their experiences, which can be found at www.immersion.com/medical/products/endovascular/case_studies.php:
- Dr. Alan Yeung, Stanford University
- Dr. Campbell Rogers, Brigham & Womens Hospital

Hysteroscopy Medical Training Simulator
The Hysteroscopy AccuTouch® System was introduced in 2004 and is the first virtual reality trainer of its kind. The System uses actual instruments augmented with force feeback to navigate within a 3D virtual model. Early reports indicate that it will become a welcome addition to gynecological training programs. Read the experiences of two users of the system at www.immersion.com/medical/products/hysteroscopy/case_studies.php:

Technology

Technology profile

Immersion develops, licenses, and markets haptic technologies that bring the sense of touch to the digital world. We help to create levels of realism that allow training environments, digital controls, gaming, and virtual interaction to be more engaging, intuitive, entertaining, or productive. Immersion holds more than 700 issued or pending patents in the U.S. and other countries.

BMW iDrive controller
Leading auto manufacturer, BMW, has licensed Immersion's TouchSense technology to create the automotive industry's first intuitive information and control system called iDrive. This system allows drivers to access numerous operations such as climate, entertainment, and navigation with a single control. ALPS Electric, the world's largest electronics components supplier, manufactures the iDrive Controller and is a preferred supplier of TouchSense-enabled controls.

Vascular Access Medical Training Simulator
The CathSim® AccuTouch® System was introduced in 1998 and over 700 systems have been installed worldwide since that time. Customers report the system supplies a highly realistic learning environment that teaches the proper technique and sequencing for IV access. It exposes students to a wide variety of cases, and in that way, improves student confidence and understanding. In addition, the system has brought cost savings, due to less need for supplies, and instructor time savings, because more independent learning is possible. Case studies detailing use of the system at the following organizations can be found at www.immersion.com/medical/products/vascular/case_studies.php:

Lower GI Medical Training Simulator
The Endoscopy AccuTouch® System with lower GI modules was introduced in 1999, and since that time, hundreds of customers have integrated the system into their training programs. They report that the systems force feedback action (haptics) makes the procedures feel real, and that its realistic learning environment helps reduce the time needed for residents to gain proficiency. The system allows independent learning in nonthreatening situations, and promotes patient safety through developing the skills needed to perform colonoscopies faster. Endovascular Medical Trainint Simulator
Users of the Endovascular AccuTouch® System report that the system provides a very safe, controlled environment in which criterion-based training can be established to control the learning process. They further believe that the system helps reduce the learning curve and could minimize complications and the costs associated with them. The systems cases progressively increase in difficulty to expand learning and help transfer good technique to real procedures. Two doctors who used the system in a training program hosted by Medtronic, a co-developer of the system, have reported on their experiences, which can be found at www.immersion.com/medical/products/endovascular/case_studies.php:

- Dr. Campbell Rogers, Brigham & Womens Hospital

Hysteroscopy Medical Training Simulator
The Hysteroscopy AccuTouch® System was introduced in 2004 and is the first virtual reality trainer of its kind. Early reports indicate that it will become a welcome addition to gynecological training programs.
